The Giza plateau is readable at multiple scales. Don't lock to one.

## Scales

- **The whole complex**: 3 pyramids, sphinx, valley temples, mortuary
  temples, 3 causeways, boat pits, basalt pavement, barracks, mastaba
  fields. The layout IS a system. Sphinx faces east (sunrise), causeways
  run east-west connecting valley to pyramid, everything oriented to
  cardinal directions.

- **A single pyramid**: base, slope, passages, chambers, coffer. The
  construction order (base → mass → geometry → endurance) maps to
  χ→μ→φ→τ. The interior has its own structure (passages as bridges,
  chambers as different math types).

- **A single room**: King's Chamber is 20 × 10 × 5√5 cubits. The room
  diagonal is exactly 25. Queen's Chamber is 10 × 11 × 12 — three
  consecutive integers. Each room encodes different mathematics.

- **A single object**: the coffer (sarcophagus) has its own dimensions,
  wall thicknesses (all 0.25 cubits), volume ratios.

## How this affects decode work

When mapping Giza to Genesis or Pyramid Texts, specify the scale.
"The pyramid encodes X" — which pyramid? The whole one? The base?
The King's Chamber? The plateau?

The same verse might map to different things at different scales.
1:1 at the plateau level might be "there IS a complex here." At the
pyramid level it might be "the base is laid." At the room level it
might be "20 × 10." Don't force one scale. Let the text tell you
which zoom level it's at.
