# Giza Pyramids — Spec Sheets

No text on the walls. The stone IS the text. These are the
variables they chose to encode in geometry.

Source: W.M.F. Petrie, "The Pyramids and Temples of Gizeh" (1883).
All measurements in inches unless noted. Petrie measured in inches;
metric conversions at 1 inch = 25.4 mm.


## Khufu (Great Pyramid)

    pharaoh:        Khufu (Cheops), 4th Dynasty, ~2560 BCE
    location:       29°58′45″N 31°08′03″E

### Exterior (Petrie §20-27)

    base sides (mean of casing):
      N:            9069.4 in  (230.36 m)
      E:            9067.7 in  (230.32 m)
      S:            9069.5 in  (230.36 m)
      W:            9068.6 in  (230.34 m)
      mean:         9068.8 in  (230.35 m)  = 440 cubits
      error:        ±0.8 in across 756 ft  (±20 mm over 230 m)

    height (original):  5776 in  (146.71 m)  = 280 cubits
    height (current):   5451 in  (138.5 m)   = 203 courses remain

    slope angle:    51°52′ ± 2′ (from casing stones)
    seked:          5½ palms (rise 14, run 11)

    base level:     ±0.8 in over entire base (±21 mm)
    north align:    3′38″ west of true north (0.06°)
    corner angles:  90° ± 12″ mean error
    joint width:    0.02 in mean (0.5 mm) — casing and inner blocks

    courses:        203 remaining (originally ~210)
    lowest course:  58.6 in high (148.8 cm)
    upper courses:  barely exceed 20 in (50 cm)

    pavement:       flat-bed rock, leveled
      N side:       -217 in below pavement  (offset from center)
      E side:       -5765 in from pavement to N.E. trench
      base level:   varies ±0.4 in across N side

    blocks:         ~2,300,000
    total mass:     ~6,500,000 tonnes
    limestone:      ~5,500,000 tonnes
    granite:        ~8,000 tonnes (Aswan, 800 km south)
    mortar:         ~500,000 tonnes

### Entrance Passage (Petrie §35-36)

    original position:  668.2 in east of center (±0.3)
                        on N face, 17.1 ft above pavement
    passage width:      41.5 in
    passage height:     47.3 in (perpendicular to slope)
    slope angle:        26°31′23″ ± 5″
    azimuth:            offset 3′44″ west (close to pyramid azimuth)
    length (built):     ~1110 in on slope
    length (rock):      extends further into bedrock
    straightness:       ±0.3 in over 150 ft in built part
                        ±0.1 in over 100 ft in rock part

### Subterranean Chamber (Petrie §37)

    location:       cut into bedrock, 1181 in below base
    large chamber:
      E-W:          553 in  (14.05 m)
      N-S:          ~324 in (~8.23 m)
    walls:          rough, not finished
    side chamber:   enlargement of passage, rough and uneven

### Ascending Passage (Petrie §38-39)

    length:         ~1546 in on slope
    slope angle:    26°2′30″
    azimuth:        offset 3′44″ west
    width:          varies, roughly same as descending
    height:         varies
    plug blocks:    3 granite blocks near lower end
                    lengths 60-64 in each

### Grand Gallery (Petrie §45-46)

    length (floor):       1883.6 in  (47.84 m)
    width (floor):        82.4 in  (2.09 m)
    width (roof):         ~41 in  (1.04 m) — corbelled inward
    height:               339 in  (8.61 m)
    slope:                26°16′40″ (slightly less than passages)
    corbelling:           7 overlaps, each ~3 in inward
    wall course height:   varies, rougher toward top
    great step at top:    36 in high

    ramps:                each side, 20 in wide, 24 in high
                          27 holes in ramps, purpose unknown

### Antechamber (Petrie §47-50)

    length (N-S):         149.4 in  (3.80 m)
    width (E-W):          65.0 in  (1.65 m)
    height:               ~149 in to flat ceiling
    granite leaf:         thickness 15.16 in, lowered from above
    wall material:        granite on E, W, and S; limestone N
    portcullis grooves:   4 slots in E and W walls

### King's Chamber (Petrie §51-57)

    length N wall:        412.40 in  (10.475 m)
    length S wall:        412.11 in  (10.468 m)
    width E wall:         206.02 in  (5.233 m)
    width W wall:         205.97 in  (5.232 m)
    height:               230.09 in  (5.844 m) — varies by corner

    = 20 × 10 × ~11.18 cubits
    = ratio 2:1:√(5/4)

    floor:                granite, not perfectly flat
    walls:                5 courses of granite, polished
    ceiling:              9 granite beams, each ~27 ft long, 5 ft wide
                          weight 25-80 tonnes each
    joints:               0.02 in (0.5 mm) average
    relieving chambers:   5 above ceiling (4 discovered by Howard Vyse)

    coffer (sarcophagus — Petrie §57-61):
      N end:  outer 89.62, inner 78.06 in
      E side: outer 38.5,  inner 26.81 in
      W side: outer 38.5,  inner 26.69 in
      height outside:     41.31 in
      height inside:      34.42 in
      material:           granite, no lid found
      position:           offset from center of room

    air channels (Petrie §56):
      N channel:          inclines upward through pyramid
      S channel:          inclines upward through pyramid
      width:              ~9 in × ~5 in
      angles:             not precisely measured by Petrie

### Queen's Chamber (Petrie §40-44)

    location:       on E-W axis, below King's Chamber
    passage to:     horizontal, length ~1307 in from gallery

    floor (doorway):
      N-S:          ~5.8 ft to higher floor
      step up:      varies (floor joint raised)

    chamber:
      E-W:          ~227 in  (5.77 m)
      N-S:          ~206 in  (5.23 m)
      height walls:  ~182 in  (4.62 m)
      height apex:   ~245 in  (6.22 m) — pointed roof
      niche (E wall): 184 in high, 62 in wide at base, corbelled
                      depth 41 in, 5 stages

    air channels:
      N channel:    originally sealed at both ends
      S channel:    originally sealed at both ends
      opened by Waynman Dixon, 1872

## Khafre (Second Pyramid)

    pharaoh:        Khafre (Chephren), 4th Dynasty, ~2520 BCE

### Exterior (Petrie §65-72)

    base sides:
      N:            8474.9 in  (215.3 m)
      E:            8475.2 in  (215.3 m)
      S:            8476.9 in  (215.3 m)  
      W:            8475.5 in  (215.3 m)
      mean:         8475.6 in  (215.3 m)  = 411 cubits

    height:         5664 in  (143.9 m)  — original
    height now:     5371 in  (136.4 m)

    slope angle:    53°10′ ± 4′ (steeper than Khufu)
    courses:        remaining casing at apex (Tura limestone)
    lower casing:   pink granite (Aswan)

    leveled site:   the surrounding rock was cut down to create
                    a level platform, "almost indestructible"

### Interior (Petrie §73-78)

    entrance passage:   slope ~25°55′
    horizontal passage: leads to chamber
    great chamber:
      N-S:          ~564 in  (14.33 m)
      E-W:          ~197 in  (5.00 m)
      height:       pointed roof, granite
    coffer:         granite, sunk into floor
    lower chamber:  rough, with passage


## Menkaure (Third Pyramid)

    pharaoh:        Menkaure (Mycerinus), 4th Dynasty, ~2490 BCE

### Exterior (Petrie §79-83)

    base sides:
      N:            4153.6 in  (105.5 m)
      E:            4155.8 in  (105.6 m)
      S:            4150.5 in  (105.4 m)
      W:            4153.3 in  (105.5 m)
      mean:         4153.3 in  (105.5 m)  = 200 cubits

    height:         ~2564 in  (65.1 m)  — original
    height now:     ~2400 in  (61.0 m)

    slope angle:    51°0′ ± 10′
    casing lower:   red granite, 16-18 courses, unfinished
    casing upper:   Tura limestone

    peribolus walls: roughly built, some over 100 tonnes

### Interior (Petrie §84-89)

    entrance passage:   slope angle similar to Khufu
    first chamber:      lined
    second chamber:     lined
    granite chamber:    burial chamber
      sarcophagus:      basalt, decorated with palace facade
                        lost at sea 1838 (ship Beatrice, Bay of Biscay)
    loculus:            additional chamber
    original entrance:  different from later forced entry


## Ratios

    Khufu base:       440 cubits    height: 280 cubits
    Khafre base:      411 cubits    height: ~274 cubits
    Menkaure base:    200 cubits    height: ~125 cubits

    Khufu perimeter/height  = 1760/280 = 2π (error 0.05%)
    Khufu base/height       = 440/280 = 11/7 ≈ π/2
    Khufu seked             = 5.5 palms = 14:11 rise:run

    all three slopes:   51°0′ to 53°10′ (within 2°)
    all three:          aligned to cardinal directions
    all three:          on the Giza plateau diagonal

    cubit (Petrie §136): 20.632 ± 0.004 in (524.1 mm)
                         = π/6 meters within measurement error

    King's Chamber:     20 × 10 cubits (floor)
                        height = 10√(5/4) cubits
