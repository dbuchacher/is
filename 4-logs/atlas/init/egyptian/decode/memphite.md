# Memphite Theology — Shabaka Stone

Source: Lichtheim translation (Ancient Egyptian Literature, Vol 1).
Stone: British Museum EA 498. 25th Dynasty, reign of Shabaka (~710 BCE).
Claims to copy a worm-eaten Old Kingdom papyrus. Disputed — may be
later composition in deliberately archaic language.

The only continuous Egyptian creation narrative. Everything else is
scattered utterances, hymns, or embedded in other texts.

---

1. The gods who came into being in Ptah.

2. There took shape in the heart, there took shape on the tongue,
   the form of Atum.

3. For the very great one is Ptah, who gave [life] to all the gods
   and their Kas

4. through this heart and through this tongue,

5. in which Horus had taken shape as Ptah, in which Thoth had taken
   shape as Ptah.

6. Thus heart and tongue rule over all the limbs

7. in accordance with the teaching that it is in every body and it
   is in every mouth

8. of all gods, all men, all cattle, all creeping things, whatever
   lives,

9. thinking whatever it wishes and commanding whatever it wishes.

10. His Ennead is before him as teeth and lips. They are the semen
    and the hands of Atum.

11. For the Ennead of Atum came into being through his semen and
    his fingers.

12. But the Ennead is the teeth and the lips in this mouth which
    pronounced the name of every thing,

13. from which Shu and Tefnut came forth, and which gave birth to
    the Ennead.

14. {The eyes'} seeing, {the ears'} hearing, {the nose's} breathing
    {of air}; they report to the heart,

15. and it {is what} makes every understanding come forth.

16. As to the tongue, it repeats what the heart has devised.

17. He (Ptah) is the one who made all the gods, (also) Atum and
    his Ennead.

18. For every {hieroglyph} came about through what the heart devised
    and the tongue commanded.

19. Thus all the {male life-principles} were made and all the
    {female life-principles} determined they that make all foods
    and all provisions,

20. through this word {that makes} what is loved {and} what is hated.

21. Thus life is given to the peaceful, death is given to the criminal.

22. Thus all labor, all crafts are made, the action of the hands,
    the motion of the legs, the movements of all the limbs,

23. according to this command which is devised by the heart and comes
    forth on the tongue and creates the performance of every thing.

24. Thus it is said of Ptah: 'He who made all and created the gods'.

25. And he is Ta-tenen, who gave birth to the gods, and from whom
    every thing came forth,

26. foods, provisions, divine offerings, all good things.

27. He is (also) Thoth, the Wise, whose power is greater than that
    of the (other) gods.

28. So has Ptah come to rest after his making everything and every
    {hieroglyph} as well,

29. He gave birth to the gods, He made the towns, He established
    the nomes,

30. He placed the gods in their shrines, He settled their offerings,

31. He established their shrines, He made their bodies according to
    their wishes.

32. Thus the gods entered into their bodies, of every wood, every
    stone, every clay,

33. every thing that grows upon him in which they came to be.

34. Thus were gathered to him all the gods and their Kas, content,
    united with the Lord of the Two Lands.

---

## What it says (no framing)

Two-step creation: conceive in heart, speak with tongue. Done.

Pipeline: senses (eyes, ears, nose) → report to heart → heart
understands → tongue speaks → thing exists. Input → process → output.

The Ennead (nine gods) are not independent creators — they're the
"teeth and lips," the output apparatus. Ptah is the process; the
gods are the interface.

Line 18: the writing system itself is a product of this mechanism.
Hieroglyphs "came about through what the heart devised and the
tongue commanded."

Line 32-33: gods enter physical bodies "of every wood, every stone,
every clay." The pattern inhabits material hosts. The pattern is
primary, the material is secondary.

Line 21: the system evaluates. "Life to the peaceful, death to the
criminal." It's not neutral creation — it tests and judges.

Line 28: Ptah "comes to rest" after making everything. Not dies,
not disappears — ceases. Compare Genesis 2:2 shavat (ceased).

## Open questions

- Is the "worm-eaten papyrus" claim real or legitimizing fiction?
  Most scholars now lean later composition. Matters for dating the
  ideas, not for what the text says.
- The Lichtheim translation uses {curly braces} for restorations
  of damaged sections. Those words are educated guesses.
- Original hieroglyphic text of the Shabaka Stone itself — we don't
  have it in our sources. The raw-creation-glyphs.txt file may
  contain some of it but that's unverified.
