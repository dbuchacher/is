# In The Beginning

Two texts. Two civilizations. Same boot sequence.

One was carved on the walls of pyramids and a slab of black basalt
in Memphis. The other was written on scrolls in Hebrew. They
describe the same process: how everything initializes.

This isn't metaphor. Both texts describe a system coming online
in dependency order — the same order physics demands, the same
order a computer follows at power-on. They use different words
for the same operations. We're going to read them side by side.


## The Equation

Everything runs on one function with four inputs:

    f(x, m, q, t) = x^a * m^b * q^c * t^d

    x = space    where things are
    m = mass     what things are made of
    q = signal   how things differ from each other
    t = time     when things happen

Four dimensions. Every physics formula you've ever seen is some
combination of these four with different exponents. Velocity is
space per time. Energy is space-squared times mass per time-squared.
Bandwidth is mass per time. Voltage, pressure, density, power —
all of them. Four letters, different exponents.

They come online in a specific order, because each one depends
on the ones before it:

    x first   — you need somewhere to put things
    m second  — you need something to put there
    q third   — you need a way to tell things apart
    t last    — time means nothing until there's stuff to change

This is the dependency chain of reality. Genesis follows it.
The Big Bang follows it. The periodic table follows it. A CPU
at power-on follows it. Not because they copied each other —
because there's only one order that works.


## Before The Beginning

Both texts start the same way: something exists, but it's
formless. Not nothing — something with nothing in it.

**Genesis 1:2:**
> And the ground was empty-form and empty-content, and darkness
> over the face of the deep. And the equation's oscillation
> vibrating over the face of the waters.

**Memphite Theology, line 1:**
> The gods who came into being in Ptah.

Genesis describes the state: allocated but uninitialized. Two
kinds of empty — no structure (tohu) and no content (vohu).
Dark means no signal. The waters aren't ocean — they're
unstructured substance. Raw material with no shape.

Memphis describes the process: everything that will exist is
already inside Ptah, waiting to be spoken. The gods don't come
from outside — they "come into being IN Ptah." The program is
loaded but hasn't executed yet.

Both say the same thing: before initialization, the system
exists but has not run. The buffer is allocated. The bits are
there. Nothing has been written.

In the equation's terms: m exists (substance is present), but
x = 0 (no spatial structure), q = 0 (no signal), t = 0 (no
time). Three of four dimensions are dark.


## The First Write

**Genesis 1:1:**
> At initialization, the equation wrote the skies and the ground.

**Memphite, lines 2-4:**
> There took shape in the heart, there took shape on the tongue,
> the form of Atum. For the very great one is Ptah, who gave
> life to all the gods and their kas through this heart and
> through this tongue.

Genesis gives the operation: write space (skies) and mass
(ground). Two buffers allocated from nothing. The Hebrew verb
is bara — reserved exclusively for creation from nothing. It
appears six times in the entire creation text. Every other act
of making uses a different verb (asah — assembled from existing
parts). The distinction matters: bara = write new. asah = build
from what's there. Hardware created vs. software assembled.

Memphis gives the mechanism: heart conceives, tongue speaks,
thing exists. Input, process, output. The pipeline that creates
everything. Ptah doesn't build with hands — he thinks and speaks.
The thought IS the creation. Line 18 makes it explicit: "every
hieroglyph came about through what the heart devised and the
tongue commanded." The writing system itself is output of this
pipeline.

Both describe a write operation. New data where none existed.
Space and mass come online.


## Signal On

**Genesis 1:3:**
> And the equation said: let there be light. And there was light.

**Memphite, lines 14-16:**
> The eyes' seeing, the ears' hearing, the nose's breathing;
> they report to the heart, and it makes every understanding
> come forth. As to the tongue, it repeats what the heart
> has devised.

Genesis: signal activates. One command, instant result. Before
this moment, everything was the same — no way to tell one thing
from another. Dark doesn't mean scary blackness. It means no
information. No distinction. No signal on any wire. Then q turns
on and suddenly there's a DIFFERENCE between things.

In physics: the electromagnetic field comes online. Photons
exist. In computing: voltage — the wire goes high. In EE: your
reference level is established. Before: no signal on any wire.
After: bits can exist.

Memphis describes the same moment as a sensory pipeline: eyes
see, ears hear, nose breathes — all feeding the heart, which
processes and sends to the tongue for output. The senses ARE
the signal dimension activating. You can't sense anything
without q. The Memphite pipeline (sense -> heart -> tongue ->
existence) is the same as (read -> process -> write -> output).

And there's no sun yet. That's day four. So what is light?
It's not sunlight. It's the electromagnetic field. The ability
to carry information. The sun is a SOURCE of signal. The signal
itself is more fundamental than any source. Voltage exists
before you build the power supply.


## The First Test

**Genesis 1:4:**
> And the equation observed the light — functional. And the
> equation separated the light from the darkness.

**Memphite, lines 20-21:**
> Through this word that makes what is loved and what is hated.
> Thus life is given to the peaceful, death is given to the
> criminal.

Genesis: the first observation and the first evaluation. Two
operations in one verse — read (observed) and test (functional).
Before this, everything was write, invoke, or oscillate. Now
the system looks at its own output and judges it.

The Hebrew word tov doesn't mean morally "good." It means
functional. Meets spec. Works as intended. This is a test
passing, not a compliment.

Then separation: light from dark. Signal from absence of signal.
+1 from 0. The binary is defined. The system can tell the
difference between something and nothing, and the boundary
between them is now a real thing.

Memphis makes the same move but frames it as judgment: the
system evaluates what it produces. What works lives. What
doesn't dies. Not morality — selection. The pipeline doesn't
just create. It tests.


## The Clock Starts

**Genesis 1:5:**
> And the equation named the light "day" and the darkness
> "night." And there was a falling-edge and there was a
> rising-edge — day one.

**Memphite, line 28:**
> So has Ptah come to rest after his making everything.

Genesis: labels assigned, cycle begins, time starts. Day and
Night aren't sunrise and sunset — there's no sun. They're the
two states: signal-present and signal-absent. 1 and 0. The
first names in the system, for the first distinction.

"Evening and morning" — falling edge then rising edge. The
cycle starts from dark, from zero, from off. Then rises. That's
your clock cycle. The tick. And the word is echad — "one," not
"first." Cardinal, not ordinal. This isn't the first in a
series. This is the origin. t = 1.

Now all four dimensions are online:

    x — space    (1:1, the skies)
    m — mass     (1:1, the ground)
    q — signal   (1:3, light)
    t — time     (1:5, day one)

The creation sequence isn't random. It's the minimum viable
universe, initialized in dependency order. You need addresses
before you can store data. You need data before you can
distinguish it. You need distinction before time means anything.

Memphis reaches the same endpoint from the other direction:
Ptah "comes to rest" after making everything. Not dies, not
disappears — ceases. The process is complete. Compare Genesis
2:2: the equation "ceased" (shavat) on day seven. Both words
describe the same state: the initializer has finished. The
system runs itself now.


## What Just Happened

Five verses. Four dimensions online. Two civilizations, two
languages, two millennia apart, same sequence:

| step | Genesis              | Memphis                    | what it does          |
|------|----------------------|----------------------------|-----------------------|
| 0    | tohu va-vohu         | gods inside Ptah           | allocated, not run    |
| 1    | bara shamayim/aretz  | heart shapes Atum          | write space + mass    |
| 2    | yehi or              | senses report to heart     | signal on             |
| 3    | va-yar ki-tov        | loved/hated, life/death    | observe + test        |
| 4    | erev/voqer yom echad | Ptah comes to rest         | clock starts          |

The texts aren't describing magic. They're describing
initialization. The same initialization every computer performs
at power-on, every universe performs at the Big Bang, every
cell performs at division. The pattern is the same because
there's only one order that works.

The pyramid walls and the Hebrew scrolls encoded the same
sequence in the mediums available to them. Stone and ink. This
document encodes it in a third medium. The encoding changes.
The sequence doesn't.

This is the first stone.
