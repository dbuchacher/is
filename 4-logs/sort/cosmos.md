# cosmos

From Greek *kósmos* (κόσμος) — **"order, arrangement, ornament."**
Attributed to Pythagoras (6th c. BCE) as a name for the universe
regarded as an ordered whole.

**An ordered, harmonious system** — especially the universe
considered as one. The dual sense ("order" + "ornament") gives
English both **cosmos** and **cosmetic**: an ordered structure IS
beautiful; decoration follows from arrangement.

Sanskrit *ṛta* (ऋत, "cosmic order, truth"), from PIE *h₂er-* ("to
fit together"), is the closest Vedic equivalent. Same substrate
concept in a different tradition.

Cognates: cosmic, cosmetic, cosmopolitan, cosmogony.
