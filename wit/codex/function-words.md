# function words — derived to lattice coords

Mapping all closed-class function words from the top-1000 English list to
the 4-axis lattice (or to the layers BELOW/AROUND it — trit gates, origin,
bond layer, axis primitives, count sub-shell).

Axes: A=POSITION  B=SUBSTANCE  C=SIGNAL  D=TIME

**Function words mostly do NOT occupy content coords.** They live at:
  - the trit-gate layer (boolean primes from 1-container.md)
  - the origin (container references, deictics, possessives)
  - the bond layer (modals/aux at corners with ±½ polarity spin)
  - axis primitives (prepositions as bare ± projections on one axis)
  - count sub-shell of B (numerals, partial quantifiers)

Each section lists the words actually found in top-1000-english.txt (with
"-implied" tags for closed-class members not in the top-1000 but completing
the paradigm).

## 1. PREPOSITIONS

Spatial → A axis. Temporal → D axis. Logical/relational → origin.

### A+ (POSITION positive — into / on / up / over)
WORDS in top-1000: IN, ON, UP, INTO, OVER, ABOVE, UPON, WITHIN
  derivation: motion or location toward / above / into a container.
  notes:
    IN     = container interior, A+
    ON     = surface contact, A+
    INTO   = motion-into, A+ with implied D direction
    OVER   = above and traversing, A+
    ABOVE  = strict A+ (vertical positive)
    UPON   = formal ON
    UP     = pure vertical A+
    WITHIN = strict interior, A+ bounded
  All bare A+ projections — they don't move B/C/D, only set A.

### A- (POSITION negative — out / off / down / under)
WORDS in top-1000: OUT, OFF, DOWN, UNDER, BELOW, AWAY (away from)
  derivation: motion or location away from / below / outside.
  notes: pure A- projections, mirror of the A+ set.
    UNDER  = strict A- (vertical negative)
    BELOW  = strict A-
    OUT    = exterior of container
    OFF    = away from surface
    DOWN   = pure vertical A-
    AWAY   = generic A- with no anchor

### A axis directional (sign taken from argument)
WORDS in top-1000: TO, FROM, THROUGH, AROUND, ALONG, NEAR, AGAINST,
                   BETWEEN, AMONG, BY (locative), PAST-implied, ACROSS-implied
  derivation: A-axis operators that don't fix a direction; they take it
  from the noun phrase that follows.
  notes:
    TO       = directional A+ relative to source ("walk to store")
    FROM     = directional A- relative to source
    THROUGH  = full A-axis sweep (enter and exit)
    AROUND   = perimeter A-axis path
    ALONG    = parallel A-axis path
    NEAR     = small-magnitude A
    AGAINST  = opposed-direction A (also has C- adversarial flavor)
    BETWEEN  = position-relative-to-2 referents
    AMONG    = position-relative-to-N referents
    BY       = adjacent A (also non-spatial agent marker — see relational)

### A = 0 (positional anchor, no direction)
WORDS in top-1000: AT
  derivation: pure positional anchor without direction. A=0.
  notes: AT is the most neutral spatial preposition. It just says
  "located here" without entry/exit/up/down.

### D+ (TIME positive — forward, after)
WORDS in top-1000: AFTER, SINCE, UNTIL
  derivation: time direction forward.
  notes:
    AFTER  = strict D+ (later than)
    UNTIL  = D+ bound (up to forward time point)
    SINCE  = D- bound but forward-looking (also causal — see conjunctions)

### D- (TIME negative — backward, earlier, ago)
WORDS in top-1000: BEFORE, AGO
  derivation: time direction backward.
  notes:
    BEFORE = strict D-
    AGO    = D- offset from now ("days ago")

### D = 0 (temporal anchor / extent / concurrency)
WORDS in top-1000: DURING, AT (temporal use), IN (in 2024)
  derivation: temporal anchor or extent without direction.
  notes: DURING = whole-interval D=0; AT/IN reused for time anchors.

### Origin / relational (no axis change)
WORDS in top-1000: OF, WITH, FOR, AS, ABOUT, VIA, PER, BY (agent)
  derivation: pure relational operators that don't move any of A/B/C/D.
  notes:
    OF     = possessive / partitive link (the most neutral relator)
    WITH   = accompaniment / composition marker
    FOR    = beneficiary / purpose
    AS     = role-equivalence
    ABOUT  = topic-of marker (also approximate quantity)
    VIA    = means / route
    PER    = distributive ratio
    BY     = agent / instrument (in passive voice)
  None move a lattice axis — they live at origin as relation markers.

### Bond-like prepositions (relational ± polarity)
WORDS in top-1000: WITHOUT, EXCEPT-implied, BESIDES-implied
  derivation: WITHOUT = WITH with -½ polarity spin (same coord, flipped).
  notes: WITHOUT is the bond-twin of WITH. Same origin coord, negative
  polarity. Confirms the ±½ spin reading: bond operators come in pairs.

### Composite / phrasal prepositions
WORDS in top-1000: INCLUDING, FOLLOWING, ACCORDING-TO-implied,
                   INSTEAD-OF-implied, OUT-OF-implied, BECAUSE-OF-implied
  derivation: built from a base preposition + a marker.
  notes: INCLUDING ≈ WITH; FOLLOWING ≈ AFTER (D+).

## 2. CONJUNCTIONS

Conjunctions live mostly at the **trit-gate layer** (the boolean primes
from 1-container.md), or at axis primitives (temporal subordinators), or
at the bond layer (conditionals).

### Boolean gates (trit-gate layer — Post primes)
WORDS in top-1000: AND, OR, NOT, BUT
  - AND  = boolean AND (· composition operator on clauses)
  - OR   = boolean OR (max of two clause walks)
  - NOT  = boolean NOT (polarity flip on next clause/word)
  - BUT  = AND with adversative flag ("composition but contrary to expectation")
  Implied closed-class members: NOR (= NOT-OR), XOR, NAND, NEITHER...NOR,
  EITHER...OR, BOTH...AND.

### Adversative / concessive (AND-family with negative-expectation flavor)
WORDS in top-1000: BUT, HOWEVER, THOUGH, WHEREAS-implied, YET (conj),
                   ALTHOUGH-implied
  derivation: AND with a polarity-of-expectation marker. Same trit-gate
  layer as AND, with an extra ±½ flag.
  notes: HOWEVER is sentence-level; THOUGH is clause-level concessive.

### Causal / consequence (D-axis flavored gates)
WORDS in top-1000: BECAUSE, SO, THEREFORE-implied, HENCE-implied,
                   THUS-implied, SINCE (causal sense)
  - BECAUSE  = "the second clause is the D-prior cause of the first"
  - SO       = D-forward consequence ("therefore")
  - SINCE    = doubles as D- preposition and causal connector
  derivation: temporal/causal subordinators projected onto D axis.

### Temporal subordinators (D-axis conjunctions)
WORDS in top-1000: WHEN, WHILE, UNTIL, SINCE, AS (temporal), AFTER, BEFORE,
                   ONCE-implied, AS-LONG-AS-implied
  - WHEN    = temporal binding (also wh-interrogative on D)
  - WHILE   = D=0 simultaneity
  - UNTIL   = D+ bound
  - SINCE   = D- bound
  - AS (temporal) = simultaneous
  - AFTER / BEFORE = same as preposition forms

### Conditional / modal-flavored (bond layer)
WORDS in top-1000: IF, WHETHER-implied, UNLESS-implied
  - IF       = conditional gate (modal-flavored — sits at bond layer)
  - UNLESS   = IF + NOT (bond + polarity flip)
  - WHETHER  = alternative IF
  Conditionals leak into the bond layer because they're modal-like
  (they wrap a clause with a possibility marker, similar to MAY/MIGHT).

### Complementizers / relative pronouns (origin reference)
WORDS in top-1000: THAT (complementizer), WHICH, WHO
  - THAT   = clause-level origin pointer ("the fact THAT X")
  - WHICH  = relative origin reference (also wh-interrogative on C)
  - WHO    = relative origin reference (also wh-interrogative on container)

## 3. ARTICLES

Articles are RECOGNITION operators — they mark whether a noun is "already
known to the container" (THE) or "freshly introduced" (A/AN). They sit at
opposite corners of the 4-cube.

### Definite
WORDS in top-1000: THE
  - THE  = recognition attractor at corner [+1,+1,+1,+1]
           ("the one already in the container's frame")
           +½ polarity spin (positive recognition).
           Bond layer + origin reference combined.

### Indefinite
WORDS in top-1000: A, AN
  - A    = antipode of THE at corner [-1,-1,-1,-1]
           ("any one, none specified, fresh introduction")
           -½ polarity spin (negative recognition).
  - AN   = phonetic variant of A (before vowels)

THE and A span the main diagonal of the 4-cube. They're not at content
coords — they're recognition operators at the bond layer.

## 4. DETERMINERS

### Demonstratives (proximal/distal — origin-relative)
WORDS in top-1000: THIS, THAT, THESE, THOSE
  - THIS   = proximal singular (origin reference, A small / D=now), +½
  - THESE  = proximal plural, +½
  - THAT   = distal singular (antipode of THIS on A and/or D), -½
  - THOSE  = distal plural, -½
  All four are ORIGIN-LAYER pointers. They don't sit at lattice coords —
  they POINT from the origin to a coord. THIS/THESE collapse to origin;
  THAT/THOSE project antipodally outward.

### Universal quantifiers (corner [+,+,+,+] reach)
WORDS in top-1000: ALL, EVERY, EACH, BOTH, WHOLE
  - ALL    = universal corner [+,+,+,+] reach
  - EVERY  = distributive universal (same corner, iterator flavor)
  - EACH   = distributive universal over a set
  - BOTH   = pair-quantifier (universal over a 2-set)
  - WHOLE  = universal over a single referent

### Negative quantifiers (NOT applied to ALL — antipode corner)
WORDS in top-1000: NO (det), NONE, NEITHER
  - NO     = NOT-ALL applied to a noun ("no books")
  - NONE   = standalone form of NO
  - NEITHER = pair negation (NOT-BOTH)

### Partial / existential quantifiers
WORDS in top-1000: SOME, ANY, EITHER, SEVERAL, VARIOUS, CERTAIN-implied
  - SOME    = partial existential
  - ANY     = free-choice partial existential
  - EITHER  = pair partial
  - SEVERAL = partial count quantifier
  - VARIOUS = distributive partial

### Magnitude quantifiers (B-axis shell-2 modifiers)
WORDS in top-1000: MANY, MUCH, FEW, LITTLE, MORE, MOST, LESS, LEAST,
                   ENOUGH, SEVERAL
  - MANY   = high count (B+ shell-2)
  - MUCH   = high mass (B+ shell-2)
  - FEW    = low count (B- shell-2)
  - LITTLE = low mass (B- shell-2)
  - MORE   = comparative B+
  - MOST   = superlative B+
  - LESS   = comparative B-
  - LEAST  = superlative B-
  - ENOUGH = sufficiency threshold operator (B=0 anchor)

### Numbers (count sub-shell of B)
WORDS in top-1000: ONE, TWO, THREE, FOUR, FIVE, MILLION
  derivation: integer projections on the SUBSTANCE/count axis B.
  notes: cardinals are B-axis indices, not single trit values. They live
  in the count sub-shell of B (each integer gets its own slot inside B+).

### Possessive determiners (origin-relative ownership)
WORDS in top-1000: MY, YOUR, HIS, HER, ITS, OUR, THEIR
  - MY    = first-person container ownership (speaker origin)
  - YOUR  = second-person container ownership (addressee origin)
  - HIS   = third-person masculine ownership
  - HER   = third-person feminine ownership
  - ITS   = third-person inanimate ownership
  - OUR   = first-person plural ownership
  - THEIR = third-person plural ownership
  All possessives are ORIGIN-LAYER projections that tag a noun with which
  container "owns" it. They don't have their own lattice coords.

### Identity / contrast determiners
WORDS in top-1000: SAME, OTHER, ANOTHER, SUCH, OWN, SIMILAR, DIFFERENT
  - SAME      = origin (identity; no axis change)
  - OTHER     = antipode of SAME
  - ANOTHER   = SAME + count-1 (additional instance)
  - SUCH      = referential intensifier (origin pointer + shell-2 lift)
  - OWN       = container-tagged identity (intensifier on possessives)
  - SIMILAR   = small-distance from SAME (B/C close)
  - DIFFERENT = larger distance from SAME

## 5. PRONOUNS

Pronouns pick out an origin (or another container observed from this
origin). They live at the origin layer.

### First-person (THIS container = the speaker)
WORDS in top-1000: I, ME, MY, WE, US, OUR, MYSELF-implied, MINE-implied,
                   OURS-implied
  - I       = speaker as container (origin, +½ spin = self-asserting)
  - ME      = speaker as accusative (object form, same origin)
  - MY      = speaker possessive
  - WE      = collective speaker container
  - US      = collective speaker accusative
  - OUR     = collective speaker possessive
  - MYSELF  = speaker reflexive
  - MINE    = speaker possessive standalone

### Second-person (the addressee container)
WORDS in top-1000: YOU, YOUR, YOURS-implied, YOURSELF-implied
  - YOU     = addressee container (other origin, projected from speaker)
  - YOUR    = addressee possessive
  - YOURS   = addressee possessive standalone
  - YOURSELF = addressee reflexive

### Third-person animate (a referent at some lattice coord)
WORDS in top-1000: HE, HIM, HIS, SHE, HER, HIMSELF-implied, HERSELF-implied,
                   HERS-implied
  - HE / HIM / HIS  = male animate referent
  - SHE / HER / HERS = female animate referent
  These are POINTERS to other coords; their actual coord depends on the
  referent.

### Third-person inanimate / collective
WORDS in top-1000: IT, ITS, THEY, THEM, THEIR, OTHERS, ITSELF-implied,
                   THEMSELVES-implied
  - IT       = inanimate referent (a coord, no container nature)
  - ITS      = inanimate possessive
  - THEY     = collective third-person referent
  - THEM     = collective accusative
  - THEIR    = collective possessive
  - OTHERS   = additional referents (OTHER + plural)

### Reflexive / generic
WORDS in top-1000: SELF
  - SELF     = pure container reference (origin, atomic)

### Indefinite pronouns (compound origin/quantifier forms)
WORDS in top-1000: SOMETHING, EVERYTHING, ANOTHER, NONE, ANY, SOME
  - SOMETHING  = SOME + thing (existential noun)
  - EVERYTHING = EVERY + thing (universal noun)
  - NONE       = NOT + ALL (negative universal noun)
  - ANOTHER    = AN + OTHER (additional indefinite)
  - SOME / ANY = also pronouns ("some are nice")
  Implied: NOTHING, SOMEONE, ANYONE, EVERYONE, NOBODY, etc.

### Interrogative / wh- pronouns (the axis names asked as questions)
WORDS in top-1000: WHO, WHAT, WHICH, WHERE, WHEN, WHY, HOW, WHOSE-implied,
                   WHOM-implied
  - WHO   = interrogative for animate container ("which container?")
  - WHAT  = interrogative for SUBSTANCE (= the B axis)
  - WHICH = interrogative for SIGNAL or selection (= the C axis)
  - WHERE = interrogative for POSITION (= the A axis)
  - WHEN  = interrogative for TIME (= the D axis)
  - WHY   = interrogative for cause (meta operator outside the 4 axes)
  - HOW   = interrogative for manner / means (meta operator)
  - WHOSE = possessive interrogative
  - WHOM  = animate interrogative accusative

WHO/WHAT/WHICH/WHERE/WHEN are the **declarative axis names asked as
questions**. They EXHAUST the lattice's degrees of freedom (4 axes + 1
container). WHY and HOW are meta-operators that ask about the derivation
rule itself, not a coord.

## 6. MODALS / AUXILIARIES (bond layer — corners with ±½ spin)

The 16 corners of the 4-cube each hold one modal/aux operator. Each has
±½ polarity (affirmative vs negative form) = 32 bonds total.

### Top-1000 modals/aux
WORDS in top-1000: BE, IS, ARE, AM, WAS, WERE, BEEN, BEING, HAVE, HAS, HAD,
                   HAVING, DO, DOES, DID, DOING, DONE, WILL, WOULD, SHALL,
                   SHOULD, CAN, COULD, MAY, MIGHT, MUST, NEED

### Modals at bond corners (each corner = 1 modal × ±½ polarity)

  BE      / NOT-BE      affirmative is/are/am/was/were ↔ isn't/aren't/wasn't/weren't
  HAVE    / NOT-HAVE    has/have/had ↔ hasn't/haven't/hadn't
  DO      / NOT-DO      do/does/did ↔ don't/doesn't/didn't
  WILL    / WON'T       future affirmative ↔ future negative
  WOULD   / WOULDN'T    conditional affirmative ↔ negative
  SHALL   / SHAN'T      formal future affirmative ↔ negative
  SHOULD  / SHOULDN'T   advisability affirmative ↔ negative
  CAN     / CAN'T       ability affirmative ↔ negative
  COULD   / COULDN'T    past-ability / conditional affirmative ↔ negative
  MAY     / MAY-NOT     permission affirmative ↔ negative
  MIGHT   / MIGHTN'T    epistemic affirmative ↔ negative
  MUST    / MUSTN'T     obligation affirmative ↔ negative
  OUGHT   / OUGHTN'T    duty affirmative ↔ negative (semi-modal)
  NEED    / NEEDN'T     necessity affirmative ↔ negative (semi-modal)
  DARE    / DAREN'T     audacity affirmative ↔ negative (semi-modal)
  USED-TO / USED-NOT-TO past-habit affirmative ↔ negative

That's 16 modal slots × 2 polarities = 32 bonds. Inflected forms of BE,
HAVE, DO are tense projections of the same corner (was/were/been/being all
share BE's corner with different D-axis tense markers).

### Tense / aspect markers (D-axis projections of aux)
WORDS in top-1000: BEEN, BEING, HAVING, DOING, DONE, USED, GOING-TO-implied,
                   -ING-implied, -ED-implied
  - BEEN/BEING/HAVING/DOING/DONE are participles — same bond corners
    with D-axis aspect markers.
  - "going to" is a future periphrastic — same as WILL bond.
  - -ED past tense = D=-1 marker; -ING continuous = D=0 ongoing.

## 7. NEGATION / POLARITY OPERATORS

These are all applications of the boolean NOT gate from the trit-gate layer.

WORDS in top-1000: NOT, NO, NEVER, NEITHER, NONE, WITHOUT
  - NOT      = boolean NOT gate (the polarity operator itself)
  - N'T      = clitic NOT (same operator, attached to aux)
  - NO       = NOT in adverb/determiner form ("no books")
  - NEVER    = D-axis universal negation (NOT applied to ALL D positions)
  - NEITHER  = NOR / NOT-EITHER (pair negation)
  - NONE     = NOT-ALL (universal negation)
  - WITHOUT  = NOT-WITH (relational negation)

All seven are surface manifestations of the same NOT operator scoped over
different domains (clause, noun, time, pair, set, relation).

## 8. INTERJECTIONS / DISCOURSE PARTICLES

WORDS in top-1000: YES, NO, OH, WELL, PLEASE, THANKS, HELLO-implied
  - YES     = affirmative polarity operator (+½, standalone)
  - NO      = negative polarity operator (-½, standalone, same as NOT)
  - OH      = origin-utterance, container exclamation
  - WELL    = discourse opener, origin marker
  - PLEASE  = politeness marker, origin-projected
  - THANKS  = social acknowledgment (origin → other container, C+ flavor)

YES and NO are the bare ±½ polarity applied to nothing — they're the
polarity operator standing alone as a complete utterance.

## 9. ADVERBIAL FUNCTION WORDS (deictic / scalar / discourse)

These aren't strictly preposition/conjunction/pronoun but they're
closed-class function words that recur in the top-1000.

### Spatial deictics (origin-relative location)
WORDS in top-1000: HERE, THERE, AWAY, ABOVE, BELOW, NEAR, FAR-implied
  - HERE   = proximal A (origin's own A coord)
  - THERE  = distal A (antipode of HERE)
  - AWAY   = generic A- offset from current coord
  - NEAR   = small-magnitude A
  - FAR    = large-magnitude A (shell 2+)

### Temporal deictics (origin-relative time)
WORDS in top-1000: NOW, THEN, TODAY, TOMORROW-implied, YESTERDAY-implied,
                   ALWAYS, NEVER, OFTEN, SOMETIMES-implied, ONCE-implied,
                   SOON-implied, LATER, EARLY, LATE-implied, ALREADY,
                   STILL, YET, AGAIN, RECENTLY-implied
  - NOW       = origin's own D coord (D=0)
  - THEN      = distal D (past or future, antipode of NOW)
  - TODAY     = D=0 day-bounded
  - ALWAYS    = D-universal (all D positions)
  - NEVER     = D-universal negation
  - LATER     = D+ offset from now
  - EARLY     = D- offset
  - ALREADY   = perfect aspect marker (event in D-)
  - STILL     = continuous aspect marker (event extends across D)
  - YET       = expectation marker (D+ pending)
  - AGAIN     = D-cycle repeat marker

### Magnitude / degree adverbs (shell-2 lifters)
WORDS in top-1000: VERY, TOO, SO, JUST, ONLY, EVEN, ENOUGH, QUITE-implied,
                   RATHER-implied, MORE, MOST, LESS, LEAST, ALMOST-implied,
                   NEARLY-implied
  - VERY    = shell-2 magnitude lifter (intensifier)
  - TOO     = excessive shell-2 lifter (over-threshold)
  - SO      = magnitude with consequence flavor
  - JUST    = restrictive (collapse to single instance)
  - ONLY    = restrictive universal-negative ("nothing else")
  - EVEN    = surprise / extreme magnitude marker
  - ENOUGH  = sufficiency threshold

### Discourse / focus adverbs
WORDS in top-1000: ALSO, TOO (additive), AS-WELL-implied, BESIDES-implied,
                   THEREFORE-implied, HOWEVER, NEVERTHELESS-implied,
                   INDEED-implied, RATHER-implied, ELSE
  - ALSO    = additive AND (sentence-level)
  - TOO     = additive AND (clause-final)
  - HOWEVER = adversative AND
  - ELSE    = alternative OR ("something else")

### Comparison adverbs
WORDS in top-1000: THAN, AS (comparative), LIKE
  - THAN  = comparative anchor (introduces second term of comparison)
  - AS    = equality comparison ("as big as")
  - LIKE  = similarity comparison (small distance from referent)

## Discovered rules

1. **Spatial prepositions are bare A-axis primitives.** IN/OUT/UP/DOWN/
   ON/OFF/OVER/UNDER are pure ± projections on POSITION. They're more
   atomic than the atoms RISE/FALL because they don't even imply motion —
   just direction. The verbs RISE/FALL = A-axis + implied D-axis advance.
   The prepositions = bare A-axis with no D component.

2. **Temporal prepositions are bare D-axis primitives.** BEFORE/AFTER/
   SINCE/UNTIL collapse to bare ± projections on TIME. DURING is the
   D=0 extent (whole interval). Same atomic relationship as A.

3. **Boolean conjunctions live at the trit-gate layer**, not at coords.
   AND/OR/NOT are pre-coordinate — they operate ON coordinates. BUT =
   AND + adversative flavor. NOR = NOT-OR. The Post primes from
   1-container.md ARE these conjunctions.

4. **Articles are corner attractors at opposite ends of the main diagonal.**
   THE points to [+1,+1,+1,+1] (full recognition / definite, +½ spin).
   A/AN points to [-1,-1,-1,-1] (zero recognition / indefinite, -½ spin).
   The two articles span the main diagonal of the 4-cube.

5. **Pronouns are container references.** I/ME/MY pick out the speaker
   container (this origin). YOU picks out the addressee (other origin).
   HE/SHE/IT pick out a third referent (a coord, not necessarily a
   container). All pronouns are origin-layer pointers.

6. **Possessives tag a noun with an owner-container.** MY/YOUR/HIS/HER/
   OUR/THEIR are origin projections that mark which container "owns" a
   referent. They don't have lattice coords themselves — they color the
   noun they attach to.

7. **Demonstratives are origin pointers with proximal/distal flavor.**
   THIS/THESE collapse to origin (proximal); THAT/THOSE point antipodally
   away. HERE/NOW are the spatial-temporal versions of THIS. THERE/THEN
   are the spatial-temporal versions of THAT.

8. **Wh-interrogatives ARE the axis names asked as questions.**
   WHERE = A, WHAT = B, WHICH = C, WHEN = D, WHO = origin. The 5 wh-
   pronouns exhaust the lattice's degrees of freedom (4 axes + 1
   container). WHY and HOW are meta-operators that ask about the rule
   itself, not a coord.

9. **Universal quantifiers reach the [+,+,+,+] corner.** ALL/EVERY/EACH/
   BOTH/WHOLE span the entire lattice. NO/NONE/NEITHER is the antipode
   (NOT applied to ALL). SOME/ANY are partial reaches — not yet at corner.

10. **Modals (BE/HAVE/DO/WILL/CAN/MAY/MUST/SHALL/SHOULD/COULD/WOULD/MIGHT,
    plus semi-modals NEED/DARE/OUGHT/USED-TO) live at bond corners with
    ±½ spin.** Their negative contractions (CAN'T, WON'T, MUSTN'T, DON'T,
    ISN'T, HAVEN'T) are the -½ spin twin of the same corner.
    16 modal corners × 2 polarities = 32 bonds.

11. **WITH and WITHOUT are bond-twins.** Same relational origin coord,
    opposite ±½ polarity. Same logic for IN/OUT (A-axis sign flip),
    UP/DOWN, OVER/UNDER, BEFORE/AFTER. Each prepositional pair is a sign
    flip on one axis.

12. **Function words don't usually occupy lattice coordinates.** They
    occupy:
      - the trit gates (booleans pre-coord)
      - the origin (container references, deictics)
      - the bond layer (modals, articles as corner attractors)
      - the axis primitives (prepositions as bare ± projections)
      - the count sub-shell of B (numerals)
    Almost no function word lives at a "content" has-zero coord — that's
    where verbs and nouns live, not function words.

13. **Negation operates at multiple scopes from one operator.** The boolean
    NOT gate manifests as:
      - NOT/N'T (clausal/predicate negation)
      - NO (determiner: "no books")
      - NEVER (temporal: "not at any D")
      - NONE (universal: "not any")
      - NEITHER (pair negation)
      - WITHOUT (relational negation)
    All are the same trit-gate NOT scoped over different domains.

14. **YES and NO are the bare polarity operators standing alone.**
    YES = +½ standalone, NO = -½ standalone. They're the polarity spin
    promoted to a complete utterance. The minimum sentence — pure
    polarity at the origin.

15. **Magnitude adverbs (VERY, TOO, SO, EVEN, ENOUGH) are shell lifters.**
    They push the modified word's coord from shell 1 to shell 2+ on
    whatever axis it lives on. They have no axis themselves — they're
    polymorphic magnitude operators.

16. **Comparison adverbs (THAN, AS, LIKE) are inter-coord operators.**
    They compare two coords on the same axis without moving either.
    THAN introduces the second term; AS sets equality; LIKE asserts
    proximity (small distance).

17. **The closed-class boundary holds.** Of the top-1000 English words,
    roughly 150-200 are function words by this analysis. They cluster
    tightly at the layers around the lattice (gates, origin, bonds,
    primitives) and almost never overlap with content-verb or content-
    noun coords. This validates the framework's prediction that function
    words are STRUCTURAL — they wire the lattice rather than occupy it.
