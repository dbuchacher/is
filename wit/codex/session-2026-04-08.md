## What got proven

1. **The 8 atoms can derive most common English vocabulary.** 555 words
   confirmed with coords. ~37 distinct lattice positions are populated.

2. **Sentences compose via the · operator.** 25 worked examples,
   spanning bare imperatives to multi-clause sentences with negation
   and quantification. Composition is element-wise trit addition;
   no new primitives needed.

3. **Real texts translate cleanly.** Three real texts (Genesis 1:1-5,
   Genesis 1:6-13, Matthew 6:9-13) walked through the lattice
   word-by-word. Every word coord derived from atoms; every verse
   coord composed via · operator.

4. **The framework's predictions hold under translation.**
   - "Heavens AND earth" sums to A=0 (antipodal cancellation =
     full axis coverage)
   - "Light AND darkness" sums to C=0 (binary established)
   - "Evening AND morning" sums to D=0 (one full oscillation)
   - The Lord's Prayer concentrates at signal shell-10
   - Both religious texts dominate on the C+ axis

5. **Container = self = origin works.** ~75 words at origin —
   verbs of cognition/perception/speech/being plus abstract nouns
   plus first-person pronouns plus proximal adverbs. The container
   layer is internally consistent across word classes.

6. **Noun-verb collision verified.** HAT=BUILD, MONEY=BLESS, DOG=LIVE,
   HOLE=DEMOLISH all confirmed. The lattice is part-of-speech polymorphic.

7. **35 derivation rules emerged from doing the work.** Not stipulated
   in advance — discovered through derivation. Each traces back to
   the 8 atoms.

8. **Negation works at three scales**: trit gate (1-container.md),
   polarity ±½ (bond level, the n't contraction), antipodal coord
   flip (the antonym structure). Same NOT operator at three scopes.

9. **Aspect of religious texts: signal axis dominates.** Both
   Genesis 1 and Lord's Prayer concentrate at high C+. This is a
   falsifiable prediction the framework makes about religious
   language: it should disproportionately move signal compared to
   secular language. (To test: see the-road.md when it's done.)
