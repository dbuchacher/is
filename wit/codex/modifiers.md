# adjectives + adverbs derived to lattice coords

Mapping the adjectives and adverbs from top-1000 English to the 4-axis lattice.
Adjectives are static axis projections (no movement, just position).
Adverbs are magnitudes/intensifiers/operators on the same axes.

Axes: A=POSITION  B=SUBSTANCE  C=SIGNAL  D=TIME
Trits: {-1, 0, +1}; shell-2 magnitudes use {-2, +2}.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

## ADJECTIVES

### [0,0,+1,0]  C+ (BLESS axis — pure positive signal)
WORDS: GOOD, BEST, GREAT, BETTER, TRUE, RIGHT, FREE, SPECIAL, POPULAR, FAMOUS-implied, OFFICIAL, IMPORTANT, REAL, NATURAL, CLEAR, SURE, FUN, WELCOME, EASY, SAFE-implied
  derivation: positive signal/value/fortune. GOOD/TRUE/RIGHT are textbook
    C+ atoms; BETTER/BEST are shell-2 intensifications [0,0,+2,0].
  notes: BETTER and BEST go to shell 2 ([0,0,+2,0]); GREAT to shell 2 also.
    FREE = positive value (no cost).

### [0,0,-1,0]  C- (CURSE axis — pure negative signal)
WORDS: BAD, WRONG-implied, FALSE-implied, HARD (difficult sense), POOR-implied
  derivation: negative signal/value. BAD is the textbook C- atom.
  notes: HARD straddles SUBSTANCE+ (dense) and C- (difficult). Listed both.

### [0,+1,0,0]  B+ (GROW axis — substance/quantity positive)
WORDS: BIG, LARGE, FULL, MUCH, MANY, MORE, MAJOR, WIDE, HEAVY-implied, HIGH (quantity sense)
  derivation: pure substance magnitude positive. MORE = comparative B+.
  notes: LARGE / BIG at shell 1; HUGE / MASSIVE would be shell 2.
    MANY is the count-version of MUCH (mass/count split, same axis).

### [0,-1,0,0]  B- (SHRINK axis — substance/quantity negative)
WORDS: SMALL, LITTLE, LESS, FEW, LOW (quantity sense), THIN-implied, LIGHT (weight sense)
  derivation: pure substance magnitude negative. LESS = comparative B-.
  notes: LIGHT is dual-mapped (also C+ for the brightness sense).

### [+1,0,0,0]  A+ (RISE — vertical / forward position)
WORDS: HIGH (height sense), TOP, UP, NORTH, FRONT, ABOVE, OVER
  derivation: position positive (vertical or directional forward).
  notes: HIGH/TOP/UP are pure A+. Adjectival use of NORTH and FRONT lives here.

### [-1,0,0,0]  A- (FALL — vertical / backward position)
WORDS: LOW (height sense), DOWN, SOUTH, BACK, UNDER, BELOW
  derivation: position negative.
  notes: BACK/REAR = A-; LOW (height) vs LOW (count) split.

### [0,0,0,+1]  D+ (BEGIN — temporal forward)
WORDS: NEW, NEXT, FUTURE, LATEST, RECENT, YOUNG, EARLY, LATER, FOLLOWING, PRESENT, CURRENT
  derivation: time-axis positive (newness, forward). NEW = the textbook D+ adjective.
  notes: CURRENT/PRESENT sit at temporal origin really, but lean D+
    relative to past. RECENT = mild D+. YOUNG = D+ on lifespan.

### [0,0,0,-1]  D- (END — temporal backward)
WORDS: OLD, LAST, PAST, PREVIOUS, FORMER-implied, FINAL, LATE-implied, ANCIENT-implied
  derivation: time-axis negative (oldness, backward, terminal).
  notes: LAST is dual: "final in sequence" = D- end-state; "previous" = D-.
    FINAL = terminal D-.

### [0,+1,+1,0]  B+ × C+  (EARN coord — substance + signal both up)
WORDS: RICH-implied, GOLD, GOLDEN, PREMIUM-implied
  derivation: high substance + high signal-value.
  notes: GOLD as adjective (color or value) sits at the EARN coord.

### [0,-1,-1,0]  B- × C-  (WASTE coord)
WORDS: CHEAP (negative-value sense), POOR
  derivation: low substance + low value.

### [0,0,+1,+1]  C+ × D+  (LIVE coord)
WORDS: ALIVE, LIVING, ACTIVE, AWAKE-implied
  derivation: positive signal + forward time = the LIVE coord.
  notes: ACTIVE = the present-tense form of LIVE-axis.

### [0,0,-1,-1]  C- × D-  (DIE coord)
WORDS: DEAD, DYING-implied
  derivation: negative signal + halted time = DIE coord.

### Origin [0,0,0,0]  (container / observer-internal qualities)
WORDS: PERSONAL, PRIVATE, INDIVIDUAL, SELF, OWN, INTERNAL-implied, MENTAL-implied,
       SOCIAL (in self-other sense), HUMAN, ALONE-implied
  derivation: properties of the container/self. No axis movement; they
    describe the observer rather than world state.
  notes: PERSONAL/PRIVATE/OWN all reference the container.
    HUMAN = the conscious-container species property.

### SIGNAL-axis (color / sensory qualia)  C±, sub-shell
WORDS: RED, BLUE, GREEN, BLACK, WHITE, YELLOW, GOLD (color sense)
  derivation: pure perceptual qualia. They sit on the SIGNAL axis as
    visual-channel projections, not on a single ± trit. Mapped to a
    color sub-shell of C: each color is a distinct C-axis "phase."
  notes: Colors are the clearest case where the lattice needs a sub-axis
    or qualia sub-coordinate. Treated as origin-rooted SIGNAL projections.
    BLACK = C-, WHITE = C+ on the lightness sub-axis.

### Temperature / sensory  C-axis flavor
WORDS: HOT, COLD-implied, WARM-implied, COOL-implied
  derivation: temperature qualia. SIGNAL axis (sensory channel).
  notes: HOT can also be shell-2 intensifier in slang ("hot deal").

### Speed adjectives  D-axis
WORDS: FAST, QUICK, SLOW-implied, RAPID-implied
  derivation: temporal magnitude (D-axis intensity).
  notes: FAST = shell-2 D+; SLOW = shell-2 D-.

### Quantity / scope adjectives
WORDS: WHOLE, COMPLETE, TOTAL, FULL, EVERY, ALL  (universals — see determiners)
       PARTIAL-implied, SOME (see determiners)
  derivation: scope quantifiers; universal forms collapse to the
    "whole-lattice" reading [+,+,+,+]-corner-implied.

### MISC adjectives mapped by judgment
  SAME       — origin (identity, no axis change)
  DIFFERENT  — antipodal/contrast operator (no fixed coord; relational)
  BASIC      — origin (foundational, no movement)
  SIMPLE     — origin (low-magnitude across axes)
  GENERAL    — origin (universal scope)
  COMMON     — origin (shared, low individuation)
  SPECIFIC   — antipode of GENERAL (corner-leaning)
  STANDARD   — origin (default)
  ORIGINAL   — D- (initial / first in time)
  FIRST      — D- shell-2 (very early in time) — also ordinal
  SECOND/THIRD/etc — ordinals; D-axis indices
  MAIN       — center-projection, origin
  CENTRAL    — origin (literally the center)
  LOCAL      — origin (proximal POSITION)
  GLOBAL     — antipode of LOCAL ([+,+,+,+] reach)
  NATIONAL   — corner-leaning
  INTERNATIONAL — shell-2 of NATIONAL
  PUBLIC     — antipode of PRIVATE (out of container)
  PRIVATE    — origin / inside container
  LEGAL      — C+ (sanctioned signal)
  ILLEGAL-implied — C-
  TECHNICAL  — origin (descriptive)
  DIGITAL/ANALOG — axis-flavor on signal channel
  ADULT      — D- shell-2 of YOUNG
  ANNUAL     — D-axis cyclic (period)
  DAILY      — D-axis cyclic (period)
  ACTIVE     — LIVE coord [0,0,+1,+1]
  AVAILABLE  — C+ (open / present-positive)
  REQUIRED   — bond layer (modal MUST applied to adjective)
  POSSIBLE   — bond layer (modal CAN applied)
  IMPOSSIBLE-implied — bond layer negation

## ADVERBS

Adverbs are operators on coordinates, not coordinates themselves. Many
push their target to shell 2 (intensifiers) or modify TIME / SIGNAL.

### Intensifiers (shell-2 multipliers)
  VERY        — pushes target adjective to shell 2 on its native axis
  REALLY      — same as VERY (doubles to shell 2)
  TOO         — excess; pushes past shell 2 (overshoot)
  MUCH        — magnitude on substance B
  SO          — intensifier (and also discourse connector)
  QUITE-implied — partial intensifier
  ENOUGH      — sufficiency operator (threshold)
  MORE        — comparative B+ operator
  LESS        — comparative B- operator
  MOST        — superlative shell-3 / corner reach on B+
  LEAST       — superlative shell-3 on B-
  BETTER      — comparative C+
  BEST        — superlative C+
  BAD/WORSE/WORST — comparative/superlative C-
  HIGHER      — comparative A+
  HIGH-implied as adverb — A+
  FAR         — shell-2 on A axis
  NEAR        — shell-1 proximal on A
  CLOSE       — proximal A
  HARD (adv)  — intensifier (push to shell 2)

### Time adverbs (D-axis references)
  NOW         — temporal origin (D=0, present moment from container)
  THEN        — antipode/displaced temporal reference
  TODAY       — origin-shell on D
  YESTERDAY-implied — D-1
  TOMORROW-implied — D+1
  ALWAYS      — D+ persistent (forever)
  NEVER       — D- absent (never any time)
  OFTEN       — D+ frequency (multi-tick on D)
  USUALLY     — D+ frequency (default-tick)
  STILL       — D continuation (time keeps the prior state)
  YET         — D anticipation (time hasn't completed)
  SOON-implied — D+ small magnitude
  ALREADY     — D- (event preceded reference)
  ONCE        — D-axis singleton (one tick)
  AGAIN       — D-axis repeat operator
  EVER        — D-axis universal quantifier ("at any time")
  EVERYTHING (as quantifier) — universal across all axes (corner reach)

### Spatial adverbs (A-axis references)
  HERE        — origin-projection on A (proximal to container)
  THERE       — antipode of HERE (distal A)
  AWAY        — A- displacement (away from container)
  ABOVE       — A+
  BELOW       — A-
  AROUND      — A axis variable / circumferential (no fixed sign)
  TOGETHER    — origin-collapsing (A→0, multiple sources merge)
  ALONG       — A axis parallel transit
  OUT         — A- (out of container)
  IN/INSIDE   — A+ container interior
  BACK        — A-
  AHEAD-implied — A+

### Magnitude / sufficiency
  ONLY        — restriction operator (filter, not a coordinate)
  JUST        — restriction / minimization operator
  EVEN        — extension operator ("also, contrary to expectation")
  ALSO        — addition operator (set union on referents)
  TOO (also)  — addition operator (= ALSO sense)

### Manner / quality (mostly inherit from their adjective)
  WELL        — C+ (manner version of GOOD)
  POORLY-implied — C-
  QUICKLY-implied — D+ shell-2
  SLOWLY-implied  — D- shell-2

### Negation / polarity
  NOT         — polarity NOT operator (trit-gate layer; flips ±½ spin)
  NO-as-adv   — same NOT operator
  N'T         — bound polarity NOT

### Discourse / logic operators
  HOWEVER     — adversative connector (boolean operator family)
  THOUGH      — concessive
  THEN        — sequence operator (D forward)
  ELSE        — alternative operator (OR family)
  YES         — affirmative polarity (+½)
  PLEASE      — politeness operator (origin-relative)
  PERHAPS-implied / MAYBE-implied — modal probability operator (bond layer)

### Quantifier adverbs
  ALL         — universal across axes (corner [+,+,+,+] reach)
  EACH        — distributive over set
  EVERY       — universal (same as ALL but distributive)
  SOME        — partial existential
  ANY         — free-choice partial
  NONE        — universal negation
  BOTH        — pair-quantifier (2 of set)
  EITHER      — disjunctive selector (OR family)
  NEITHER-implied — disjunctive negation

## Layer assignments summary

  Shell 1 (basic 81):     RED, BLUE, GREEN, BIG, SMALL, GOOD, BAD, NEW, OLD,
                          HIGH, LOW, HOT, FREE, FULL, FAST, FAR, NEAR, ...
  Shell 2+ (intensified): GREAT, BEST, BETTER, MOST, LEAST, FASTEST, HUGE,
                          plus VERY/REALLY/TOO when applied
  Origin (container):     PERSONAL, PRIVATE, OWN, SELF, HUMAN, BASIC,
                          SIMPLE, MAIN, CENTRAL, SAME, COMMON, GENERAL,
                          STANDARD
  Bond layer (modal-tinted): REQUIRED, POSSIBLE, AVAILABLE — these are
                          adjectives derived from corner modal operators
  Trit-gate layer:        NOT, NO, N'T (the polarity NOT operator itself,
                          pre-coordinate)
  Origin-projection:      HERE, NOW, TODAY, THIS-as-adv, SAME — proximal
                          reference adverbs collapsing to container

## Discovered rules

1. **Adjective polarity = its native axis sign.** GOOD/BAD live on C±,
   BIG/SMALL on B±, NEW/OLD on D±, HIGH/LOW on A±. Adjective antonym
   pairs are antipodal sign-flips on a single axis.

2. **Comparatives push to shell 2; superlatives to shell 3.**
   GOOD [0,0,+1,0] → BETTER [0,0,+2,0] → BEST [0,0,+3,0]. Same for
   BIG/BIGGER/BIGGEST, FAST/FASTER/FASTEST. Morphology = shell jump.

3. **Intensifier adverbs (VERY, REALLY, TOO, EXTREMELY) are
   shell-multiplier operators**, not coordinates. They take a shell-1
   adjective and lift it to shell 2 on the same axis.

4. **Proximal adverbs collapse to origin.** HERE, NOW, TODAY, THIS-here
   all reference the container's own position/time. They're not at a
   coord — they ARE the origin from which coords are seen.

5. **Distal adverbs antipode the proximals.** THERE/THEN/THOSE/AWAY
   point AWAY from origin. Not specific coords either; they're
   "origin-relative displacement" markers.

6. **Quantifier adverbs map to corner reach.** ALL/EVERY = corner
   [+,+,+,+] (universal across all 4 axes); NONE/NO = its antipode.
   SOME/ANY = partial / not-yet-corner.

7. **Color adjectives need a SIGNAL sub-axis.** RED/BLUE/GREEN don't
   sort onto a single ± trit; they're distinct phases of the SIGNAL
   axis (visual-channel qualia). Currently mapped as C-axis sub-shell.

8. **Stative adjectives that name internal states (PERSONAL, PRIVATE,
   HUMAN, ALIVE-as-state) tend to collapse to origin** for the same
   reason container verbs do — they describe the observer, not the
   world.

9. **Frequency adverbs (ALWAYS, OFTEN, USUALLY, NEVER) are D-axis
   density operators**, not single trit values. They tile the D axis
   with positive density (ALWAYS = full tile, NEVER = empty tile).

10. **Manner adverbs inherit their root adjective's coord** (WELL ≈
    GOOD, BADLY ≈ BAD, QUICKLY ≈ FAST). The -LY suffix is a
    grammatical coat, not a lattice transformation.
