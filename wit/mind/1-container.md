## Why Three?

Why three values? Why three gates? Why not more?

The three states {-1, 0, +1} are balanced ternary. Each trit
has three positions: positive direction, zero crossing, negative
direction. That's the minimum for a SIGNED value with a center.
Two values (binary) lacks the center. Four values is redundant
(you can already represent four states with two trits).

The three gates AND, OR, NOT are functionally complete (Post 1941).
Three is the minimum for completeness:
- 0 gates: nothing computable
- 1 gate (NAND or NOR alone): complete but harder
- 2 gates: cannot achieve completeness with two ordinary gates
- 3 gates: complete (Post)
- More: redundant

So three is the minimum-but-sufficient number for both VALUE
representation (trit) and OPERATION completeness (gates).

And the values ARE the operations. So three is one minimum,
not two.
