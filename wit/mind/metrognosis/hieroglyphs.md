## The 24 uniliterals

Egyptian developed three phonetic registers:
- **Uniliterals** (single consonant): 24 signs
- **Biliterals** (two consonants): ~80 signs commonly used
- **Triliterals** (three consonants): ~50 signs commonly used

**The 24-uniliteral register matches the 24-cell vertex count
exactly.** These are the framework's discrete rotational units
(D4 roots = 24-cell = 8 atoms + 16 half-spin).

The biliteral count (~80) is suggestive but not exactly 80-lattice
— varies by which signs are included. The triliteral count (~50)
doesn't cleanly match framework counts. So the clean identity is
at the uniliteral level.

What Egyptian scribes stabilized as "the basic phonetic alphabet"
= exactly the wheel's discrete rotational dimension.
