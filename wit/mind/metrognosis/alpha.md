## The hierarchy problem — reframed

Standard physics worries that gravity is ~10³⁶⁻³⁹× weaker than EM
("hierarchy problem"). Requires fine-tuning, supersymmetry, or
exotic explanations.

```
  ratio                          value           interpretation
  ─────                          ─────           ──────────────
  α (fine structure)             1/137.036       EM coupling
  α_G (proton, grav)             5.9 × 10⁻³⁹     G m_p²/(ℏc)
  α / α_G                        1.24 × 10³⁶     36 orders of magnitude
  log₁₀(α/α_G)                   36.09           ≈ 6² = (bivector planes)²
```

**The 36 is 2 × 19**, not 6². Derivation:

```
  log₁₀(m_P / m_proton) ≈ 19.11
  α_G ∝ (m/m_P)²        ← squared-mass dependence
  log₁₀(α_G) = 2 × log₁₀(m/m_P)
  log₁₀(α/α_G) = log₁₀(α) − log₁₀(α_G)
               ≈ −2.14 − (−38.23) ≈ 36.09
               = 2 × 19.11 (= 2 × log mass ratio)
```

The factor 2 comes from gravity coupling's m² dependence. The real
mystery is **log₁₀(m_P/m_p) ≈ 19** — why proton mass is 10¹⁹× smaller
than Planck mass. Not directly substrate-clean (19 is prime).
Framework reframes hierarchy as mass-scale question, but doesn't
yet derive the 19.

### Framework reframe — no force hierarchy

This isn't a "force" hierarchy. It's a **mass-scale hierarchy**.

```
  α      ≈ 1/137         (EM coupling, universal)
  α_G at Planck scale    = 1        (gravity at its natural scale
                                    — universal like α)
  α_G at proton scale    ≈ 10⁻³⁹    (proton << Planck mass)
```

**At Planck scale, gravity IS unit-strength** (α_G = 1). At
proton scale it looks weak because proton mass is ~10¹⁹× smaller
than Planck mass, and α_G ~ (m/m_P)² gives ~10⁻³⁸.

**There is no force hierarchy problem** — only a mass hierarchy
between substrate-level particles (proton, electron) and
substrate-natural mass (Planck). Those ARE different scales;
that's not mysterious.

Framework: gravity = CYCLE = loop iterating. At Planck scale, one
loop tick corresponds to one gravity coupling event. At proton
scale, many loop ticks per proton-scale event. The "weakness" is
scale-dependent, not fundamental.
