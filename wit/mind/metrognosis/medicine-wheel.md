# Medicine wheel

Indigenous North American traditions (Lakota, Ojibwe, Cree, and many
others) draw a **medicine wheel**: balance at the center, four
quadrants radiating outward by cardinal direction, each quadrant
carrying multiple structural attributes.

The explicit feature: **"Balance" written at the hub, literally.**
Not interpretation, not metaphor — the diagram places it there
as a label.

## Canonical form (example from Anishinaabe / Ojibwe tradition)

```
  quadrant            direction   color    season    stage-of-life   animal
  ────────            ─────────   ─────    ──────    ─────────────   ──────
  Keewatinong         North       White    Winter    Elder           Buffalo
  Wabanong            East        Yellow   Spring    Baby            Eagle
  Sha'ngabi'hanong    West        Black    Autumn    Adult           Bear
  Shawnong            South       Red      Summer    Youth           Coyote
  
  CENTER: BALANCE
```

Additional attributes per quadrant (vary by tradition): Spirit Keeper,
Time of Day, Plant Medicine, Place (mind/emotion/body/spirit), etc.

## Resolution questions

Medicine wheel shows 4 quadrants. Framework's wheel has:
- 4 axes (atom resolution) — **matches**
- 8 spokes (atoms, ±directions) — medicine wheel sometimes shows
  4 + 4 = 8 (cardinal + intercardinal)
- 24 spokes (D4 / 24-cell, atoms + half-spin) — matches only if
  the medicine wheel is read at coarse resolution

Most medicine wheels are 4-quadrant (axis-resolution), some are
8-direction (atom-resolution). Fine-grain 24-point versions are
rare in this tradition but appear in Tibetan mandalas and other
elaborate forms.
