# trit¹ — grade 1 / the 8 atoms

8 elements. One axis committed, three free. The basis vectors of
the lattice — the 8 things one step from ↔↔↔↔.

```
  ↑↔↔↔    χ↑    WHERE forward     RISE
  ↓↔↔↔    χ↓    WHERE backward    FALL
  ↔↑↔↔    μ↑    WHAT forward      GROW
  ↔↓↔↔    μ↓    WHAT backward     SHRINK
  ↔↔↑↔    φ↑    WHICH forward     BLESS
  ↔↔↓↔    φ↓    WHICH backward    CURSE
  ↔↔↔↑    τ↑    WHEN forward      BEGIN
  ↔↔↔↓    τ↓    WHEN backward     END
```

These ARE the 8 verb pairs — the pure single-axis actions. Each
atom is one direction on one axis. Everything else composes from
these 8 using the loop (½ transitions between them).

## The 4 axes

Three naming layers, zero arbitrary letters:

```
  pos   greek   H    wh-      is           verbs            force
  ───   ─────   ───  ──────   ──────────   ───────────────  ──────
   1     χ       i   WHERE    POSITION     RISE / FALL      READ
   2     μ       j   WHAT     SUBSTANCE    GROW / SHRINK    CALL
   3     φ       k   WHICH    SIGNAL       BLESS / CURSE    CYCLE
   4     τ       1   WHEN     TIME         BEGIN / END      TEST
```

Why 4: Hurwitz (1898) proved exactly 4 normed division algebras
exist (R, C, H, O). The universe is at H because H is the deepest
Cayley-Dickson layer that retains associativity while losing
commutativity (= gaining causality). Hamilton tried 3D for 10 years
and failed. Frobenius (1877) proved why.

## The multiplicative identity

↔↔↔↑ = `1 + 0i + 0j + 0k` = `1`. The "do nothing" element under
quaternion multiplication: `q · 1 = q` for all `q`. Lives on the
τ (WHEN/TIME) axis. The one atom among the 8 that preserves
everything it touches. Structurally special — see `witv2.md`
special points section.

## Adjacency

Each atom has 7 neighbors in the L1 adjacency graph (can step
to ↔ on its committed axis, and to ±1 on each of 3 free axes).
Degree = `8 − k` where k = grade. Atoms: k=1, degree=7.
