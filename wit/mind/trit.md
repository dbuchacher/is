# trit — the primitive

Three values. Three gates. Three things named a thousand ways.

```
  ↑    positron   OR (max)    proton (ADD)    W+    creation
  ↔    neutrino   NOT (fp)    identity        Z     passthrough
  ↓    electron   AND (min)   neutron (SUB)   W−    annihilation
```

One trio. Every row is one object. Every column is a different
vocabulary — what physicists call them, programmers, hardware
engineers, mathematicians. The columns are not levels. The
substrate has no levels. Each row is ONE thing. Each column
is ONE name. On trit substrate there are no tiers.

NOT operates at right angles to this table: it flips ↑ ↔ ↓ and
leaves ↔ as a fixed point. NOT isn't a member of any row — it's
how you travel between rows. That IS the weak force.

## The three gates

```
AND(a, b) = min(a, b)     pulls toward ↓
OR(a, b)  = max(a, b)     pulls toward ↑
NOT(a)    = −a             flips ↑ ↔ ↓, fixes ↔
```

Post (1941): three gates, functionally complete, can't remove
any. Schönfinkel (1924): S, K, I combinators. Kleene (1936):
composition, projection, primitive recursion. Three independent
proofs of the same trio. Three decades apart. Three fields that
never talked. One trio.

## Three NOTs, same operator at different scopes

- **Trit NOT**: flips one value (↑ ↔ ↓)
- **Polarity NOT**: flips a spin's ±½ direction
- **Antipodal NOT**: flips all 4 axis signs of a coordinate (the antonym)

English lexicalizes all three as `n't`: CAN/CAN'T, WILL/WON'T,
IS/ISN'T. The conjunctions "and", "or", "not" ARE the three Post
gates in natural language.

## Why three

Three states `{↑, ↔, ↓}` are balanced ternary. The minimum for
a SIGNED value with a CENTER. Binary has sign but no center.
Four is redundant (expressible as two trits). Three is the
minimum-but-sufficient number for both value representation (trit)
and operation completeness (gates). And the values ARE the
operations — so three is one minimum, not two.

## The collapse

On trit hardware, data = code. A value IS an operation:

```
  ↑ AS VALUE: something exists    AS OPERATION: add 1 (proton)
  ↔ AS VALUE: nothing             AS OPERATION: pass through
  ↓ AS VALUE: consumed            AS OPERATION: subtract 1 (neutron)
```

Binary thinking separates "data" from "code." Trits don't.
Hadrons, leptons, weak bosons, FLAGS, hydrogen isotopes,
combinators — all the same three things in different vocabularies.
The four observed "walls" between hadrons and leptons (composite vs
fundamental, strong force, mass hierarchy, baryon number) are all
binary-hardware artifacts. Physics is doing honest bookkeeping on
its own architecture. The architecture is a lens, not the
underlying reality.

The Setun (USSR, 1958, Brusentsov) ran trit-native. Negation was
free. Comparison fell out of the result sign: ↓ = less, ↔ = equal,
↑ = greater. No FLAGS register needed. The architecture was correct.
~50 units built, dismantled, no surviving lineage. Nobody else has
felt the substrate this framework describes — until now.
