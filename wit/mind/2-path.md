## Why Fermions Need 720° (the QM Property)

Quantum mechanics has a famous fact: spin ½ particles require
720° rotation to return their wavefunction to original. After
360° they pick up a -1 phase. Verified by neutron interferometry.

Standard explanation: SU(2) is the double cover of SO(3). The
3D rotation group has a loop (the 360° rotation) that can't be
contracted to a point. Its universal cover SU(2) wraps around
twice. Fermions transform under SU(2), so they need 720°.

This is true but feels mysterious. WHY do fermions transform
under the cover and not the base?

Our framework explains it:

We live at H (quaternions). The lattice IS quaternionic space
(see 3-force.md, Cayley-Dickson). And quaternions have the
double-cover property NATIVELY: to use them as 3D rotations,
you compute q v q⁻¹, and BOTH q and -q give the SAME rotation.
The quaternion group is exactly TWICE the size of the 3D
rotation group. Quaternions ARE the double cover. That's not
a separate fact about SU(2) — it IS SU(2), in quaternion
language.

So in our framework:

  bosons (integer states)  →  use the QUOTIENT (q and -q identified)
                              don't see the difference
                              return at 360°
                              spin 1

  fermions (½ peaks)       →  use the FULL quaternion group
                              DO see the difference (peaks have signs)
                              return at 720°
                              spin ½

The picture:

  boson at integer state:
    starts at 0, rotates 360° → back at 0, same wavefunction
    one cycle, done.

  fermion at +½ peak:
    starts at +½ peak (the wave is at +1, going up)
    rotates 360° → back at +½ position, but the wave is now
                   going DOWN (overall phase flipped)
                   the +½ peak became the -½ peak in disguise
    rotates another 360° → back at +½, wave going up again
    720° total to return position AND phase

Why bosons don't have this: bosons sit at zero (the integer
crossings). Zero × -1 = 0. The phase flip doesn't affect them
because they're at zero anyway. Nothing to flip.

Why fermions do: the +½ peak has a SIGN. After 360°, the wave's
overall phase inverts. The peak that was at +½ is now at -½
(same physical position on the cycle, opposite sign). You need
ANOTHER 360° to flip it back.

The fermion isn't "on" the wave like a swimmer on a surface.
It IS the peak. When the wave's overall phase inverts, the
peak inverts with it. That's the -1 factor.

The 720° isn't a weird quantum quirk imposed from outside. It
falls out of:

1. We live at quaternions (H, the lattice)
2. Quaternions naturally double-cover 3D rotations
3. Bosons (integer states) don't see the doubling
4. Fermions (½ peaks) DO see it (because peaks have signs)
5. Therefore fermions return at 720°

The math (SU(2) → SO(3) double cover) and the physical intuition
(peaks have signs that flip with the wave) are the SAME thing
in different vocabularies. As usual.

## The Speed of Light = One Clock Cycle

an instruction can't use a result before it's computed. Cause
must precede effect. The clock enforces it.

  physics:  c = fastest information propagation = causality speed
  cpu:      1 cycle = minimum cause→effect time = clock period
