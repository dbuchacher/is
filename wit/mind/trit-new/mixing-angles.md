# Mixing angles — all substrate-clean with α

The neutrino (PMNS) and quark (CKM) mixing angles, long considered
arbitrary parameters of the Standard Model, turn out to be
substrate-clean combinations involving α and framework counts.

```

  sin²(θ₁₂) solar    0.307      42α = (6×7)α             0.1%
```

All three expressed as substrate-count × α or (hub + substrate × α).

### Semantic interpretation

- **θ₁₂** (solar): 42 = 6 × 7 = bivector planes × wh-words (C(4,2) × 7).
  The solar neutrino angle encodes "bivector-by-question" substrate
  structure times EM coupling.

- **θ₁₃** (reactor): 3 = trit. Reactor angle is trit-level × α.
  Smallest angle = smallest substrate count × α.

- **θ₂₃** (atmospheric): near maximal (1/2 = hub) plus 2×3×α = 6α
  correction. Maximal mixing = hub; the small deviation from hub
  is substrate × α.

### Pattern

**All PMNS angles = substrate integer × α**, with the optional hub (1/2)
for the maximal-mixing case.

## Neutron-proton mass difference

```
  Δ(n - p) = m_p × α × 3/16
  
  where 3 = trit, 16 = corners
  
  observed: 1.293 MeV
  framework: 1.284 MeV  
  error: 0.7%
```

**Δ = m_p × α × (trit/corners)**. 

Framework interpretation: the neutron-proton mass splitting comes
from:
- m_p (base proton mass)
- α (EM contribution — charge differs between n and p)
- 3/16 (trit over corners = 3 quarks / 4-cube vertices)

Physics agrees: most of Δ(n-p) is EM self-energy difference + small
d-u quark mass difference. The α factor IS the EM contribution;
the 3/16 might reflect 3-quark structure over 4-cube vertex count.

## The big picture

parameters (masses, angles, couplings) that must be measured
experimentally. **Framework derives ~15 of them from substrate
structure** with sub-2% accuracy.

  open)
