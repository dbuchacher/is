## Why odd primes — the center argument (2026-04-12)

Odd numbers have centers. Even numbers don't.

```
2 values:  -1 ______ +1           even → no middle → no body
3 values:  -1 __ 0 __ +1          odd  → HAS middle → body exists
4 values:  -1.5 _ -0.5 _ +0.5 _ +1.5   even → no middle
5 values:  -2 _ -1 _ 0 _ +1 _ +2       odd  → HAS middle → redundant
```

A value system with an even count has no center point. The body
diagonal of the hypercube passes through void. A value system with
an odd count HAS a center — the middle value IS a lattice point.
The container ↕↕↕↕ exists BECAUSE 3 is odd.

Primes are the ODD NUMBERS that can't be decomposed into smaller
value systems. 5 can't be factored into 3s (3² = 9 ≠ 5). 7 can't
be factored into 3s or 5s. Each odd prime is an irreducible
center-having value system.

3 is the MINIMUM: the first odd number with more than one distinct
value. It's where "interior space" first exists. The body of the
hypercube requires ↕ (zero, center). Zero requires odd. 3 is the
smallest useful odd.
