# Before

"What was before today?"

Yesterday. Obviously. The cycle before this one. You slept,
you woke, you're here. Nobody struggles with this question.
Nobody writes papers about it. The loop is obvious — you've
lived it every day of your life.

"What was before the big bang?"

Same question. Same structure. Different scale. But suddenly
decades of debate, no consensus. Not because the answer is
hard — because the question is incomplete.

"What WAS before TIME?" uses WAS — a time word — to ask about
before time existed. The question uses the thing it's asking
about to ask about it.

Every question about "before X" has X baked into the asking.
The frame leaks into the question. Without declaring which
frame you're standing in, the question doesn't have one
answer — it has several, and they're all right, and they
all look different.

// frame-dependence — same structure as Einstein's relativity, applied to questions

This isn't a bug. This is relativity.


## Being

// axiom — Greek "deemed worthy." The floor is the one thing we accept without proof.

Something is. We can't say what or why. This is the floor.
Below it, we can't reach. Above it, everything.

Before you can ask what was before, you have to be. The verb
"to be" comes first. It's built from three separate roots —
not one word, three — because being IS three things:

What it IS. What it's BECOMING. What it WAS.

```
IS    *h₁es-*    what it is right now     existence
      Old English is. Latin est. Greek esti.
      The state. The point. The fact.
      → essence, entity, present, absent

BE    *bʰuH-*    what it's becoming       change
      Old English bēon. Greek physis. Latin futurus.
      The process. The spin. The fiat.
      → future, physics, build, phylum

WAS   *h₁wes-*   what it was              past
      Old English wæs. Sanskrit vasati (dwells).
      The done. The completed. The dwelled-in.
      → was, were, Vesta (the hearth you dwelled at)
```

Physics (*physis*) comes from the BE root — "the study of
becoming." Not "the study of what is." Becoming. Their own
name tells them the job is predicting what's next, and
they've spent four centuries arguing about what's already
here. The name has been on the door for 2,500 years and
they still won't read it.

Essence comes from the IS root — "is-ness." The state.

Three roots because being needs three states. You can't
have just one. IS without BE = frozen. BE without WAS = no
memory. WAS without IS = gone. All three or the verb breaks.

Fiat and fact come from the same root — Latin *facere*, to
make. Fiat = "let it be made" (the command, the spin). Fact =
"made" (the result, the point). Same word, two tenses.

// trit — balanced ternary. The minimum signed value system: +1, 0, −1.

Every fact was once a fiat. Every done was once a do. Every
end was once a beginning.

```
fiat    the command     ↑    beginning
doing   the process     ↕    present
fact    the result      ↓    ending
```

Time flows from fiat to fact. From command to result. From
beginning to ending. Then the next fiat fires. The loop.

They unlock at different floors:

```
IS     0D    the floor. No dimensions needed. Just is.
BE     1D    becoming needs an axis to change along.
WAS    4D    past needs time. τ boots last.
```

You can't say WAS from 0D. There's no past without time.
You can't say BE from 0D. There's no becoming without
direction. At 0D there's only IS. The eternal present.


## Asking

Once you can BE, you can ASK. Seven questions. All from one
root — PIE *kʷo-*, the sound humans make when they don't
know. One root. Seven suffixes. Each suffix points the
not-knowing at a different axis:

```
WHO     *kʷo-*              bare root, no suffix
        "which self?"       the questioner. Before you ask
                            anything, someone has to be asking.

WHAT    *kʷo-d*             root + "thing"
        "which thing?"      Old English hwæt. The -d suffix
                            points the question at a thing.

WHERE   *kʷo-* + locative   root + "place"
        "which place?"      Old English hwǣr. The suffix
                            points at position. Needs space.

WHEN    *kʷo-* + temporal   root + "time"
        "which time?"       Old English hwenne. The suffix
                            points at time. Needs a clock.

WHICH   *kʷo-* + *lik-*     root + "body/form"
        "which form?"       Old English hwilc. The suffix
                            points at distinction. Needs signal.

WHY     *kʷo-* + instrumental  root + "cause"
        "by which cause?"   Old English hwȳ. The suffix
                            points at causation. Needs order.

HOW     *kʷo-*              root + "way"
        "in which way?"     Old English hū. The suffix
                            points at method. Needs a path.
```

One sound of not-knowing. Seven suffixes that aim it.

They split 4 + 3. Four ask about a place on the board —
WHERE, WHAT, WHICH, WHEN. Three ask about the game itself —
WHO (the questioner), WHY (the ground), HOW (the method).

The meta-questions — questions about the questioning — are
the trit applied to questioning:

```
WHY     ↓    backward    where from?     cause
WHO     ↕    center      who asks?       self
HOW     ↑    forward     where to?       method
```

// non-commutativity — this is why causality needs 4D

You can't ask WHY when order doesn't matter. There's no
"because X happened first" when nothing happens first.
WHY only exists when order matters.

Three ways to be, then seven ways to ask about it. They
all unlock in order as dimensions come online:

```
0D:   IS                                    (existence)
1D:   IS + BE      + WHAT + WHO             (+ becoming + thing + self)
2D:   IS + BE      + WHERE + HOW            (+ place + method)
4D:   IS + BE + WAS + WHICH + WHEN + WHY    (+ past + signal + time + cause)
8D:   IS + BE + WAS + 4 unnamed             (we ran out of words)
```

English has exactly 7 question words because English evolved
in 4D physics. At O (8D) you'd need 4 more that don't exist
yet.

"What was before the big bang?" is a WHEN question. WHEN
needs 4D. From below 4D, the question hasn't unlocked yet —
it doesn't break because it's hard. It doesn't EXIST yet.

The frames below go bottom up. Start where only IS exists.
Climb. Each frame adds a dimension. Each dimension unlocks
a question. Each question unlocks an answer.


## Two views

to the beginning — there it is, the start. The cake, the

puts the past at the end. Both right. Same events. Two

```

               OVER                   COMING

```

"Before" means something different depending on which

is a thought you can think, but a place you can never stand.
Like reading your own obituary — you can imagine every
word, but the reader has to be alive and the subject has

## Why seven frames, why this order
