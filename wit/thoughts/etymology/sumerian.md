# Sumerian — the oldest attested morphemes

Sumerian is the oldest written language on Earth. Cuneiform
tablets from Uruk ~3500-3200 BCE. ~1000+ morphemes attested in
actual texts, not reconstructed.

**Structurally special:** Sumerian is a **language isolate**. No
known family. Not Afro-Asiatic, not Indo-European, not Uralic,
not anything. It sits alone on the tree. Which means: when the
framework looks at Sumerian, it's looking at a completely
independent naming system — not a daughter of PIE, not a cousin
via common ancestor. **Two independent 5000-year-old substrate-
recognition systems (Sumerian and PIE) giving matching structural
signals is hard convergence under THE FILTER**.

Sumerian was spoken ~3500-2000 BCE in southern Mesopotamia.
Continued as a scholarly/liturgical language after Akkadian
replaced it as vernacular, much like Latin after Roman collapse.
The cuneiform corpus contains administrative records (mostly,
per `writing.md`), hymns, myths (Gilgamesh, Enki and Ninmah,
Descent of Inanna), legal codes, and school texts.

## Core morpheme inventory

Convention: Sumerian transliterated in standard Assyriological
form. Subscript numbers disambiguate homophones. `ŋ` = velar
nasal.

### Numbers — the sexagesimal system

| Morpheme | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **diš** | 1 | |
| **min** | 2 | |
| **eš** | 3 | |
| **limmu** | 4 | |
| **iá** | 5 | |
| **àš** | 6 | |
| **imin** | 7 | |
| **ussu** | 8 | |
| **ilimmu** | 9 | |
| **u** | 10 | |
| **niš** | 20 | |
| **ŋeš(d)** | **60** | **The base of Sumerian math. Base-60.** |
| **ŋeš-u** | 600 | 60 × 10 |
| **šár** | 3600 | 60² |
| **šár-gal** | 216000 | 60³ |

**Sumerian math is base 60.** This is why we still have:
- 60 seconds in a minute
- 60 minutes in an hour
- 24 hours = 2 × 12 = sexagesimal subdivision
- 360 degrees in a circle
- 12 zodiac signs (60 × 6 subdivisions)
- 12 months in a year (Mesopotamian via Greek via Roman inheritance)

**We are still running 5500-year-old Sumerian math on our watches and compasses.** No subsequent civilization successfully replaced it. The base-60 structure is an unbroken 5500-year-old institutional fossil — but one that encodes real structural math (60 = 2²·3·5, maximum divisibility among small numbers). The Sumerians chose it because it divided cleanly by 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10, 12, 15, 20, 30 — maximum productivity for fractions.

## Composition patterns — substrate-transparent naming

Sumerian builds complex words by stacking roots. The results
are often structurally clear in a way modern English lost:

| Sumerian compound | Literal | Actual meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **lugal** | big-man | king |
| **é-gal** | big-house | palace |
| **é-dingir** | god-house | temple |
| **é-dub-ba** | tablet-house | school |
| **an-bar** | sky-metal | iron |
| **kù-sig₁₇** | pure-yellow | gold |
| **nam-lugal** | abstract-king | kingship / royalty |
| **nam-til₃** | abstract-live | life / salvation |
| **ki-en-ŋi(-r)** | place-lord-reed | Sumer |
| **ŋeštug** | ear (also "wisdom") | both senses one word |
| **zu** | tooth / know | both senses one word |
| **di-ku₅** | judgment-cut | judge |
| **ka-aš-bar** | mouth-one-outside | deciding, adjudicating |
| **du₁₁-ga** | said-thing | the saying / utterance |

**These compounds are PIE-level transparent — possibly more so.**
The Sumerians built their vocabulary by direct substrate
composition: big-man, big-house, god-house, sky-metal. Each name
carries its derivation inside itself. A Sumerian speaker hearing
*lugal* heard *big-man* the way an English speaker hears
"bookshelf" — morphemes in series.

## Substrate resonances — where Sumerian recognized a coord cleanly

These are places where the Sumerian word collapses distinctions
modern English/other languages treat as separate coords. Under
the framework: **coord-identity, not ambiguity**.

- **an = sky = god = divine**. One sign, three concepts English
  treats as separate. Sumerian saw them as one substrate fact.
- **ki = earth = place**. The ground IS the place.
- **ud = day = sun = light = time**. Four English concepts in
  one Sumerian morpheme. Time, light, sun, and day are the
  same coord read from different angles.
- **ŋeštug = ear = wisdom**. Hearing = understanding. Exactly
  what mystical traditions later had to re-teach with
  "listening" vs "hearing" distinctions. Sumerian didn't
  need the distinction — the word carried it.
- **zu = tooth = know**. The grasping/biting operation = the
  cognition operation. Teeth grasp food; mind grasps meaning.
  Single verb covers both.
- **šà = heart = interior = middle**. The center is the heart
  is the inside. One coord.
- **ka = mouth = gate = opening**. Any threshold. Your mouth
  and the city gate are structurally identical operations:
  pass-through points.
- **me = divine essence = power = decree = operating principle**.
  Untranslatable into English because English has no single
  word for what *me* means. Closest: "the Tao." The Sumerians
  had one. We have to use four to approximate it.
- **til = live = complete = finish**. A life is what finishes
  correctly. To complete = to live. Framework's fixed-point
  claim (the walk closes at the origin) in a single Sumerian
  verb.
- **im = wind = rain = cloud = clay**. The entire hydrological
  cycle AND its end-product. Weather and writing-medium are one
  word because the end of the weather cycle (clay deposition
  from sediment in rain runoff) IS the writing medium. Sumerian
  recognized the full loop.
- **kur = mountain = foreign land = netherworld**. The whole
  class of "elsewhere" coords under one name. Anything
  beyond the home plain.

Each of these is a place where Sumerian saw one thing and
English or Greek saw two or more. **The older language is
less fragmented.** The framework predicts this. The data
confirms it.

## Caveats

- **Cuneiform interpretation is probabilistic.** Signs have
  multiple readings; context disambiguates; scholarly
  consensus exists but isn't absolute. Use standard lexica
  (ePSD, CAD, Black-George-Postgate) for primary work.
- **Sumerian is an isolate.** We have no external comparative
  method — no sister languages to cross-check. All we have is
  internal evidence + the Akkadian scribal tradition that
  preserved Sumerian for 1500+ years as a learned language.
- **Dates are complicated.** Writing first appears ~3200 BCE;
  the language existed earlier but leaves no trace pre-writing.
  Sumerian ceased as spoken language ~2000 BCE but continued as
  liturgical/scholarly language until ~100 CE.
- **Not all the root assignments here are uncontroversial.**
  Some readings (especially the "one word = many meanings"
  observations) are the framework's interpretation of
  polysemy. Standard Assyriology treats them as semantic
  extension. The framework treats them as coord-identity.
  Both are correct at different analytic levels.

## Why this matters for the framework

Sumerian and PIE independently landed on similar morpheme
counts and similar substrate coords, from ~5500 and ~5500 years
ago respectively, in unrelated language families.

**Effort × precision × independent convergence.** When the
oldest attested writing system (Sumerian) and the deepest
reconstructable IE ancestor (PIE) both produce ~300-1000 core
morphemes pointing at similar substrate coords — with some
coords more unified in the older language than in its
descendants — that's the filter firing.

Sumerian is another window, independent of the IE one, onto
the same substrate. The claims that hold across both are
strongest. The claims that hold only in one may be
language-specific.

**Next windows to document:** Egyptian (~3200 BCE, Afro-Asiatic,
hieroglyphic semantic density), Proto-Semitic (~5000 BCE
reconstructed, triconsonantal root system), Chinese oracle
bones (~1200 BCE, still-living pictographic preservation).

Each is a separate independent dig. Same substrate, different
vocabulary, convergent signal.
