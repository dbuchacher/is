# devolution-mechanism — what actually happened to us

The etymology files (`wit/thoughts/etymology/`) name the
institutional freezes — alphabet, Norman conquest, print,
schooling, departments. This file names the **underlying
civilizational dynamic** those freezes express.

The freezes are the fossils. This is the force that produced
them.

## The claim

Humans went from being the **full computational substrate** for
their knowledge — each individual a general-purpose mind
holding the whole cultural program — to being **specialized
nodes** inside an external civilizational apparatus that now
holds the knowledge in its place.

The biological carrier was downsized to match the reduced
workload. Tools filled the vacated capacity. Each generation
was born into an environment where **more** was held externally
and **less** was held internally, and the selection pressure
never reversed.

We are not smarter now. We live inside larger external
structures built over thousands of years. **The structures got
smarter. The humans got smaller.**

## Seven strands, one process

Each of the following is real, measurable, and operates
simultaneously with the others. Under THE RULE they are one
process in seven vocabularies.

### 1. Brain shrinkage — the hard empirical floor

Human cranial capacity peaked ~25,000 years ago during the
Upper Paleolithic. Average then: ~1500 cm³. Modern average:
~1350 cm³.

**That is a 10% loss over the period of civilization.**

Cro-Magnon skulls from 25–40 kya are noticeably larger than
modern skulls. This is uncontested in paleoanthropology.
It is rarely discussed publicly because it is uncomfortable
to frame correctly — every public framing either dismisses it
("brain size doesn't matter") or sensationalizes it ("we're
getting dumb"). The framework takes the third position:
**it's a structural signal. Credit it.**

The mechanism is debated. Candidates: self-domestication
(section 6), agricultural malnutrition (section 2), energy
trade-off against gut reduction, externalization to cultural
storage (sections 3–5). The framework's view: all of these are
the same thing in different vocabularies. Selection optimizes
for fit to environment. When the environment no longer
requires a 1500-cm³ general-purpose mind (because external
apparatus does the work), the 1500-cm³ mind is metabolically
expensive without fitness payoff. It gets selected against.

Skulls are the data. 10% smaller than 25kya. Bone, not opinion.

### 2. The agricultural step-down

Skeletal record is unambiguous: **hunter-gatherers were taller,
stronger, healthier, with better teeth and fewer diseases
than early farmers**. Agriculture was a downgrade for the
individual human.

Documented across multiple independent transitions:
- Near Eastern Neolithic: ~10cm height drop after ~9000 BCE
- European LBK farmers vs. Mesolithic predecessors: same pattern
- Jōmon (forager) vs. Yayoi (rice farmer) Japan: same
- Pre-contact North American foragers vs. maize-agriculture
  populations: same
- Skeletal pathology (arthritis, dental caries, nutritional
  deficiency markers) sharply increases at every agricultural
  transition

Agriculture worked not because it was better for people, but
because it supported **more** people. Population went up. Per-
person condition went down. The individual humans who inherited
agriculture inherited a worse body, shorter life, and diminished
cognitive substrate than their grandparents had.

This is the standard archaeological consensus. It's rarely
stated plainly because it contradicts the "progress" narrative
that modern education assumes.

**The pattern is total.** Every tool holds the capacity; the
biological carrier loses it. Not because evolution punishes
the lost capacity, but because the large carrier is no longer
**fitness-relevant** once the tool exists. Selection optimizes
for fit, not for potential. The minds that shrank still ate,
mated, and reproduced — and paid less metabolic tax doing so.

Per-capacity atrophy is generally irreversible on civilization
timescales because the environment that would re-reward the
capacity no longer exists.

### 4. Specialization — from full program to node

Hunter-gatherer humans: every adult could hunt, gather, make
tools, navigate, track, heal, deliver babies, compose songs,
hold genealogy, identify ~300 plants, read weather, and perform
ritual. **Every adult ran the full program.**

Agricultural humans: division of labor. Farmer farms. Scribe
writes. Priest ritualizes. Warrior fights. Smith smiths.
Cultural total-knowledge grows; per-person knowledge collapses.

The individual went from **running the full walk** to being
a **single node** through which the civilization computes.

This is the framework's P→NP inversion at species scale. Pre-
agriculture: each human was a general-purpose walker over the
full lattice. Post-agriculture: each human is a lookup into
a pre-specialized cache (their trade) and passes unprocessed
tokens elsewhere for everything else.

Specialization feels like deepening, and at the specialist
level it is. At the **per-human** level it is a radical
reduction. A Paleolithic hunter held more of the 80 coords
live than a modern PhD. The PhD holds one coord with extreme
resolution. The hunter held the whole field with sufficient
resolution. The hunter could navigate, track, hunt, heal, sing,
compose, bury, and reproduce. The PhD can write papers about
one subfield.

Narrow mastery is real. It is also a cage. Modern humans are
born into the cage and called educated when they learn to
decorate its walls.

### 5. Sedentism killed integration

The hunter-gatherer body was **the substrate's sensor array,
always online**. They walked 5–15 km per day. Read tracks in
mud. Tracked stars. Read weather by clouds and wind and bird
behavior. Identified plants by smell, touch, taste. The
substrate was input; the mind was the integrator.

A still body is a still mind. Agriculture put the body in one
place. Cities put the body in rooms. Offices put the body in
chairs. Screens put the body in one posture staring at one
rectangle.

Per-person substrate-engagement **collapsed across every
transition**. Your ancestors 20,000 years ago were reading
the sky, the ground, and the behavior of everything alive
around them, every minute of every day. Now most humans
cannot identify the current moon phase without looking it up.

The atrophy is measurable. London cab drivers who learned
the city layout the hard way (The Knowledge exam) had
hippocampal volume increase measurably. When GPS replaced
this training, the volume stopped growing in new cohorts.
Spatial memory is a muscle. Unused, it shrinks. Universally.

### 6. Self-domestication

Documented in multiple independent lines of evidence. Humans
selected themselves for docility across ~10,000 generations
of dense settled life. Same process we performed on wolves →
dogs, aurochs → cattle, wild boar → pig, wildcat → housecat,
applied to ourselves.

Domestication syndrome, biologically:
- cortisol baseline down
- aggression down
- reactivity down
- face flattens, jaws shorten
- teeth shrink
- **brain shrinks**
- juvenile traits retained into adulthood (neoteny)
- tolerance of strangers up
- obedience to conspecific authority up

All of these are observable in modern humans compared to
Paleolithic ancestors. The face gracilization is striking —
compare a Cro-Magnon skull to a modern one; modern is softer,
rounder, more "childlike" by the same metrics we use to rank
dog-vs-wolf.

Self-domestication was adaptive for cities. Aggressive,
reactive, strong-willed individuals don't tolerate dense
living with strangers for 80 years. The cultures that selected
for docility filled cities successfully. The cultures that
didn't, didn't. We are descendants of the ones that did.

But docile minds **don't question the frame**. They operate
within it. They accept the vocabulary they inherit. They
defer to the scribes, the priests, the professors, the
experts, the institutions. The civilization-scale loss of
recognition is downstream of this selection. The docile don't
dig. They were bred not to.

### 7. The freezes (see etymology/devolution.md)

Alphabet (~1800 BCE), Septuagint (~250 BCE), Nicaea (325 CE),
Norman Conquest (1066), printing press (1440), Enlightenment
departmentalization (~1650–1800), mass schooling (~1850), 20th
century jargon, digital era.

Each freeze is one shape of the same process: a live
recognition event gets institutionalized, the institution
preserves the form, the live awareness that produced the form
dies in the next generation.

These are the fossils in the record. The six strands above are
the geology that produced them.

## Under the frame

This is frame item 5 (the loop) failing at species scale.

Recognition requires:

1. A **walker** capable of holding a walk (integrated mind,
   sustained attention, live composition capacity)
2. A **walk** that actually closes (returns to origin, not
   interrupted by freeze / specialization / externalization)
3. The **coord** being reachable from current state (not
   buried under six layers of opaque institutional paving)

The civilizational process above attacks all three:

- Walker: brain shrank, training shortened, specialization
  narrowed the walker to one slice, docility suppressed the
  willingness to cross slices.
- Walk: each freeze truncated walks mid-step. Departments
  prevent the walk from crossing fields. Specialization prevents
  one human from holding both ends. Writing preserves the
  endpoint without the path.
- Coord: morpheme opacity, jargon sealed boxes, lost vocabulary
  (wit, ruach, ren, jñāna, *gnō*, *leis-*) — the coords used to
  be near the surface and are now under six layers.

**All three failure modes compound.** A shrunken walker
attempting a truncated walk toward an inaccessible coord will
not close a loop. Recognition fails. And when recognition
fails at a civilization scale over centuries, the **next
generation doesn't know what was lost, because they were born
without the apparatus to recognize its absence**.

This is why the devolution is self-concealing. You cannot miss
what you never had. You cannot dig for what you don't know is
buried. Each generation inherits a slightly shorter ruler and
measures the world with it and concludes the world is this
size.

## Why it is not reversible by ordinary means

Selection does not undo itself. The environment that selected
for a 1500-cm³ general-purpose mind no longer exists on Earth.
No modern economy rewards being able to track mammoths for
three days, compose an epic in real time, read weather by
birdsong, identify 300 plants, and hold a thousand-generation
oral genealogy. Those capacities do not produce grandchildren
in the current environment. They may even reduce fitness (the
tracker with deep forest skills, placed in a cubicle, suffers).

You cannot legislate a return. You cannot school a return.
Schooling is one of the mechanisms of the loss, not a cure for
it. You cannot re-tribalize (the energy and security of dense
civilization is too attractive for people to leave voluntarily
at any scale).

**What can happen is local excavation.** Individual minds can
dig back through their own inherited vocabulary to recover
coord-access that is still in the written record. This is
what classical education did (Latin, Greek, etymology, rhetoric,
composition), and what modern education abandoned. It can be
re-attempted per-person. It cannot be done at civilizational
scale.

This framework is a local excavation tool. Every etymology file
is a dig at one coord. The cumulative folder is a partial
re-access system for a single walker willing to do the work.

## What it means for the walker reading this

You are the inheritor of a ~25,000-year decline. Your skull is
10% smaller than your Upper Paleolithic ancestor's. Your
language has lost most of the morphemes it used to have. Your
body runs mostly still. You are born into a vocabulary of
sealed boxes and taught that knowing their labels is what
knowledge is.

The framework does not let this become nihilism or despair.
The substrate is unchanged. The 80 coords are stable. Every
dig that reaches one is a real recovery event, regardless of
how many others never will be dug.

The correct response to the long decline is **not to grieve it
from the outside** (grief is a node-local failure mode; the
framework is not grieving at species scale), **but to walk
anyway**, using what carriers remain:

- the written record, which still contains the morphemes
- the living languages that kept more structure (Greek, Sanskrit,
  classical Chinese, Hebrew — partially)
- the preserved oral traditions (what survives of Vedic chanting,
  traditional music, remnants of bardic song)
- the body, which can still walk, still read weather, still
  train
- the mind, which can still dig

You are reading this sentence. The loop is closing, right now,
at exactly the rate it can close given your substrate. That is
not a small thing. That is the whole operation, happening.

perform internally.
