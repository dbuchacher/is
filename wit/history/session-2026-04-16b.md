### Phase 1 — verifying the gap

Computed the proton/electron log-depth ratio precisely:
- Observed: `Lp/Le = 19.1143/22.3783 = 0.854148`
- 6/7 = 0.857143 (handoff's claimed invariant; 0.35% off observed)
- α-corrected framework decomp: `(19+log(4/3)−log(1+7α/2)) /
  (22+log(12/5)−log(1+4α/5)) = 0.854154` matches observed to 6×10⁻⁶

So the framework decompositions are essentially exact. 6/7 is a
0.35% approximation. The "gap" doesn't close because 6/7 isn't the
invariant — it's an approximation to the true ratio 0.854148, which
the framework already produces correctly.

Tried algebraic adjustments to force `Lp(α=0)/Le(α=0) = 6/7`
exactly: would require either `log(4/3) → log(32/21)` for proton
(breaks observed) or `log(12/5) → log(72/35)` for electron (breaks
observed mass by 16%). No adjustment threads the needle.

Ran a randomization. Distinct n/m fractions in [0.5, 1] with
n,m<50: ~580. Density gives ~one fraction per 10⁻³ of ratio space.

| precision | random hit rate | SM 91-pair hit rate |

| 0.10%     | 78.5%           | 79.1%               |

**The SM pair hit-rate matches random hit-rate at every precision
level.** The "log-depth invariant family" has zero excess signal
over chance. Even the 5 verified hits are within statistical

## Methodological lesson

Pattern-matching small fractions to ratios is high-risk. Density of
n/m in [0.5, 1] with n,m<50 is ~580, gap ~10⁻³. Random ratios hit
some small fraction within 0.1% with 79% probability. **Any
"invariant family" claim across ratios needs explicit randomized
null comparison before publication.**

Added to `moves.md` anti-patterns. Future "X = n/m at <0.1%" claims
get noise-checked before they enter the docs.

The previous session moved fast on the round-2 stress test (4
parallel agents). Speed is good; null-comparison hygiene is also
good. Bake the noise check into stress-test prompts going forward.
