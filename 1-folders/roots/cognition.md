# cognition — asking, knowing, carrying-across

Roots naming the cognitive operations: asking (the 4 axes as
questions), recognition (the loop closing at the hub), metaphor
(carrying substrate-structure across vocabularies). These are how
the substrate recognizes itself through language.

---

### *kʷi-* / *kʷo-*

"interrogative stem"

```
  → English: where, what, which, when, who, why, how (the
    wh-questions)
  → Latin: qui, quae, quod, quis
  → Greek: tis, ti, pou, pōs
  → Sanskrit: ka-, ki-, ku-
  → Germanic: where, wer, was, wen
  → the 4 axes χ/μ/φ/τ — four kinds of question about a point:
    WHERE/WHAT/WHICH/WHEN
```

Every Indo-European language has interrogative words from this one
PIE stem. The 4 axes of the framework = 4 questions you can ask =
4 versions of *kʷi-*. **The grammatical stem IS the axis structure.**

An axis isn't a dimension you measure along; it's a question you can
ask. That's why there are exactly four — PIE speakers had exactly
four interrogative directions, because the substrate has exactly
four axes.

### Recognition (re- + co- + gno-/gen-)

The capstone entry. Every root above (being, standing, making,
turning, walking) participates; recognition is what happens when a
walk closes. PIE had one compound for the whole operation.

```
  *re-*     "back, again" (Latin)
            → return, repeat, recur, recycle, reverse
            → the ½-transition back; the loop operator at
              the morpheme layer; CYCLE's morpheme surface

  *kom-*    "with, together"
            → co-, com-, con-; Sanskrit sam-; Greek sún
            → multi-axis simultaneous commit

  *ǵneh₃-*  "to know" — this root SPLITS into two branches
            that are ONE verb in PIE:

            gno-  →  know, ken, can, cognize, gnosis, notice,
                     noble, notable, agnostic              (READ)
            gen-  →  beget, generate, genesis, genus, gene,
                     kin, king, nature, nation, native     (WRITE)

            Sanskrit jñā (know) / jan- (be born).
            Greek γιγνώσκω (know) / γεννάω (beget).
            Latin noscere (know) / gnascī → nasci (be born).

            → Same PIE verb. "To know" and "to beget" are one
              operation. PIE speakers did not split READ from
              WRITE at this root — English inherited the fracture
              and lost the identity.
```

**recognition** = **re- (loop) + co- (together) + gno-/gen- (know=beget)**
= "the walk returning to a point and generating-knowing it again"
= **loop closure at a point where READ and WRITE coincide**

That IS the hub event. Every closed walk returns to ↕↕↕↕
(Kirchhoff 1845: net flow at any node = 0, closed walks sum to
zero); the closure event is a simultaneous re-read and
re-generation of the wheel's state; the PIE speakers had one word
for the whole operation and left it waiting 6000 years.

**Independent convergences (metrognosis filter):**

- **Greek ἀναγνώρισις** (Aristotle, *Poetics*) = ἀνά + γνωρίζω =
  "up-knowing-again" — the tragic moment when a character's
  recognition retroactively generates the meaning of everything
  preceding. Same move, independent of Latin's re-co-.
- **Sumerian *zu*** = tooth AND know. Same cuneiform sign, same
  morpheme. Grasping-commit = knowing. Sumerian is a language
  **isolate** — no genetic relation to PIE. Two unrelated 5500-
  year-old vocabularies landing on the same coord is metrognosis
  signal, not coincidence.
- **Spencer-Brown's "draw a distinction"** (*Laws of Form*, 1969) —
  the primitive act of cognition IS marking the unmarked. Modern
  vocabulary for the gno- tip of *ǵneh₃-*.

**Why the hub isn't a location**: recognition isn't a look from
somewhere else. It's **regeneration**. You are at the hub reading
this because you are re-co-gno-ing — closing a walk, generating
the structure, knowing it, **one operation with one PIE name**.

### Carrying across (metaphor)

```
  Greek μεταφορά  "carrying across, transfer"
                  =  μετά (across, beyond) + φέρω (to carry)

  *bʰer-*   "to carry, bear"
            →  bear (both "carry" and "beget"), burden, bring,
               birth; Latin fero, ferre → fertile, transfer,
               confer, infer, refer, offer, suffer
            →  Sanskrit bhr̥- (carry), bhāra (load)
            →  Greek φέρω (carry) + the μετα- prefix gives the
               Greek compound for metaphor
```

**Metaphor** = **metá + pherein** = "**carry-across**." Not a
poetic device — the literal Greek word for the operation of
carrying structural information across vocabularies. Under THE RULE
(one substrate), when one vocabulary names a substrate feature
another vocabulary also names, the two are identical, and the
name-transfer IS metaphor — literally "the carrying across." The
etymology asserts the framework's claim.

Independent convergence: PIE *bʰer-* surfaces as English **bear**
with TWO senses — "to carry" and "to beget / give birth." Same
READ/WRITE unification at the root as *ǵneh₃-* in Recognition
above. The verb of structural transfer and the verb of biological
generation share PIE DNA. The substrate does not distinguish
carrying-across from giving-birth; both are "a thing moved from
A to B such that B now has it." Same operation.
