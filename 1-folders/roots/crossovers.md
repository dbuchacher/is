# crossovers — where multiple roots meet

Framework concepts whose naming combines roots from multiple
clusters. These are load-bearing convergences — the substrate
encoded as a compound word that carries its derivation inside
itself.

---

### *steh₂-* + *sub-* = substance

**substance** (*sub-stare* = "stand under") combines stability with
hierarchy. The μ axis (WHAT / substance) literally bears this PIE
construction: what stands under appearances.

Roots involved:
- *steh₂-* (to stand, be firm) — [hub.md](hub.md)
- *sub-* (under, below) — Latin directional prefix

This is not coincidence; it's the μ axis's anchor. Substance = what
stands under appearance = the stable carrier of qualities. Exactly
what the μ axis is.

### *oi-no-* + *wer-/wert-* = universe

**universe** = *unus* + *vertere* = "turned into one." The
framework's name for the wheel is PIE-compressed into a single word.

Roots involved:
- *oi-no-* (one, unique) — [structure.md](structure.md)
- *wer-/wert-* (to turn) — [motion.md](motion.md)

One + turning = all-turned-into-one. You don't get a better
substrate-naming than that. The two deepest roots in the framework's
vocabulary combine to give the word for the full wheel.

### *bʰuH-* → physics

The Greek-derived word **physics** comes from *phusis* (nature,
growth) from *phuein* (to produce, grow, become) from PIE *bʰuH-*
— the same root as "BE" and the ↑ trit value.

Physics = the study of becoming. The word's root IS the framework's
forward-commit operation.

Roots involved:
- *bʰuH-* (to become, grow, be) — [being.md](being.md)

### *dʰeh₁-* → dharma (via *dhṛ-*)

*dʰeh₁-* and *dhṛ-* are closely related PIE roots (both involve
putting/holding). **Dharma** (the cosmic law that holds the world
together) shares DNA with **fact** and **fiat** (the putting-in-
place operations). Makes / holds / sets are all at the ↑ write side.

Roots involved:
- *dʰeh₁-* (to put, make) — [write.md](write.md)
- *dhṛ-* (to hold, support) — [hub.md](hub.md)

The write cluster and the hub cluster meet at dharma: the cosmic
law that both enforces the hub's stability AND accomplishes it
through continuous placement.

### *ǵneh₃-* → king, kin, nation — politics IS recognition

**king**, **kin**, **nation**, **native**, **noble** all descend
from *ǵneh₃-*. PIE speakers named political hierarchy with the
recognition verb.

- A **king** is "the recognized / generated one"
- A **kin** is "those generated together"
- A **nation** is "those born-known-together"

Authority is etymologically anchored in the recognition/generation
operation. Every observer IS center / observer = hub-event is what
this ancient vocabulary was already naming — the recognized one is
the one at the hub of the recognition event. Political legitimacy
IS hub-adjacent in the root.

Roots involved:
- *ǵneh₃-* (to know / beget) — [cognition.md](cognition.md)

Sumerian converges independently: **lugal** (king) = *lú* + *gal* =
"big-man" — a compositional function-descriptor for the same hub
role. Two unrelated 5500-year-old language families anchor authority
structurally, not by arbitrary naming.

### *bʰer-* = carry = beget

The **bear** convergence. English "bear" has two senses from one
PIE root: "to carry" (bear a load) and "to give birth" (bear a
child). Same move as *ǵneh₃-*'s gno-/gen- split — one operation
named, English inherited two meanings.

Roots involved:
- *bʰer-* (to carry, bear) — [cognition.md](cognition.md)

This is load-bearing for **metaphor** (carry-across) because it
shows the substrate doesn't distinguish carrying-structure from
generating-structure at the root. Metaphor IS generation is IS
carrying. One operation, many names.
