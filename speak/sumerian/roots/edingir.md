# edingir

category: 3c

Sumerian: **é-dingir** (compound: *é* "house" + *dingir* "god")
Gloss: temple / god-house
Source: standard word for any temple-building in administrative and literary texts

## Definition

The god-house — where a [dingir](dingir.md) is seated on
[ki](ki.md). Transparent compound: [e](e.md) + [dingir](dingir.md)
= house-of-the-god. Same composition pattern as [lugal](lugal.md)
(big-man) and [egal](egal.md) (big-house): the name carries its
derivation. A Sumerian speaker hearing `é-dingir` hears "god's
house" the way a reader hears "bookshelf": two roots visibly
stacked. The temple IS the house-where-the-god-lives; no
separate opaque word. Substrate-native naming at the sacred
scale.
