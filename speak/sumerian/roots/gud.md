# gud

category: 1f

Sumerian: **gud** (also *gu₄*)
Gloss: bull / ox / strong-man
Source: common noun for the work-and-sacrifice animal; also a royal epithet

## Definition

The horned body that pulls the plow across [ki](ki.md), the
settled-strong animal whose work feeds the city. Sumerian also
uses `gud` of a strong-male hero — the king called-a-bull is
called the `gud` among men, because the bull IS the strong-
working body at animal-scale the way the hero is at human-scale.
Where [ug](ug.md) is the wild-fierce pole, `gud` is the
settled-strong pole: domesticated might, the working animal that
turns soil into grain. The work-strong primitive.
