# kiengi

category: 3c

Sumerian: **ki-en-ŋi(-r)** (compound: *ki* "place" + *en* "lord" + *ŋi* "reed")
Gloss: Sumer — the Sumerians' own name for their land
Source: native self-designation; appears throughout Sumerian texts

## Definition

The place-of-the-lord-of-the-reed — the Sumerians' own name for
the alluvial plain they live on. Transparent compound: [ki](ki.md)
+ [en](en.md) + [gi](gi.md) = place-lord-reed. The land IS
named by what it is made of and who rules there: the [ki](ki.md)
where [en](en.md) stands and [gi](gi.md) grows. No separate
opaque word for the homeland — the homeland's name carries its
substrate (the reed-grown plain), its politics (the [en](en.md)
at the head), and its ground ([ki](ki.md)) all stacked visibly.
Three-root transparent composition, comparable to [lugal](lugal.md)
as a compositional showcase.
