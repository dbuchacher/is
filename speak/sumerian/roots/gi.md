# gi

category: 1g

Sumerian: **gi**
Gloss: reed / stalk
Source: the central plant-material of Mesopotamian life; the stylus that makes [dub](dub.md) is cut from `gi`

## Definition

The tall hollow stalk that grows from the [a](a.md) of
irrigation-canals — the plant the Sumerians cut for boats,
baskets, roof-beams, and the stylus that presses marks into
[dub](dub.md). Where [gish](gish.md) is tree-wood, `gi` is the
hollow-stalk-plant: the lighter, bending material. The
substrate of Mesopotamian craft: one plant, many uses. The
reed-primitive, tightly paired with [a](a.md) (grows from
water) and with the name of Sumer itself, which is built on
[gi](gi.md).
