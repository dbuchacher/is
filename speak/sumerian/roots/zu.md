# zu

**seed** | category: 1j

Sumerian: **zu**
Gloss: tooth / to know / to grasp
Source: common verb AND body-part; the sign is the same

## Definition

Grounds by pointing at the biting-organ AND the grasping act of
knowing. The teeth in a body's [ka](ka.md) are `zu`, and so is
the cognitive act of `zu`-ing (knowing, grasping-with-mind). The
Sumerian speaker saw biting-down-on-food and grasping-an-idea as
one stroke: both are closings-of-the-grip on something that was
outside and is now held. The two grips — teeth on meat, mind on
truth — are one morpheme because they are one operation at two
scales. Body-grip and mind-grip share their verb.
