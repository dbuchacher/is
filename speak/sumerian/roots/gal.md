# gal

**seed** | category: 1m

Sumerian: **gal**
Gloss: big / great / large
Source: ubiquitous quality-word; productive "bigifier" in compounds (lú-gal = big-man = king, é-gal = big-house = palace)

## Definition

Grounds by pointing at what takes up more [ki](ki.md) than
its neighbors — a body taller than another body, a house larger
than another house, a river wider than another river. `gal` is
the bare greater-size-than-baseline quality. When Sumerians wanted
to name a chief, they compounded `gal` onto the generic: the
chief IS the big-one, the palace IS the big-house. One of the
most productive morphemes in Sumerian because greater-size maps
onto greater-rank, greater-importance, greater-power by direct
substrate-transparent composition.
