# ud

category: 1e

Sumerian: **ud** (also *u₄*)
Gloss: day / sun / light / time
Source: ubiquitous morpheme; one of the most polysemic signs in Sumerian

## Definition

The bright stroke that comes when [an](an.md) lights up — the sun
is `ud`, the daylight the sun casts is `ud`, the span-of-bright
from sunrise to sunset is `ud`, and the wider sense of "time"
(as measured by day-counts) is also `ud`. Four modern English
concepts in one Sumerian word because sun-light-day-time are one
substrate fact at the scale of a body watching the sky from
[ki](ki.md). The Sumerian speaker did not separate the
light-source from the light from the span-of-light from the
time-measured-by-light. Paired against darkness and against the
moon-cycle ([iti](iti.md)). The bright-pole of the day-night
pair.
