# iti

category: 1e

Sumerian: **iti** (also *itud*)
Gloss: month / moon
Source: calendrical and astronomical texts

## Definition

The pale-body that crosses [an](an.md) at night, and the span of
days measured from one full pale-body to the next. Moon and
month are one word because the month IS the moon's cycle: from
new to full and back is the unit of time a Sumerian speaker
counts by. Paired against the sun-body as the counterpart sky-body: the
two sky-bodies name the two time-units. Unity-preservation: the
counted time AND the counting body are one word.
