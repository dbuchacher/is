# lil

category: 1e

Sumerian: **lil₂**
Gloss: wind / breath / spirit / open air
Source: root of the god-name Enlil; common noun for the moving-air between [an](an.md) and [ki](ki.md)

## Definition

The moving-air that blows across [ki](ki.md) — not the still sky
[an](an.md) above, not the solid ground below, but the layer of
breath-of-the-world in between. `lil₂` is the wind that the body
feels, the open-space where air moves, and the life-breath that
a [til](til.md)-ing body pulls in and pushes out. The same word
names the wind in the reeds, the gust across the plain, and the
breath of a being. Spoken-breath in [dug4](dug4.md) is `lil₂`
shaped at [ka](ka.md). Paired with [a](a.md) (water) and [izi](izi.md)
(fire) as one of the moving substances.
