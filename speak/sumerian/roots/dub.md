# dub

category: 1e

Sumerian: **dub**
Gloss: tablet / inscribed clay
Source: the word for the cuneiform writing-medium; foundational in scribal texts

## Definition

A flat piece of [im](im.md) pressed with a reed and dried — the
clay-surface that holds marks after a scribe has cut them. The
Sumerian writing-substrate, named. Each `dub` is shaped from
[im](im.md) (the same `im` that is wind-rain-cloud), then
marked, then dried, then kept. Where [im](im.md) names the
substance and its weather-cycle, `dub` names the worked piece
that carries marks — the record-bearing tablet. The scribe writes
on it and the tablet-house holds rows of them ready for later
[igi](igi.md).
