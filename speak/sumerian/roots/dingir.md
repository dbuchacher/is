# dingir

category: 1e

Sumerian: **dingir**
Gloss: god / divinity
Source: the word pronounced when reading the sign 𒀭 in its divine use

## Definition

The same sign as [an](an.md) — 𒀭 — read `dingir` when it stands
alone as "god" and `an` when it means "sky." Sumerian writing
does not separate the two readings because the underlying
substrate is one: a god IS a sky-being, a sky-thing IS a god.
Every god's name in Sumerian writing begins with 𒀭 because
divine-nature is sky-nature. The written sign [an](an.md) carries
two pronunciations for two applications of one fact. `dingir`
points at a being seated in [an](an.md), the divine-pole of the
vertical axis.
