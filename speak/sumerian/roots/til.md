# til

**seed** | category: 1h

Sumerian: **til** (also *til₃*)
Gloss: to live / to complete / to finish / to be full
Source: throughout Sumerian; the common verb for both "living" and "completing"

## Definition

Grounds by pointing at a body that walks, breathes, eats — the
body that [me](me.md)-s over time rather than in one moment. But
the same word means "to complete, to finish, to make full" — a
life is what FINISHES correctly, and completing something IS
bringing it to its living state. The Sumerian speaker named
being-alive and being-completed with one word because a life that
does not [til](til.md) does not reach its end, and a thing that
does not [til](til.md) is not full. Loop-closure named as a verb:
the walk that completes IS the life lived.
