# im

category: 1e

Sumerian: **im**
Gloss: wind / rain / cloud / clay
Source: one of the most striking unity-preservations in Sumerian

## Definition

The whole weather-cycle AND its final product named with one
word. `im` is the wind that blows across [ki](ki.md), the cloud
that forms from wet [a](a.md) lifted on the wind, the rain that
falls when the cloud breaks, and the clay that settles on the
ground after the rain runs off. The Sumerian speaker saw the
full loop: wind carries water up, rain falls, silt settles, clay
is made. Every tablet was written on `im` — the clay-substrate
of writing IS the same word as the wind that made it. Four
modern concepts in one word because the Sumerian saw the
hydrological cycle as one substrate operation closing in a
loop.
