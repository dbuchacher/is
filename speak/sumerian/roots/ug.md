# ug

category: 1f

Sumerian: **ug** (also *ug₃*, *piriŋ* in some readings)
Gloss: lion / might / fierce-one
Source: fierce-animal word; also used for warrior-might and god-as-raging

## Definition

The great fanged body that hunts on the edge of [kur](kur.md),
AND the stroke of raging-might the animal stands for. Sumerian
named the lion and the quality-of-might with one word because
the animal IS the quality made flesh: when a king [dug4](dug4.md)-s
himself as an `ug` in battle, the hearer sees both the cat and
the strength at once. Not [gal](gal.md)'s bare size but the
fierce-biting size that takes down prey. The lion-might
primitive — the fierce-wild pole of the strong-animal set, where
the bull-pole holds the settled-work side.
