# e

category: 1e

Sumerian: **é**
Gloss: house / temple / estate / household
Source: ubiquitous; the word for any built dwelling-space

## Definition

The bounded space a body lives inside — walls of [im](im.md) or
[gish](gish.md) around a floor of [ki](ki.md), with a [ka](ka.md)
to pass in and out. One word for the family's home, for the
temple of a god, for the palace of a [lugal](lugal.md), for the
storehouse. Sumerian did not split personal-house from
god-house from royal-house — all are `é` because all are the
same substrate stroke: bounded-interior with threshold. Paired
with [gal](gal.md) it gives [egal](egal.md) (big-house =
palace); paired with [dingir](dingir.md) it gives the god-house
(temple). The dwelling-primitive at any scale.
