# geshtug

**seed** | category: 1j

Sumerian: **ŋeštug** (also *ŋeš-tug₂*)
Gloss: ear / hearing / wisdom / understanding
Source: literary texts; central in wisdom-literature

## Definition

Grounds by pointing at the hearing-organ AND the understanding
that comes through hearing. The two ears of a body are `ŋeštug`,
and so is the act of [ŋeštug](geshtug.md)-ing (hearing) — and so
is the wisdom that the [ŋeštug](geshtug.md)-ing body carries
after enough has been heard. Sumerian named hearing-organ,
hearing-act, and wisdom-as-accumulated-hearing with one word: the
wise one IS the one whose ears have been open. Cognitive-grasp-
through-hearing as a single morpheme, parallel to [zu](zu.md)
(knowing-through-grasping).
