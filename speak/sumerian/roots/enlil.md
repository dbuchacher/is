# enlil

category: 3c

Sumerian: **en-lil₂** (compound: *en* "lord" + *lil₂* "wind/breath/air")
Gloss: Enlil — the lord of wind / god of the middle-air
Source: head of the Mesopotamian pantheon from Early Dynastic texts onward; chief god of Nippur

## Definition

The lord-of-wind, the [en](en.md) whose domain is [lil](lil.md).
Transparent compound: [en](en.md) + [lil](lil.md) = lord-of-the-
moving-air. Paired in the divine set with [an](an.md) (sky-god,
the upper pole) and the lord-of-waters (the lower pole): three
gods for three cosmic layers, and each name carries its domain
inside itself. The middle-pole of the divine triad, master of
the space between [an](an.md) and [ki](ki.md) where storms move
and breath goes. The god's name IS his domain.
