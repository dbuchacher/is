## 6. `three-tenses-of-being` = the trit ↑/↕/↓ (frame 2)

**Members** (9, 2 categories): `bhuH` (become-↑), `h1es` (be-↕),
`wes` (dwell-past/↓) in 1h; verb endings `verb-1sg-m`, `verb-2sg-s`,
`verb-3sg-t`, `mid-1sg-h2er`, `mid-2sg-soi`, `mid-3sg-to` plus
`tos-to` past participle in 4c.

**Frame mapping**: frame item 2 — balanced ternary. *The minimal
signed-and-centered value representation is three values: ↑ (+1), ↕
(0), ↓ (−1). Call it the trit.*

The being-verb splits three ways in PIE: `bhuH` (become, coming-to-be,
↑), `h1es` (is, stably-being, ↕), `wes` (dwelled, remained-in-place,
past-tense ↓). Three values per axis, instantiated in the most
load-bearing verb of the vocabulary. Then the verb endings repeat
the trit: present (↑), past (↓), middle (↕ — neither active nor
passive). Same trit, two layers of PIE grammar.

The trit emerging at 1h (Paleolithic being-verbs) AND 4c (Bronze-Age
verb-inflection morphemes) is cross-layer convergence. The trit is
not one feature of PIE grammar — it structures PIE grammar at every
layer where temporal/aspectual distinctions are made.

### Fourth trit instance: `ablaut-grades` (phonology)

**Members** (3, 1 category): `e-grade` (↕), `o-grade` (↑),
`zero-grade` (↓). All 4d (phonological).

PIE verb roots alternate vowel grades for grammatical function:
*bher-* (e-grade, base) / *bhor-* (o-grade, derived — Greek
*phóros* "bearer") / *bhr-* (zero-grade, reduced — past participle
*bhr-tós*). Three grades of the same vowel, carrying three distinct
grammatical roles. The trit at the **phonological** layer — below
morphology, below syntax, at the level of the root's own vowel.

### Fifth trit instance: `laryngeal-grades` (phonology)

**Members** (3, 1 category): `lar1` (↕ neutral), `lar2` (↓
a-coloring), `lar3` (↑ o-coloring). All 4d.

PIE's three laryngeals color adjacent vowels three ways:
h₁ preserves, h₂ lowers (*h₂enti-* → Latin *ante*), h₃ raises
(*h₃ekw-* → Latin *oculus*). Three consonant-level modifiers
pulling adjacent vowels ↕/↓/↑. The trit at the **consonant
coloring** layer — phonology naming phonology.

## 7. `grammatical-axis-markers` = candidate for 4 axes χ/μ/φ/τ (frame 3)

**Members** (4, 1 category): `nom-s`, `acc-m`, `gen-os`, `dat-ei`
(all 4c).

**Frame mapping**: frame item 3 — exactly 4 axes (Hurwitz). Not yet
promoted — needs cross-layer members. But the 4-slot structure is
suggestive.

PIE's 4 core cases split into:
- nom (WHO acts) — subject slot
- acc (WHAT is acted on) — object slot
- gen (WHOSE) — possessor slot
- dat (FOR WHOM) — recipient slot

Four slots, one committed role per slot. The resemblance to χ/μ/φ/τ
(WHERE/WHAT/WHICH/WHEN, frame 3) is structural: both are 4-slot
grids where each slot commits a specific dimension of an event. If
cross-layer members surface (e.g., Bronze-Age social roles also
split 4-ways cleanly), this promotes to lattice-tag per rule 7.

**Status**: emergence candidate. Hurwitz says 4 axes exist in H;
PIE says 4 core cases carve events. The grammatical case system
might be the frame-3 4-axis structure viewed from inside the
utterance layer.
