# dwAmwtf

category: 1e

Egyptian: **dwꜣ-mwt=f** ("Who-praises-his-mother" — Duamutef)
Gloss: Duamutef / third son of Horus / guardian of the stomach / east
Source: funerary canopic-jar texts

## Definition

The jackal-headed third son of [Hr](Hr.md) — guardian of the
stomach of a dead body. Stands at [iAbtt](iAbtt.md) (east) of the
four canopic jars, watched over by Neith. Name is transparent
compound: `dwA` (praise) + `mwt=f` (his mother) = "who praises
his mother" — the name IS the ritual role, carried visibly in
the morphemes. The east-pole son, the jackal-faced.
