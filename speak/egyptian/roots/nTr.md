# nTr

category: 1e

Egyptian: **nṯr** (written with the god-flag 𓊹)
Gloss: god / divine / sacred
Source: universal; the generic word for any god

## Definition

The generic word for any divine body — [ra](ra.md), [Hr](Hr.md),
[ptH](ptH.md), any of them is a `nTr`. The classifier 𓊹 attaches
to every divine name in writing, just as Sumerian prefixes 𒀭
(an/dingir). Parallel naming-pattern: Egyptian has one generic
divine-marker, one generic divine-word, and names each specific
deity as a particular `nTr`. The generic pole of the divine
class.
