# itm

category: 1e

Egyptian: **itm / tm** (Atum)
Gloss: Atum / the completed-one / the self-arising creator
Source: Pyramid Texts; central to Heliopolitan creation

## Definition

The self-arising god who [xpr](xpr.md)-ed himself out of
[nwn](nwn.md) at the first dawn, before [pt](pt.md) and [tA](tA.md)
were separated. `itm` means "the completed one" — the god who
holds all-that-is in himself before anything is separated from
anything else. From `itm` the first divine-pair was spat forth
(air and moisture), beginning the unfolding of the Ennead of
nine gods. The creation-hub-pole: the first body
that [wn](wn.md)-s, from whom all others derive.
