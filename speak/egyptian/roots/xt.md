# xt

category: 1f

Egyptian: **ḫt** (written with a tree-branch 𓆱)
Gloss: thing / wood / stick / staff / tree
Source: universal noun; Gardiner sign-list M3

## Definition

The thing-of-wood — a branch, a staff, a post, the timber of a
tree. Used as the generic noun for any named item when the
scribe wants to point at something-with-shape: "nb xt" = "lord
of [nb](nb.md) things" = lord of all. Wood-of-tree AND generic-
thing in one word because a tree-branch was the first named-solid
— shaped, holdable, countable. From `xt` a staff is made; from
the staff a scepter is made; from the scepter authority is named.
The thingness-pole of Egyptian vocabulary.
