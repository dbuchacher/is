# Hwt

category: 1f

Egyptian: **ḥwt** (written with an enclosure-plan 𓉗)
Gloss: enclosure / mansion / temple / precinct
Source: common; appears in Hwt-nTr (god-temple), Hwt-kA (ka-chapel)

## Definition

The walled-ground around a [pr](pr.md) or a shrine — a larger
bounded space, often including a courtyard plus the house
within. Where `pr` is the house itself, `Hwt` is the whole
enclosed-ground: mansion, palace-precinct, temple-complex. `Hwt-
nTr` is "enclosure of [nTr](nTr.md)" = temple proper. `Hwt-kA` is
"enclosure of [kA](kA.md)" = the offering-chapel where food feeds
the dead body's life-force. Nephthys is "nbt-Hwt" ([nb](nb.md) +
`Hwt`) = "lady of the enclosure." The bounded-sacred-ground.
