# Hkmw

category: 1l

Egyptian: **ḥkꜣw** (often written *heka*; ḥkmw is an alternative rendering)
Gloss: wisdom-speech / magic / effective-utterance / heka-force
Source: Pyramid Texts, Coffin Texts; personified as the god Heka

## Definition

The force-inside-the-word that makes it work — when [Dd](Dd.md)
is spoken with `Hkmw`, the thing said [xpr](xpr.md)-s. Related
to [Hw](Hw.md) (authoritative-utterance) but wider: `Hkmw` is
the wisdom-quality a body carries that makes its speech-acts
effective, and the force itself behind the creation-act. Any
body — god, king, priest, magician — who knows the proper
[mdw](mdw.md) and has `Hkmw` in their [ib](ib.md) can bring
things into [wn](wn.md). Personified as a minor [nTr](nTr.md),
Heka, who was present at the creation-hub alongside [sjA](sjA.md)
and [Hw](Hw.md).
