# xmt

category: 1b

Egyptian: **ḫmt**
Gloss: three / triad
Source: standard numeral; base for many triadic divine structures

## Definition

Grounds by pointing at three bodies together — the paired
[snwy](snwy.md) plus one more. The third count, the first
triad-complete. Egyptian theology uses `xmt` as the base count for
divine triads: father/mother/child triads (wsir/Ast/Hr), thought/
command/action triads ([sjA](sjA.md)/[Hw](Hw.md)/[xpr](xpr.md)),
capital-god triads (Amun/Ra/Ptah). The triad is the completed
minimum-structure of a named group. Three completes what two
cannot.
