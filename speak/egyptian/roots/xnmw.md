# xnmw

category: 1e

Egyptian: **ḫnmw** (Khnum)
Gloss: Khnum / the potter-god / ram-of-Elephantine / shaper-of-clay
Source: Pyramid Texts and later hymns; central at Elephantine

## Definition

The ram-headed [nTr](nTr.md) who shapes bodies on his potter's
wheel — Khnum molds each newborn body and its [kA](kA.md) from
Nile-[mw](mw.md) clay before placing it in the mother's womb.
Where [ptH](ptH.md) creates by [ib](ib.md)-and-[rA](rA.md)
(thought-and-speech), `xnmw` creates by hand-on-clay: the
shaping-pole of the creation-verbs. Guardian of the Nile-source
cataract at Elephantine where the flood-[mw](mw.md) emerges.
Ram-body AND potter-god as one word: the ram is the visible
strength-of-making, the potter is the divine-making-hand.
