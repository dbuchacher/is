# HmnTr

category: 1g

Egyptian: **ḥm-nṯr** (literally "servant of the god")
Gloss: priest / prophet / god's-servant
Source: standard priestly title; every temple had its Hm-nTr ranks

## Definition

Transparent compound: [Hm](Hm.md) (servant) + [nTr](nTr.md) (god)
= "servant of the god" = priest. The body bound in service to a
specific [nTr](nTr.md) — tending the cult-image, performing the
daily offerings, reciting the ritual utterances in the [Hwt-
nTr](Hwt.md) (temple). First-rank, second-rank, third-rank,
fourth-rank `HmnTr` held the priestly hierarchy of each temple.
A priest is a servant-of-the-god by the same logic that the king
is [praA](praA.md): naming a body by its bound-role.
