# nsw

category: 1g

Egyptian: **nsw** (also *nswt*; written with the sedge-plant 𓇓)
Gloss: king / king of Upper Egypt / sedge-king
Source: royal titulary; the "nsw-bjty" formula

## Definition

The king of [rsj](rsj.md) (Upper Egypt, the south-land) — named
by the sedge-plant that grows along the upstream Nile-banks. The
sedge IS the south-land's heraldic plant; the king of that land
is named by it. `nsw` pairs with the bee-king of the north-land in the
royal formula "nsw-bjty" = king of Upper-and-Lower Egypt. One
king, two heraldic-halves: sedge on top, bee below, as the land
itself is upstream and downstream of [mw](mw.md). Sedge-plant
AND king-of-south as one word.
