# Sw

category: 1e

Egyptian: **šw** (Shu — air-god; the word also means "air," "dryness," "sunlight")
Gloss: Shu / air / dry-light-space / god of atmosphere
Source: Pyramid Texts; first son of Atum

## Definition

The first being [itm](itm.md) spat forth — the dry atmosphere
that holds [pt](pt.md) apart from [tA](tA.md). `Sw` is the god,
the air itself, the dry-light space between earth and sky. 

Atum. The 3rd god of the Ennead (counting Atum=1, Shu=2, Tefnut
=3). Shu holds up Nut (sky) with his arms so Geb (earth) can lie
beneath — a cosmological move that separates the primordial
unity into the vertical axis [pt](pt.md)-[tA](tA.md).
