# nb

category: 1m

Egyptian: **nb** (written with the basket 𓎟)
Gloss: lord / master / all / every / the-totality
Source: ubiquitous; appears in every royal and divine title

## Definition

The holder-of-all — `nb` names the body that possesses every
item in a set. Pharaoh is "nb tAwy" (lord of the
two lands). A [nTr](nTr.md) is "nb Xrt-nTr" (lord of the
necropolis), etc. But `nb` is ALSO the generic word for "all /
every": "nb" after a noun means "every such". Lord AND all-of-
them share one word because the lord is the one who holds the
all. The basket-glyph 𓎟 is the classifier for this
containing-all: a basket holds a set of things; the lord holds a
set of lands or people. The totality-primitive.
