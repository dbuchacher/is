# snwy

category: 1b

Egyptian: **snwj** (dual form of *sn*)
Gloss: two / the two / pair / brothers
Source: appears in "the two lands," "the two ladies," etc.

## Definition

Grounds by pointing at two bodies taken together — [wa](wa.md)
plus [wa](wa.md), the paired-set. `snwy` is the dual form: `sn`
means brother, `snwy` means "the two" (brothers or any pair).
Egyptian uses `snwy` in the kingdom-name "Lord of the Two Lands"
(`nb-tAwy`) — Upper and Lower Egypt as paired entities. The
paired-count: exactly-two bodies in one set.
