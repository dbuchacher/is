# xnt

category: 1i

Egyptian: **ḫnt / ḫntj** (boat with sail up — wind from north)
Gloss: to go upstream / to travel south / to sail against the current
Source: paired opposite of xd; base motion-verb for upstream travel

## Definition

To [Sm](Sm.md) the way [mw](mw.md) comes from — upstream,
toward [rsj](rsj.md) (south), toward the Nile's source in the
far south-land. Where downstream-motion goes with the current,
`xnt` goes against it, needing the sail raised to catch the
prevailing north-wind that lets boats beat upstream. The glyph
for `xnt` shows the sail up. Motion AND cardinal-south as one
verb. Also extends to "foremost / in front of" — `xnty` = "the
one-who-goes-in-front" = chief, leader. The upstream-pole of
the Nile-motion axis.
