# kA

category: 1l

Egyptian: **kꜣ** (written with two upraised arms 𓂓)
Gloss: life-force / ka-soul / double / bull / sustenance
Source: central theological term; one of the three parts of the Egyptian soul

## Definition

The life-force a body carries — the animating double that is born
with the body and receives offerings after the body stops
[wn](wn.md)-ing. `kA` is one of three Egyptian soul-parts; the other two surface
as individual-soul and glorified-spirit. The ↑ of the soul-trit:
`kA` is the coming-into of life-force at [xpr](xpr.md), the
upward-commit that makes a body alive. Same word as "bull" (the strong male animal) because
the bull IS the visible life-force in herd form. Food-offerings
are called "the kAw" — the life-forces that keep the soul
nourished after death.
