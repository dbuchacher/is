# isft

category: 1l

Egyptian: **jsft**
Gloss: disorder / wrongdoing / lie / un-ma'at
Source: wisdom-texts and judgment-scenes; always paired against mAat

## Definition

The missing of [mAat](mAat.md) — when things are NOT in their
right-fit, when the [ib](ib.md) devises a lie, when a verdict is
unjust, when [ra](ra.md)'s journey is interrupted. Where `mAat`
is the holding-in-proper-place, `isft` is the falling-out of
proper-place at every scale. In judgment scenes the dead body's
heart is weighed against the [Sw](Sw.md)-feather of [mAat](mAat.md);
if `isft` weighs down the heart, the body is devoured by Ammit
and fails to become [Ax](Ax.md). Paired against [mAat](mAat.md)
as the chaos-pole against the order-pole — and tied to
[stS](stS.md) at the divine scale (Set brings `isft`; Osiris
restores `mAat`).
