# tr

category: 1o

Egyptian: **tr** (also written with season-determinative)
Gloss: season / time-of-the-year / appointed-time
Source: standard time-word; Egypt had three seasons

## Definition

The medium-scale span inside the year — Egypt held three `tr`
per year: Akhet (flood, when [mw](mw.md) rises over
[tA](tA.md)), Peret (growing, when land [xpr](xpr.md)-s green),
and Shemu (harvest, when crops are cut). Three `tr` per year,
four months per `tr`. `tr` is also "appointed time / the proper
[mAat](mAat.md)-moment for a thing" — as in "the `tr` of the
flood" or "the `tr` of the offering." Season-span AND appointed-
proper-time in one word: both ARE the right-fit slot of the
cycle when its event belongs.
