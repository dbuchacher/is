# imn

category: 1e

Egyptian: **jmn** (Amun)
Gloss: Amun / the hidden-one / the unseen-god
Source: Theban theology; rose to state-god in New Kingdom

## Definition

The [nTr](nTr.md) whose name means "the hidden-one" —
`imn` IS the verb "to be hidden" made into a divine-body. Amun
is the god who cannot be [mAA](mAA.md)-ed directly: his true form
is unknown, his breath is the wind, his presence is felt without
being seen. Paired in the capital-triad with [ra](ra.md) (sun =
visible) and [ptH](ptH.md) (thought-and-speech creator). Amun-Ra
is the hidden-revealed compound: the sun's unseen depth and its
visible disc held together. Hidden-one AND god as one word, same
unity as [ra](ra.md) (sun=god) and [nwn](nwn.md) (waters=god).
