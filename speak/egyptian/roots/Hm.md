# Hm

category: 1g

Egyptian: **ḥm** (written with a seated-servant-glyph)
Gloss: servant / worshipper / majesty / incarnation
Source: common; appears in Hm-nTr (god-servant) and Hm=f (his majesty)

## Definition

A body bound in service to a [nb](nb.md) or [nTr](nTr.md) —
servant at the mundane scale, worshipper at the divine scale.
But `Hm` also runs the other way: when said of the king himself,
`Hm=f` = "his majesty," the king-as-incarnation. Servant AND
majesty in one word because `Hm` names the body-that-stands-in-
for-another: the servant stands in for his master's hand, the
king's `Hm` stands in for the king's body in speech. Base of
the compound "Hm-nTr" = servant-of-god = priest.
