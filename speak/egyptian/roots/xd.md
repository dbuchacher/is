# xd

category: 1i

Egyptian: **ḫd** (written with the boat-moving-downstream sign)
Gloss: to go downstream / to travel north / to sail with the current
Source: paired opposite of xnt; base motion-verb for Nile travel

## Definition

To [Sm](Sm.md) the way [mw](mw.md) flows — downstream, toward
[mHty](mHty.md) (north), toward the delta-mouth of the Nile.
Because the river flows south-to-north, Egyptian fused
"downstream" and "north" into one motion-verb: `xd` IS "go-
with-current" AND "go-north." Paired against [Sm](Sm.md)-
upstream. The boat-glyph for `xd` shows sails down (wind at the
back, or no-sail-needed because the current carries the boat).
Motion AND cardinal-north as one verb — the Nile-axis of
Egyptian space made into the motion-axis of Egyptian verbs.
