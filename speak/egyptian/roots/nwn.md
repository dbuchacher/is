# nwn

category: 1e

Egyptian: **nwn** (also *nꜣ-nꜣ*)
Gloss: primeval water / primordial flood / Nun (deified)
Source: central in Pyramid Texts and Memphite Theology — the source-water before creation

## Definition

The [mw](mw.md) that was there before [pt](pt.md) and [tA](tA.md)
were separated — the formless waters from which Atum [xpr](xpr.md)-ed
himself when he first came into being. The primordial-state of
water, out of which [ra](ra.md) rises each dawn as a renewal of
the first becoming. `nwn` is both the waters AND the god of the
waters (same unity-preservation pattern as [ra](ra.md) sun-and-
god). When a Sumerian temple speaks of the abyss, the Egyptian
cognate is `nwn`. The pre-existence-water from which what IS
arose.
