# wj

**seed** | category: 1a

Egyptian: **=j** / **wj** (1sg suffix / enclitic)
Gloss: I / me (first-person singular)
Source: attested from Old Kingdom; standard pronominal suffix throughout

## Definition

Grounds by pointing at the speaker — the body whose [rA](rA.md)
shapes the utterance. The speaker-pole. When a scribe carves an
utterance and names the speaker's self, the morpheme is `=j`.
Paired against [tw](tw.md) (addressee) and [sw](sw.md) /
[sy](sy.md) (third body).
