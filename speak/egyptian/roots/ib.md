# ib

**seed** | category: 1d

Egyptian: **ib**
Gloss: heart / mind / inner-self / will / desire
Source: one of the central Egyptian nouns; the seat of cognition

## Definition

Grounds by pointing at the inner-beating-organ AND at the seat of
thought and will. Egyptian did not split mind from heart: `ib` is
where thinking happens, where decisions are made, where wisdom is
kept, AND it is the muscle in the chest. When a scribe writes
`ib=j` ("my heart"), the speaker means both organ and mind at
once. In Memphite theology, Ptah creates by what [ib](ib.md) has
devised — thought lives in heart, and heart is the READ-seat of
the creative act. Paired with [rA](rA.md) (mouth/tongue) in the
creation-pair: ib devises, rA commands.
