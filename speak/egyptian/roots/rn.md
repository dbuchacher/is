# rn

category: 1c

Egyptian: **rn** (written with the mouth + wavy-line 𓂋𓈖)
Gloss: name / identity / the-said-of-a-body
Source: universal; central to Egyptian funerary theology of survival

## Definition

The saying by which a body is called out — what comes out of
[rA](rA.md) when another body is pointed at. But `rn` is far
more than a label in Egyptian thought: the `rn` IS a part of the
body, one of the things that must survive for the dead to
[wn](wn.md) in the afterlife. Destroying a body's `rn` (scratching
it off a monument) IS destroying the body itself. Preserving the
`rn` in stone is preserving the body. Saying a dead body's `rn`
in offerings keeps its [kA](kA.md) fed. Name AND body-part AND
survival-vehicle, all one word, because the saying-of-a-body
holds what the body IS.
