# mdw

category: 1k

Egyptian: **mdw** (written with the staff 𓌂)
Gloss: word / speech / staff / to speak
Source: Pyramid Texts; base of mdw-nTr ("god's-words" = hieroglyphs)

## Definition

The staff-that-speaks — `mdw` is both the physical staff a body
leans on and the word a body [Dd](Dd.md)-s. One morpheme for
upright-holdable-stick AND upright-spoken-word: the staff holds
the body, the word holds the meaning. Where [Dd](Dd.md) is the
bare speech-act, `mdw` is the uttered-word as a standing-thing —
the word-shaped-like-a-staff, the word-with-authority. The king
holds a `mdw` (staff) and speaks `mdw` (words); the two acts are
named by one word because both are the upright-commitment of the
body. Base of the great compound "god's-words" (hieroglyphs):
`mdw` + `nTr` = divine-staff-made-visible.
