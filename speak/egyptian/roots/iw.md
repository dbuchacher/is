# iw

**seed** | category: 1h

Egyptian: **iw**
Gloss: is / circumstantial "being" particle
Source: very common in Middle Egyptian narrative; marks circumstantial clauses

## Definition

Grounds by pointing at a body in a state — "is X" when what
matters is the ongoing-condition, not the bare existence. Where
[wn](wn.md) is the plain is-there, `iw` is the holding-in-this-
state is. Appears at the start of most Middle Egyptian
affirmative clauses, carrying the now-state of the predicate.
The circumstantial-pole of the being-verb set.
