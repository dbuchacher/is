# bA

category: 1l

Egyptian: **bꜣ** (written with the jabiru-bird 𓅡)
Gloss: soul-individual / ba-soul / ram / manifestation
Source: central soul-term; appears widely in funerary literature

## Definition

The individual-soul that leaves the body at death and flies as a
human-headed bird — a soul with a personal identity that travels
between [pt](pt.md) and [tA](tA.md). Where [kA](kA.md) is the
life-force, `bA` is the individuated-soul, the one-body-one-bA.
Also "ram" (the strong male animal) — visible bA-manifestation in
herd form, paralleling kA's bull. Paired in the soul-trit: `kA`
↑ carries life-force, `bA` ↕ is the individual-body expression,
[Ax](Ax.md) ↓ is the glorified-state after death. Three souls,
three roles, one body.
