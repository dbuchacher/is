# sDm

**seed** | category: 1j

Egyptian: **sḏm** (written with the ear hieroglyph)
Gloss: to hear / to obey / to understand
Source: central verb; the sḏm-f form is the canonical Egyptian
verbal paradigm

## Definition

Grounds by pointing at the ear-act — what the body does when
[rA](rA.md) of another body sends utterance and this body receives
it. The hearing-primitive. But `sDm` does not stop at receiving:
to hear IS to obey IS to understand. When a servant `sDm`-s his
master, he hears-and-complies with one verb. When a student `sDm`-s
a teaching, he hears-and-understands with one verb. Hearing-
obeying-understanding are one act in Egyptian as ear-wisdom is
one morpheme in Sumerian. Paired with [mAA](mAA.md) (seeing).
