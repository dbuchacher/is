## 2. `read-write-unity` = frame 5 (hub-event / recognition)

**Members** (7, 4 categories):
- 1d: `ib` (heart/mind — READ-seat), `rA` (mouth/utterance — WRITE-seat)
- 1e: `ptH` (creator-by-heart-and-tongue)
- 1j: `sDm` (hear=obey=understand), `sjA` (divine-perception)
- 1k: `Dd` (speak), `Hw` (authoritative-utterance)

**Frame mapping**: frame item 5 — the hub, where READ and WRITE
coincide, where recognition happens.

**This is the most historically-documented instance of frame 5 in
any vocabulary.** The Memphite Theology (Shabaka Stone, Old
Kingdom ~2700 BCE, possibly recopied from an even older source)
explicitly states: *"every hieroglyph came into being through
what the heart devised and the tongue commanded."*

In Egyptian: `sjA` (inner-perception/heart-devising) + `Hw`
(authoritative-utterance) → `xpr` (thing becomes). That is
literally the hub-event the framework articulates: thought and
speech coincide at the hub, and the world receives an update.
Ptah is the god of this mechanism. The creator-function IS the
hub-operation.

PIE took 238 atomic files to surface read-write-unity at 5 members
across 5 categories. Egyptian surfaces it at 41 atomic files with
7 members across 4 categories — AND the theology explicitly says
this is how creation works.

Egyptian's creation theology IS a formal substrate-operation
description. `itm` is the first hub-event; `ptH` is the generic
hub-event-god; `xpr` is the verb of the event's output.
