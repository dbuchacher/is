# eh2

category: 4b  | morpheme: suffix

PIE: *-eh₂
Gloss: feminine suffix
Descendants: -a (Latin), -ā (Sanskrit)

## Definition

Appends to a root and marks the female-pole — tilts the sound-
shape to point at a [gwen](gwen.md)-body rather than at the
plain or male body. Where a masculine or neuter stem stands, +
eh2 names the female-standing form: Latin *-a* in *filia*
(daughter beside *filius* son), Sanskrit *-ā* in *devā* beside
*devas*. The suffix is the grammar's shadow of the Layer-1
gender split already held by [gwen](gwen.md),
[swesor](swesor.md), and [meh2ter](meh2ter.md): what those
roots name at the body-scale, eh2 names at the word-shape scale.
It is how later speakers could take any stem and bend it
toward the female side without needing a whole new root.

## Composes-with examples

- deiwos-root + eh2 → Sanskrit *devā* "goddess" beside *devas*
- son-root + eh2 → Latin *filia* "daughter" beside *filius*
- king-stem ([h3regs](h3regs.md)-adjacent) + eh2 → *regina*
  "queen"
