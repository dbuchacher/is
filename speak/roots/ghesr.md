# ghesr

category: 1d

PIE: *ǵʰesr-
Gloss: hand
Descendants: Greek *cheir* (chirurgy, surgery)

## Definition

Second PIE word for the same piece of [krep](krep.md) that
[mr-gh](mr-gh.md) names. Two roots for one body-part because
the hand holds two pointings: `mr-gh` catches the carrying and
working hand (the Latin-branch reading), `ghesr` catches the
hand as the fine-gripping tool that shapes and handles — the
Greek-branch reading that gives "chirurgy," the hand-work of
the healer. Same flesh, different shade: where `mr-gh`
leans toward [bher](bher.md) (carry), `ghesr` leans toward the
careful turning-of-a-thing under close [weid](weid.md). The
Paleolithic speaker had both words available and picked
whichever the moment called for.
