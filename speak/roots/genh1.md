# genh1

category: 1c

PIE: *ǵenh₁-
Gloss: beget / kin
Descendants: **kin**, **king**, **gene**, **genus**, **genesis**, **Latin *genus***, Greek *genos*

## Definition

The gen-branch of the know-beget single-verb [gneh3](gneh3.md) —
same root-stem, split by tongue-drift into two glosses. Where
[gneh3](gneh3.md) keeps the take-in-and-put-out unity visible as
one stroke, `genh1` carries only the putting-out half: the
bringing-forth of new life, the act that makes a fresh young kin
stand where none stood before. [h1eg](h1eg.md) and the mate do
the `genh1` act together; what comes out is kin, same line, same
blood. The root then widens: those who share one `genh1` are one
kin-group, and the head of a kin-group is its king — all
stemming from the same bringing-forth stroke.
