# posti

category: 3f

PIE: *po(s)ti-
Gloss: after
Descendants: post-, Latin *post*

## Definition

The time-side "after" and the space-side "behind" — the flip-side
of [pro](pro.md). What lies behind the body's walking, what lies
ahead of time in the not-yet. When the [h1eg](h1eg.md) walks
[h1ey](h1ey.md)-ward, `posti` is the ground falling away behind;
when the speaker stands in [nu](nu.md), `posti` is the moments
still to come, the not-yet side of time. Latin *post* keeps the
full stroke ("behind, after"); English *post-* prefix ("post-war,
post-hoc, post-mortem") runs the same. A small stroke with one
clear pointing: the other-side-from-here on the time-line. Pairs
with [pro](pro.md) as the after-pole against before, and with
[nu](nu.md) as the later-pole against now. The three together —
[pro](pro.md), [nu](nu.md), `posti` — give a trit at time-scope
matching what [wes](wes.md), [h1es](h1es.md), [bhuH](bhuH.md) give
at being-scope: was/is/will-be.
