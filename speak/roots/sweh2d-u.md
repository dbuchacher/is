# sweh2d-u

category: 1m

PIE: *sweh₂d-u-
Gloss: sweet
Descendants: sweet, persuade, Latin *suavis*, Greek *hēdus*

## Definition

The taste the tongue pulls toward — what [gusH](gusH.md) reads
on the ripe berry, on honey robbed from the wild comb, on the
fat ([peyH](peyH.md)) of a well-fed kill, on the first milk of
the mother. `sweh2d-u` names the quality that makes the mouth
want more: the taste the child reaches for without being
taught, the taste the hand returns to again. A `sweh2d-u`
thing draws the eater in; the body reads it as food-that-feeds,
not poison, not bitter, not empty. From the tongue-pull the
word carries to any drawing-in: the sweet-voiced speaker whose
[wekw](wekw.md) pulls the ear, the gentle weather that pulls
the body out of the shelter. What pulls-by-being-good rather
than by force. Set against the sharp-bitter of gall
([ghelh3](ghelh3.md)-tinged) and the mouth-curling of unripe
things.
