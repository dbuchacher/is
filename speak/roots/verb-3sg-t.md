# verb-3sg-t

category: 4c  | morpheme: verb-ending

PIE: *-t
Gloss: verb 3sg past
Descendants: Latin *-t* (erat), Sanskrit *-t*

## Definition

Appends to a verb-stem and marks that-one-did-this-before —
binds the distal pole ([h1e](h1e.md) / [so](so.md)) and the
staying-past pole ([wes](wes.md)) into the verb's tail. Where
stem + verb-3sg-ti carries that-one-now-does, stem + verb-3sg-t
carries that-one-did. Latin *erat* "he/she/it was" holds the
clean pair with *est* — the presence/past minimal swing at
the third-person pole. Sanskrit aorist and imperfect *-t* ride
the same hinge.

## Composes-with examples

- [h1es](h1es.md) stem + verb-3sg-t → Latin *erat* "he was"
- [bher](bher.md) stem + verb-3sg-t → "he bore"
- [deh3](deh3.md) stem + verb-3sg-t → "he gave"
