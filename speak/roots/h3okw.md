# h3okw

category: 1d

PIE: *h₃ókʷ-
Gloss: eye
Descendants: eye, Latin *oculus*, Greek *ops*, Sanskrit *akṣi*

## Definition

The wet round opening on the front of [kap-ut](kap-ut.md)
through which [weid](weid.md) happens — the part of
[krep](krep.md) that light enters and through which
[h1eg](h1eg.md) takes in what stands at [h1e](h1e.md). `h3okw`
is the read-organ: shut it and [weid](weid.md) stops, open it
and the world pours in. A speaker points at the eye when
naming this root, and the finger lands on the same place that
does the seeing — the pointing and the organ and the verb
fold into one. Paired against [h2ews-a](h2ews-a.md) which
takes in sound; what the one catches in light the other
catches in breath-noise.
