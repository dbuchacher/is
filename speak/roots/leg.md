# leg

category: 1k

PIE: *leg-
Gloss: to collect / speak
Descendants: logic, lecture, -logy, Latin *legere*, Greek *legein*

## Definition

To speak IS to gather-and-set-forth in PIE: the hand picks
berries off the bush one by one, the mouth picks words out of
[men](men.md) one by one and sets them in a row the ear can
follow. `leg` is the collecting-speech — the telling that has
been put-in-order, the gathering that is itself the saying.
Latin *legere* keeps both halves: to gather grain, to pick out
letters, to read. Where [wekw](wekw.md) is the bare pushing-out
of voice and [bheh2](bheh2.md) is the shining-forth, `leg` is
the careful laying-one-after-another — the stem under logic
(what-gathers-rightly) and under every -logy (a speaking-that-
collects what there is to know about a thing).
