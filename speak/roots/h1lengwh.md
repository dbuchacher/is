# h1lengwh

category: 1m

PIE: *h₁lengʷʰ-
Gloss: light (weight)
Descendants: light, lung, Latin *levis*

## Definition

Light-in-the-hand — the quality of a thing that lifts easy,
that the carrying ([bher](bher.md)) of does not bend the back,
that the wind ([h2weh1](h2weh1.md)) can move. `h1lengwh` names
the dry leaf ([leyp](leyp.md)) that drifts down slow, the bird
([h2ewis](h2ewis.md)) the child holds in cupped hands, the
empty skin-bag, the lung pulled fresh from a kill that floats
on [h2ekweh2](h2ekweh2.md) (this is where the lung-naming
comes from — the light inside-thing). A `h1lengwh` thing rises
rather than sinks, goes fast rather than slow, climbs a
[bheregh](bheregh.md) place without trouble. It is the opposite-
pole of [peyH](peyH.md) (fat-thick-heavy): what `h1lengwh`
lifts, what [peyH](peyH.md) drags down. Note: this is light-as-
weightlessness, not [leuk](leuk.md) (light-as-brightness) —
two different qualities, one English word later.
