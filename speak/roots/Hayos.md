# Hayos

category: 3b

PIE: *(H)áyos
Gloss: copper / bronze / ore
Descendants: ore, era, Latin *aes* (bronze)

## Definition

The base working-metal of the era — the reddish-brown heavy stuff
[h1eg](h1eg.md) pulls out of ore-rich [dheghom](dheghom.md), smelts
over [peh2wr](peh2wr.md), and beats into axe-heads, spear-points,
pots, and the blades that cut the [deru](deru.md) for
[kwekwlos](kwekwlos.md) and the wagon-frame. Where
[h2eusom](h2eusom.md) is the shining dawn-toned rare metal kept
for token and adornment, `Hayos` is the workaday one the whole
craft of the age rests on: the stuff the smith [dheh1](dheh1.md)-s
daily into tool and blade. The root carries both the ore
([wes](wes.md)-ing in the ground) and the worked metal — one word
because the stuff of the stone and the stuff of the tool are one
substance, only cooked. Latin *aes* keeps *bronze*; descendants
*ore* and *era* split outward — *era* because the age itself was
named for the metal that shaped it. The base on which
[kwekwlos](kwekwlos.md) and every cart are built: without `Hayos`
blades, no shaped wood.
