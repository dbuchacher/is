# verb-2sg-si

category: 4c  | morpheme: verb-ending

PIE: *-si
Gloss: verb 2sg present
Descendants: Greek *-si*, Sanskrit *-si*

## Definition

Appends to a verb-stem and marks you-are-doing-this-now — binds
the addressee-pole ([tu](tu.md)) and the present-pole
([h1es](h1es.md)) into the verb's tail. Where a stem names the
bare act, stem + verb-2sg-si carries both the who-addressed and
the when: you-now-do. Sanskrit *asi* "you are" and *dadāsi*
"you give" ride this hinge; Greek *-si* carries it forward.
Sits as the addressee-pole mirror of [verb-1sg-mi](verb-1sg-mi.md)
in the person-present row.

## Composes-with examples

- [h1es](h1es.md) stem + verb-2sg-si → *asi* "you are"
- [deh3](deh3.md) stem + verb-2sg-si → *dadāsi* "you give"
- [bher](bher.md) stem + verb-2sg-si → "you bear"
