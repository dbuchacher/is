# verb-2sg-s

category: 4c  | morpheme: verb-ending

PIE: *-s
Gloss: verb 2sg past
Descendants: Latin *-s* (amabas), English *-st* (thou wast)

## Definition

Appends to a verb-stem and marks you-did-this-before — binds
the addressee-pole ([tu](tu.md)) and the staying-past pole
([wes](wes.md)) into the verb's tail. Where stem + verb-2sg-si
carries you-now-do, stem + verb-2sg-s carries you-did. English
*thou wast* keeps the ending alive into early modern speech;
Latin *-s* (amabas "you loved"), Sanskrit aorist *-s* ride the
same hinge. Completes the 2sg pair (si / s) as the addressee's
minimal now/past swing.

## Composes-with examples

- [wes](wes.md) stem + verb-2sg-s → *wast* "thou wast"
- love-root + verb-2sg-s → Latin *amabas* "you were loving"
- [bher](bher.md) stem + verb-2sg-s → "you bore"
