# klew

category: 1j

PIE: *ḱlew-
Gloss: to hear
Descendants: listen, loud, Latin *cluere*, Greek *kluō* (Clio)

## Definition

To catch the loud sound that carries far — the ear-stroke of
[h2ews-b](h2ews-b.md) pulled toward the side of what rings out
strong enough to reach many at once. `klew` is the hearing that
gives "loud" and "listen": the shout from across the camp, the
name a [suHnus](suHnus.md) carries after his [ph2ter](ph2ter.md)
has [mer](mer.md)-ed, the tale that travels mouth to mouth until
every ear in the tribe holds it. Where [h2ews-b](h2ews-b.md) is
the bare catching of any breath-shaped sound, `klew` is the
catching that lasts — what the [men](men.md) keeps because it was
loud enough or worth enough to keep.
