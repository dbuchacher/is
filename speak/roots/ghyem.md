# ghyem

category: 1e

PIE: *ǵʰyem-
Gloss: winter
Descendants: hibernate, Latin *hiems*, Greek *kheimōn*

## Definition

The cold season — the long stretch when [soh2wl](soh2wl.md) rides
low and weak, when [snoygwh](snoygwh.md) lies on the ground,
when [deru](deru.md)-s have dropped their [leyp](leyp.md)-s and
stand bare, when the [gel](gel.md) settles into the body and the
fire must burn through every [nokwts](nokwts.md). `ghyem` is the
time the camp must have stored food; the time the small beasts
[legh](legh.md) deep in the ground and do not stir; the time the
hunter goes out in fur and returns fast. The speaker counts the
years by how many `ghyem`-s have passed — one full turn of cold
is one year lived. It is the test-season: what the hunter did in
the warm months either keeps her alive through `ghyem` or does
not. Paired against [wesr](wesr.md) as the closed half of the
year against the opening half.
