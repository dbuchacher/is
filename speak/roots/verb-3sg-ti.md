# verb-3sg-ti

category: 4c  | morpheme: verb-ending

PIE: *-ti
Gloss: verb 3sg present
Descendants: Latin *-t*, Greek *-ti* (esti), Sanskrit *-ti* (asti), English *-s* (he runs)

## Definition

Appends to a verb-stem and marks that-one-is-doing-this-now —
binds the distal pole ([h1e](h1e.md) / [so](so.md)) and the
present-pole ([h1es](h1es.md)) into the verb's tail. Where the
first-person endings point at the speaker and the second-
person at the addressee, the third-person ending points away
from both, at the body the talk is about. Greek *esti*
"he/she/it is" and Sanskrit *asti* both ride this hinge; Latin
*-t* drops the final vowel but keeps the pivot; English he-
runs has the fossil *-s* as its descendant. The third slot of
the present row, completing the person-triangle.

## Composes-with examples

- [h1es](h1es.md) stem + verb-3sg-ti → *esti* "he/she/it is"
- [deh3](deh3.md) stem + verb-3sg-ti → *dadāti* "he gives"
- [bher](bher.md) stem + verb-3sg-ti → *bhereti* "he bears"
