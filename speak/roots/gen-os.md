# gen-os

category: 4c  | morpheme: case-ending

PIE: *-os / *-es / *-s
Gloss: genitive singular
Descendants: Latin *-is*, Greek *-os*, English *'s*

## Definition

Appends to a noun-stem and marks the whose-is-it slot — the
body that the thing belongs-with. Where a stem names wolf,
stem + gen-os names "of-the-wolf": the wolf whose tooth,
whose track, whose pack. The suffix is the grammar's way of
binding two nouns so one holds the other — the belonging-edge
that [ke](ke.md), [h1e](h1e.md), and [so](so.md) already mark
at gesture-scale now carried by a word-tail. English *'s*
(wolf's tooth), Latin *-is* (lupi), Greek *-os* all ride this
hinge. Sits in the four-case grid as the whose-pole,
complementing subject, target, and recipient.
