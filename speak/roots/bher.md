# bher

**seed** | category: 1h

PIE: *bʰer-
Gloss: to carry / bear
Descendants: **bear**, **burden**, **fertile**, Latin *ferre*, Greek *pherein*

## Definition

Grounds in pointing at a body moving across [dheghom](dheghom.md) while
holding a thing: [h1eg](h1eg.md) lifts the wood in [mr-gh](mr-gh.md)
and walks it from the forest to the hearth. The root binds two moves
into one: the thing is held (not released as in [deh3](deh3.md), not
set down as in [dheh1](dheh1.md)) while the body goes ([h1ey](h1ey.md))
from one spot to another. A woman `bher`-s a child in her womb across
the months; a hunter `bher`-s meat across the plain; a river `bher`-s
silt across its bed. The motion is carrying-through — the thing
arrives where the carrier arrives. By extension the root names the
bringing-forth of offspring: the womb carries, then delivers.
