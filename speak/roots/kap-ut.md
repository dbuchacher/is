# kap-ut

category: 1d

PIE: *kap-ut-
Gloss: head
Descendants: head, captain, chapter, Latin *caput*

## Definition

The top-knob of [krep](krep.md) where the mouth sits and the
eyes look out — the part [h1eg](h1eg.md) points at when saying
"here I am," the part that rises highest when the body
[steh2](steh2.md)-s. `kap-ut` names the head as the leading
end of the walking body: what goes first through the door,
what [h1ey](h1ey.md) ahead, what turns to face what
[wekw](wekw.md)-s. The one who walks at the front of the band
is the head of the band because the head of the body is what
leads; the later split into "captain" and "chapter" holds this
same pointing — the first-part, the going-first-part. Sits on
the neck [h2en](h2en.md) the shoulders, carries
[h3okw](h3okw.md), [h2ews-a](h2ews-a.md), [nas](nas.md),
[dnghuh2](dnghuh2.md), and [h1dont](h1dont.md) as its crew.
