# acc-m

category: 4c  | morpheme: case-ending

PIE: *-m
Gloss: accusative singular
Descendants: Latin *-um*, Greek *-on*, Sanskrit *-am*

## Definition

Appends to a noun-stem and marks the who-is-acted-on slot — the
body that takes the verb's stroke. Where a subject-marker
tags the doer, acc-m marks the done-to: the target of the
carrying, the placing, the giving. When [h1eg](h1eg.md)
[bher](bher.md)-s the meat, the meat-word takes acc-m to show
it is what the carrying lands on. Latin *-um* (lupum "wolf-
as-target"), Greek *-on*, Sanskrit *-am* all hold the same
target-marker. Sits as the target-pole of the four-case grid —
subject and target, the two bare edges of the verb's aim.

## Composes-with examples

- [wlkwos](wlkwos.md) stem + acc-m → *wlkwom* "the wolf (as
  what the verb hits)"
- [h3regs](h3regs.md) stem + acc-m → *h3rēǵm* "the king (as
  target)"
- [h1nomn](h1nomn.md) stem + acc-m → "the name (as what one
  speaks)"
