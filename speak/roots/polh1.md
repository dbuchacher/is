# polh1

category: 1m

PIE: *polh₁- / *polu-
Gloss: much / many
Descendants: poly-, plenty, Latin *plus*, Greek *polus*

## Definition

Many-in-count — the quality the speaker points at when the things
before [h1eg](h1eg.md) are too many for quick counting: a herd
that fills the plain, the [h2ster](h2ster.md)-s scattered across
[nokwts](nokwts.md)-sky, the leaves of a [deru](deru.md) in summer,
the hairs on a head. Where [meg](meg.md) names one-thing-that-is-big,
`polh1` names many-things-together, the pile past what
[kwetwores](kwetwores.md) and [penkwe](penkwe.md) can reach. Past
[penkwe](penkwe.md) the speaker stops counting one-by-one and
just says `polh1`: the hand-count fails and the bulk-sense takes
over. The fish in the shoal, the drops in the rain, the grains
that pour from the fist.
