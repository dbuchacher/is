# dnghuh2

category: 1d

PIE: *dń̥ǵʰuh₂
Gloss: tongue
Descendants: tongue, Latin *lingua*, Sanskrit *jihvā*

## Definition

The wet moving flesh [h1en](h1en.md) the mouth — the part of
[krep](krep.md) that shapes breath into the sound [wekw](wekw.md)
carries. Without `dnghuh2` no word comes out; the tongue
writes what the ear ([h2ews-a](h2ews-a.md)) will read. It
also tastes: the same organ that speaks is the one that learns
whether food is [sweh2d-u](sweh2d-u.md) or bitter before it goes
down. `dnghuh2` names the write-organ paired against the
read-organs [h3okw](h3okw.md) and [h2ews-a](h2ews-a.md): where
those take in, this one puts out. Sits between the two rows of
[h1dont](h1dont.md) and moves against them to make the
breath-shapes.
