# roth2o

category: 3a

PIE: *rot-h₂o-
Gloss: wheel (different word)

Descendants: Latin *rota* (rotate, round), German *Rad*

## Definition

A second PIE word for the same made-round body that
[kwekwlos](kwekwlos.md) names — one tribe said *kʷékʷlos*, another
said `roth2o`, both pointing at the disc of [deru](deru.md) that
turns on [h2eks](h2eks.md) under the [wegh](wegh.md). Two
words, one thing: the split shows that different PIE-speaking
bands met the wheel on different sides and named it each their
own way, and later tongues kept one or the other. Latin kept
`roth2o` as *rota* — giving *rotate*, *round*, *rotunda* — while
Greek and Sanskrit kept the doubled [kwel](kwel.md)-form. German
*Rad* descends here too. `roth2o` carries the same stroke as
[kwekwlos](kwekwlos.md): the turning made solid, [kwel](kwel.md)
held in wood. The polysemy runs outward in the daughter tongues
(where *rotate* and *cycle* end up feeling like two strokes), but
at the PIE layer they are two names for one built thing.
