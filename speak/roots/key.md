# key

category: 1i

PIE: *ḱey-
Gloss: to lie at rest
Descendants: cemetery (Greek *keimai*)

## Definition

To be laid down and staying laid — the body that [legh](legh.md)-s
and then [wes](wes.md)-es in that posture, not about to rise.
Where [legh](legh.md) names the act of going-down-flat (a hunter
dropping behind a ridge, a sleeper taking the bed), `key` names
the held state of already-being-down — the [wes](wes.md)-ing
that comes after [legh](legh.md)-ing. Sibling of [swep](swep.md)
(sleeping-lying) and [mer](mer.md) (dead-lying); Greek *keimai*
"I lie" gives *koimētērion* the place where bodies `key` — the
cemetery, the resting-place of the dead. The root's edge is the
held posture, not the going-into-it.
