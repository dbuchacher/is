# dwoh1

category: 1b

PIE: *dwóh₁
Gloss: two
Descendants: two, Latin *duo*, Greek *duo*, German *zwei*

## Definition

The pair counted after [oynos](oynos.md): [h1eg](h1eg.md) points
at [ke](ke.md), then at a second [ke](ke.md) standing beside it,
and the two spots hold together without merging. Where
[dwis](dwis.md) names the splitting motion — one torn apart —
`dwoh1` names the standing-result: two heres side by side. The
second pace of the count-line, the first place where
[sem](sem.md) and its dividing already show at once: the pair
holds as a pair only because the split already happened. Sibling
of [dwis](dwis.md) on the number-side: dwis- tears, dwoh1- counts
what tearing left.
