# tis-sis

category: 4b  | morpheme: suffix

PIE: *-tis / *-sis
Gloss: act-as-event noun suffix
Descendants: -sion, -tion, Greek *-sis*, analysis, thesis

## Definition

Appends to a verb-root and names the act itself held as a
standing word — not the doer, not the tool, not the made-piece,
but the doing-as-event. Where [dheh1](dheh1.md) names the
placing, `dheh1` + tis-sis names "the placing" — Greek *thesis*,
the setting-down held still so the speakers can talk about it.
Where [gneh3](gneh3.md) names the knowing, `gneh3` + tis-sis
names "the knowing" — *gnōsis*. The suffix freezes the verb's
flow into something a sentence can carry as its subject: the
act looked at from outside, the happening named as a held
sound-shape. Fourth pole of the nominalizing set next to
[ter-tor](ter-tor.md), [trom-tlom](trom-tlom.md), and
[mn](mn.md).

## Composes-with examples

- [dheh1](dheh1.md) + tis-sis → *thesis* "the act of placing,
  the setting-down"
- [gneh3](gneh3.md) + tis-sis → *gnōsis* "the knowing as a
  held word"
- [bher](bher.md) + tis-sis → "the carrying as event"
