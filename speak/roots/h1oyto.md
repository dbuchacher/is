# h1oyto

category: 3e

PIE: *h₁oyto- / *yewes-
Gloss: oath

Descendants: (Oath words are complex; some IE branches formed them independently)

## Definition

The sworn-binding — a word [wekw](wekw.md)-ed that now holds the
speaker to a thing as firmly as [seh2kro](seh2kro.md) holds a bound
stone apart. `h1oyto` is the stroke by which a man puts his own
body and his own [kred](kred.md) under the law: he stands before
[peh2wr](peh2wr.md) or before [dyews](dyews.md) or with hand on the
[seh2kro](seh2kro.md) thing, and he [wekw](wekw.md)-s the word,
and from that moment the word holds him. If he breaks it,
[h1yewos](h1yewos.md) demands his body back. The binding joins
two strands: [wekw](wekw.md) (the speaking) and the bound-off
state of [seh2kro](seh2kro.md) (the word-now-made-un-ordinary).
The daughter tongues built their oath-words many different ways
(Germanic *aiþ*, Latin *ius-iurandum*, Greek *horkos*), each
tribe marking the binding-stroke in its own way — but the stroke
itself is one: a spoken word that now holds.
