# h2erg

category: 1m

PIE: *h₂erǵ-
Gloss: shining white / silver
Descendants: argent, Latin *argentum*, Greek *arguros*

## Definition

The bright flash-white of a surface that throws [leuk](leuk.md)
back sharp, not the soft pale of [bhel](bhel.md) or the heavy
white of [snoygwh](snoygwh.md) settled on the field. `h2erg`
names the flicker of a fish's side turning in [h2ekweh2](h2ekweh2.md),
the glint off a rain-wet leaf ([leyp](leyp.md)) when
[soh2wl](soh2wl.md) catches it, the cold dazzle of
[snoygwh](snoygwh.md)-crust under low morning light. Where
[bhel](bhel.md) is the held pale (a surface that is white),
`h2erg` is the quick bright (a surface that flashes). Kin to
[leuk](leuk.md) — both carry the giving of sight — but `h2erg`
is specifically the sharp, silvered flash, the brightness that
moves. The eye catches it, loses it, catches it again. The
Bronze-Age smith who beats out silver beside [h2eusom](h2eusom.md)
and [Hayos](Hayos.md) names the metal from the same flash: the
cold-pale stuff IS a piece of that sharp brightness held solid,
and Latin *argentum* / Greek *arguros* descend from the same
root pointing at the same shine. The metal and the flash are
one act — the metal IS named from the flashing. Pairs with
[h2eusom](h2eusom.md): two metals, two lights — the dawn-warm
and the moon-cold.
