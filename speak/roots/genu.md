# genu

category: 1d

PIE: *ǵénu-
Gloss: knee
Descendants: knee, Latin *genu*, Greek *gonu*

## Definition

The hinge in the middle of the leg — the joint that folds so
[pods](pods.md) can lift off [dheghom](dheghom.md) and swing
forward, and that bends so [krep](krep.md) can drop into
[sed](sed.md) or [legh](legh.md). `genu` names the hard
swelling a speaker feels when reaching down, the one place
in the long bone where the leg breaks to let walking happen.
Without knees the body could only [steh2](steh2.md) stiff;
with them it can [h1ey](h1ey.md), climb, and rise. Also the
place where the child kneels before
[ph2ter](ph2ter.md) — the body-posture of yielding, because
to bend the knee is to stop walking and hand over going-power.
