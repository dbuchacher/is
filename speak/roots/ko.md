# ko

category: 4b  | morpheme: suffix

PIE: *-ko-
Gloss: diminutive suffix
Descendants: -k, -ch (Germanic)

## Definition

Appends to a noun-root and marks a small-version of the stem's
thing — the little-one, the young-one, the lesser-of-its-kind.
Where a root names a body, root + ko names the little body of
the same shape: the pup, the cub, the small-thing. The suffix
is the grammar's way of shrinking: the speaker holds the full
thing and bends the word toward its smaller sibling. Germanic
-k, -ch, later Latin and Romance -culus and -chen ride this
pivot forward. Sits cousin to the adjectival suffixes but
tilts toward size-marking rather than quality-marking.

## Composes-with examples

- [h2wiH-ro](h2wiH-ro.md) + ko → "little man, young fellow"
- [wlkwos](wlkwos.md) + ko → "wolf-pup, little wolf"
- horse-root + ko → "horse-foal, little horse"
