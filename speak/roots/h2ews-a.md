# h2ews-a

category: 1d

PIE: *h₂éws-
Gloss: ear
Descendants: ear, Latin *auris*, Greek *ous*

## Definition

The folded opening on the side of [kap-ut](kap-ut.md) through
which [h2ews-b](h2ews-b.md) happens — the part of
[krep](krep.md) that catches the breath-shaped noise a
speaker [wekw](wekw.md)-s and passes it inward for
[men](men.md) to hold. `h2ews-a` shares its very sound-shape
with the verb [h2ews-b](h2ews-b.md) because the organ and the
act are one thing to the Paleolithic speaker: the ear IS the
hearing, the hole in the head-side IS where listening lives.
Shut both ears and [h2ews-b](h2ews-b.md) stops. Paired with
[h3okw](h3okw.md) as the second read-organ; the two stand on
opposite sides of the head and take in two different kinds of
news.
