# nokwts

category: 1e

PIE: *nókʷts
Gloss: night
Descendants: night, Latin *nox/noctis*, Greek *nuks*

## Definition

When the [soh2wl](soh2wl.md) has gone under the far edge and the
[dyews](dyews.md) goes dark — the stretch of time in which
[h1eg](h1eg.md) sees only the fire close at hand and the
[h2ster](h2ster.md)-s far overhead. `nokwts` is the time the eye
fails and the ear takes over: the [weid](weid.md)-ing shrinks
toward the fire-circle and [h2ews-b](h2ews-b.md) grows to catch
what moves beyond the light. The beasts that hunt in dark now
[h1ey](h1ey.md), and the speaker [legh](legh.md)-s close to
others under a roof or in a cave. It is the dark pole of the
turning [kwel](kwel.md) that [soh2wl](soh2wl.md) walks: night is
not the absence of day, night is the time that [h1es](h1es.md)
when day [h1es](h1es.md) not. Paired against [dyews](dyews.md) as
one turn paired against the next.
