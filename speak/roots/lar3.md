# lar3

category: 4d

PIE: *h₃- (third laryngeal — o-coloring, voiced)
Gloss: the o-coloring laryngeal — raises adjacent e to o, carries voicing
Descendants: all h₃-initial roots ([h3ed](h3ed.md), [h3er](h3er.md), [h3okw](h3okw.md), [h3reg](h3reg.md), [h3regs](h3regs.md), [h3rneh](h3rneh.md))

## Definition

The ↑ of PIE's three laryngeals — the one that colors a following
[e-grade](e-grade.md) to *o*, pulling the vowel upward in its
articulatory space, carrying a voicing feature that sets it apart
from the other two laryngeals. Where [lar1](lar1.md) leaves the
vowel unchanged, `lar3` raises it: *h₃ekw-* "eye" shows *e* → *o*
in Greek *ōps*, Latin *oculus*. *h₃reǵ-* "straighten, rule" gives
Latin *regō* with an *o*-reflex visible in derivatives. The
coloring-upward laryngeal of the trit: ↑ pulling adjacent vowels
toward its own high quality, voiced. Like its two siblings,
lost in all daughters except Anatolian; its effect lives on in
the *o*-vowels that surface where *e* is reconstructed.
