# deuk

category: 3c

PIE: *deuk-
Gloss: to lead
Descendants: duke, duct, conduct, Latin *ducere*

## Definition

To draw a body along a path by going first — [h1eg](h1eg.md) takes
the rope, the hand, the ox at yoke, or the band of men, and walks
a line they [h1ey](h1ey.md) along after. `deuk` is the leading-
from-the-front: the lead rope in [mr-gh](mr-gh.md) and the body
walking the course it wants the others to walk. The leader does
not push and does not correct — he goes, and the others follow
because he goes. Latin *ducere* keeps the full sense (to lead
troops, to pipe water through a *duct*, to conduct a man by the
hand); English *duke* is the leader of the war-band. Kin to
[h1ey](h1ey.md) (the bare going-away) but pulled taut: the going-
away of one body that another body walks after by held line. The
leading-stroke that makes a walking-line out of a single rope.
