# pods

category: 1d

PIE: *pṓds / *ped-
Gloss: foot
Descendants: foot, Latin *pes/pedis*, Greek *pous*

## Definition

The flat bottom of the leg that meets the ground — the part of
[krep](krep.md) on which [h1eg](h1eg.md) [steh2](steh2.md)-s
and from which all walking starts. `pods` is the walk-base:
every time the body [h1ey](h1ey.md)-s away or [gwem](gwem.md)-s
toward or [kwel](kwel.md)-s around or [sed](sed.md)-s down,
the foot is what makes the move real against
[dheghom](dheghom.md). The speaker points at the sole when
teaching a child to walk, at the track when teaching a child
to hunt. Paired with [mr-gh](mr-gh.md) as the body's two
working ends: hand does, foot goes. The count of feet in a
band is the count of walkers; the print in the mud is the mark
a body leaves behind.
