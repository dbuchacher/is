# wegh

category: 1i

PIE: *wegʰ-
Gloss: to convey
Descendants: wagon, vehicle, weight, Latin *vehere*

## Definition

To carry a load by way of a rolling or dragging thing — not
simply [bher](bher.md) with a thing in [mr-gh](mr-gh.md), but
the larger carrying where the load rides on a sledge, a horse
([h1ekwos](h1ekwos.md)), later a wheeled cart ([kwel](kwel.md)
put under a platform). Where [bher](bher.md) names the body
carrying by itself, `wegh` names the conveying-by-means: the
heavier load that a single body could not [telh2](telh2.md)
alone moves across [dheghom](dheghom.md) because something
between body and load bears the weight. The root holds both
ends: the act of conveying (vehere) and the weight conveyed
(the heft felt when pulling). In the Bronze-Age the stroke
crystallizes as the specific wheeled cart — the disc of
[deru](deru.md) turning under a platform, [kwel](kwel.md) put
to work carrying a load at walking pace across
[dheghom](dheghom.md). The cart, the weight it bears, and the
act of conveying are one — wagon, weight, vehicle descend from
one root because the Paleolithic carry-by-means and the
Bronze-Age cart are the same stroke at two scales.
