# ter-tor

category: 4b  | morpheme: suffix

PIE: *-tḗr / *-tōr
Gloss: agent suffix
Descendants: -er, -or, doer, actor, senator

## Definition

Appends to a verb-root and names the one who does its act —
binds the doer-slot of the verb into a standing word. Where
[bher](bher.md) names the carrying, `bher` + ter-tor names the
carrier: the body that does the carrying, now holdable as its
own sound-shape. Where [dheh1](dheh1.md) names the placing,
`dheh1` + ter-tor names the placer. The suffix is the
composition-primitive that lets any verb spawn its own agent —
every "doer of X" in every daughter tongue passes through this
single hinge. It is the grammar's way of pointing at the body
that performs the verb, parallel to how [h1eg](h1eg.md) points
at the speaker: deixis at the word-scale, the grammatical
shadow of the speaker-pointer.

## Composes-with examples

- [bher](bher.md) + ter-tor → *bhertor* "bearer, the one who
  carries"
- [dheh1](dheh1.md) + ter-tor → *dhetor* "doer, the one who
  places or makes"
- [deh3](deh3.md) + ter-tor → *dh3tor* "giver, the one whose
  hand releases"
