# isto

category: 4b  | morpheme: suffix

PIE: *-isto-
Gloss: superlative suffix
Descendants: -est (biggest), Latin *-issimus*

## Definition

Appends to an adjective-root and marks the far end of the
scale — the one standing past all the others along the same
line. Where a one-notch-up tilt marks a comparative step, isto
takes the tilt all the way: root + isto names the body that
sits at the top of the whole heap. English -est (biggest,
tallest, oldest), Latin *-issimus*, Greek *-istos*. The
suffix is the grammar's way of doing the out-of-all gesture:
the speaker sweeps across the set and stops at the pole-body.
Sits on the scale's far edge — the furthest-up pole against
the one-step-up tilt.

## Composes-with examples

- big-root + isto → *biggest* "the one past all the bigs"
- old-root + isto → *oldest*
- tall-root + isto → *tallest*
