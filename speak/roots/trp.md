# trp

category: 1l

PIE: *tr̥p-
Gloss: to delight
Descendants: Latin *trepidus*, Greek *terpō*

## Definition

To be filled-full with gladness at something good — the
brimming-over the [kred](kred.md) does when the hunt comes home,
when the child laughs, when [soh2wl](soh2wl.md) breaks the long
[nokwts](nokwts.md). `trp` names the glad-quivering of the whole
self, the pleasure that makes the body almost shake with it —
Latin *trepidus* keeps the trembling edge, Greek *terpō* keeps
the filling-with-joy. Where [preyH](preyH.md) is the glad-love
between two and [leubh](leubh.md) is the caring-love that wills,
`trp` is the bare delight — the body's own yes at good news,
before any word for it.
