# nos

category: 4b  | morpheme: suffix

PIE: *-nos
Gloss: passive participle suffix
Descendants: -en (fallen, broken), -ne

## Definition

Appends to a verb-root and marks the state of having-been-done-
to by something outside — the thing is not the doer but the
done-to. Where a break-root like PIE *bhreg-* runs as an act,
that stem + nos names "broken" — the thing that took the break
and now holds it. Where [tos-to](tos-to.md) marks done-ness
plain, nos tilts the same done-edge toward the patient: the
pole that received the act rather than performed it. English
*fallen*, *broken*, *taken* ride this suffix; Germanic -en
carries it forward. Sits mirror to [ter-tor](ter-tor.md): the
agent-suffix names the one who does, nos names the one who
was-done-to.

## Composes-with examples

- [bher](bher.md) + nos → "borne-upon, the one carried"
- [dheh1](dheh1.md) + nos → "placed-upon, the one set down"
- fall-root + nos → *fallen* (the one who took the fall)
