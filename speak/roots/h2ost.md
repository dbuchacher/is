# h2ost

category: 1d

PIE: *h₂ost-
Gloss: bone
Descendants: osseous, Latin *os/ossis*, Greek *osteon*

## Definition

The hard white inside-frame of [krep](krep.md) — what holds
the body up when the flesh is soft, what stays after meat and
skin are gone. `h2ost` is what a speaker feels pressing
against the skin at elbow and knee, what shows through when a
beast is fresh-killed and butchered. The hunter reads tracks
by bone-shape in the skull of an old kill, the child learns
that under the soft arm there is the hard thing that will not
bend. Kin to [ker-a](ker-a.md) (where the bone rises as horn
or skull-ridge) and to [h1dont](h1dont.md) (tooth as one kind
of bone). Bone is the body's [deru](deru.md): the firm inner
wood that does not give.
