# ker-a

category: 1d

PIE: *ḱer- / *ḱerh₂-
Gloss: head / horn
Descendants: horn, hart, cerebrum, Latin *cornu*, Greek *keras, kara*

## Definition

The hard-jutting top of the head — the [kap-ut](kap-ut.md) seen
as the place where bone rises out, where the stag's antler
climbs [h2en](h2en.md) into the sky and where the ram's horn
curls forward. `ker-a` names the head in its hardness: the
skull-cap under the skin, the bone that survives after the
flesh goes, and in the horned beast the spike that juts out
and points where the head points. A hunter sees the stag's
`ker-a` first above the brush, a child feels the hard ridge
above the [bhruH](bhruH.md), and both pointings share one
word. This is the head as [h2ost](h2ost.md) made visible —
where [kap-ut](kap-ut.md) names the leading-end of
[krep](krep.md), `ker-a` names the hard crown that caps it.
