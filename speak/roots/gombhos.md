# gombhos

category: 1d

PIE: *ǵómbʰos
Gloss: tooth / peg
Descendants: comb, Sanskrit *jambha*

## Definition

The tooth seen as a peg in a row — [h1dont](h1dont.md) named
for its shape rather than its cutting-work. Where
[h1dont](h1dont.md) points at the biter, `gombhos` points at
the peg-form itself: the single hard spike set beside others
in a line, which is why the same root later makes English
"comb" (a row of pegs shaped like a row of teeth). A speaker
uses `gombhos` when the tooth is being thought of as a
stand-alone piece — one peg pulled from the mouth, one tusk
of a beast, one carved pin in a tool-row. Same piece of
[krep](krep.md) as [h1dont](h1dont.md), different pointing.
