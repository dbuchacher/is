# h2eiw

category: 3f

PIE: *h₂eiw- / *h₂óyu-
Gloss: life / age / eternity
Descendants: ever, aye, aeon, Latin *aevum*, Greek *aiōn*

## Definition

The full span a [h2wiH-ro](h2wiH-ro.md) lives from first
[newos](newos.md)-breath to last [mer](mer.md) — and by stretching
the same word, the span beyond any one life: the time that holds
all lives together. `h2eiw` is the life-arc itself: the arc that
runs from [bhuH](bhuH.md) through [h2yuh1en](h2yuh1en.md) through
the holding-middle of [h1es](h1es.md) into the [wes](wes.md) of
[senos](senos.md) and finally out. The root then widens: what
holds across many lifetimes is also `h2eiw` — Latin *aevum*
"age, eternity," Greek *aiōn* "age, epoch, eternity," English
*ever* and *aye* ("for all time"). The same root carries "a life"
and "for all time" because in PIE seeing the longest span a
speaker can [men](men.md) is built by stretching lifetime-after-
lifetime in a line. The longest span: the long run through which
many season-cycles and many counted years roll — the arc at whose
ends stand the first [newos](newos.md)-breath of one life and the
last [mer](mer.md) of the last life the folk remember.
