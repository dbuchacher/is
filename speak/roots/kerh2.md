# kerh2

category: 1e

PIE: *kerh₂-
Gloss: heat
Descendants: carbon, Latin *cremare*

## Definition

Heat strong enough to change what it touches — the
[peh2wr](peh2wr.md)'s work on meat, on wood, on bone. Where
[gwher](gwher.md) is warm that the body welcomes,
[tep](tep.md) is warm that is mild, and [gel](gel.md) is
cold — `kerh2` is heat as consumer: the temperature at which flesh
cooks, wood blackens, stone cracks, water boils off. It is what
[h1ngwnis](h1ngwnis.md) does to what it touches. The speaker
meets it when the hand comes too close to the hearth, when the
cooking stone glows, when the body is scorched. The fire at its
hot edge, the heat that transforms — food becomes food through
`kerh2`, wood becomes charcoal through `kerh2`, bodies burn
through `kerh2`. Not the comfort-warm but the biting-warm, the
heat that forces a change in what it enters.
