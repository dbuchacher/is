# h2eg

category: 3c

PIE: *h₂eǵ-
Gloss: to drive / lead
Descendants: act, agent, agile, Latin *agere*, Greek *agō*

## Definition

To drive a beast or a body forward by pushing it from behind —
[h1eg](h1eg.md) with a stick in hand walking the herd down the
track, the shout and the prod keeping them moving where the
speaker wants. `h2eg` is the driving-from-behind: the one who
stands at the rear and puts the herd into motion. Latin *agere*
keeps the whole width of it — to drive cattle, to do, to act, to
make a thing happen — because every doing is a driving of some
body toward some end. From this stem comes "act" (a stroke that
moves a body), "agent" (one who drives), "agile" (quick to be
driven by one's own will). Kin to [dheh1](dheh1.md): where
[dheh1](dheh1.md) places a thing at rest, `h2eg` keeps a thing in
motion. The acting-stroke of the PIE verb — pairs with
[h3er](h3er.md)'s rising-start as its onward-push cousin.
