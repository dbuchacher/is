# mn

category: 4b  | morpheme: suffix

PIE: *-mn̥
Gloss: result / thing-done suffix
Descendants: -men, -ma, -min, Latin *nomen*, Greek *-ma* (pragma)

## Definition

Appends to a verb-root and names the thing that stands after
the act is done — the made-piece, the left-behind, the
product. Where [dheh1](dheh1.md) names the placing-act, a
`dheh1` + mn is the placed-thing, what the placing leaves
holding. Where [wekw](wekw.md) names the speaking-out, `wekw` +
mn would be the spoken-piece. The seed-word
[h1nomn](h1nomn.md) itself is built on this shape: the sound-
shape that the tongue puts forth, the product of naming.
Greek *pragma* (thing-done) and Latin *nomen* (thing-named)
ride the same hinge. Sits next to [ter-tor](ter-tor.md)
(doer) and [trom-tlom](trom-tlom.md) (means) as the result-
pole of the same triad.

## Composes-with examples

- [h1nomn](h1nomn.md) already carries this shape: the
  naming-act's named-piece
- [dheh1](dheh1.md) + mn → "the placed thing, the made piece"
- [bher](bher.md) + mn → "the borne thing, the burden"
