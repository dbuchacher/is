# lar2

category: 4d

PIE: *h₂- (second laryngeal — a-coloring)
Gloss: the a-coloring laryngeal — lowers adjacent e to a
Descendants: all h₂-initial roots ([h2eg](h2eg.md), [h2el](h2el.md), [h2enh1](h2enh1.md), [h2erg](h2erg.md), [h2ster](h2ster.md), [h2ewsos](h2ewsos.md))

## Definition

The ↓ of PIE's three laryngeals — the one that colors a following
[e-grade](e-grade.md) to *a*, pulling the vowel downward in its
articulatory space. Where [lar1](lar1.md) leaves the vowel
unchanged, `lar2` lowers it: *h₂entiom* "front" shows *e* → *a*
before descendants split (Latin *ante*, Greek *anti*). *h₂ster-*
"star" gives Greek *astēr* with the *a* rather than *e*. The
coloring-downward laryngeal of the trit: ↓ pulling adjacent vowels
toward its own low quality, leaving a residue of *a*-timbre in
every descendant. Lost as a consonant in all languages but
Anatolian; its effect lives on in the *a*-vowels that appear
where *e* is reconstructed.
