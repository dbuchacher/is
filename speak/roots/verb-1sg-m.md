# verb-1sg-m

category: 4c  | morpheme: verb-ending

PIE: *-m
Gloss: verb 1sg past
Descendants: Latin *-m* (eram), Sanskrit *-m*

## Definition

Appends to a verb-stem and marks I-did-this-before — binds the
speaker-pole ([h1eg](h1eg.md)) and the staying-past pole
([wes](wes.md)) into the verb's tail. Where stem + verb-1sg-mi
carries I-now-do, stem + verb-1sg-m carries I-did: the act
that [wes](wes.md)-es behind the speaker. Latin *eram* "I was"
is built from [h1es](h1es.md)'s stem plus this ending; Sanskrit
aorist *-am* and imperfect *-m* ride the same hinge. The pair
(mi / m) sits as the grammar's minimal swing between now and
past at the first-person pole.

## Composes-with examples

- [h1es](h1es.md) stem + verb-1sg-m → *eram* "I was"
- [bher](bher.md) stem + verb-1sg-m → "I bore"
- [deh3](deh3.md) stem + verb-1sg-m → "I gave"
