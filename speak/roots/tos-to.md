# tos-to

category: 4b  | morpheme: suffix

PIE: *-tos / *-to-
Gloss: past participle suffix
Descendants: -ed, -t, Latin *-tus*, -ate

## Definition

Appends to a verb-root and marks the done-ness of the act — the
state that holds once the verb has run its course. Where
[dheh1](dheh1.md) names the placing, `dheh1` + tos-to names
"placed, having-been-put." Where [bher](bher.md) names the
carrying, `bher` + tos-to names "borne, having-been-carried."
The suffix pins the verb to its aftermath: the act is done and
now the thing [wes](wes.md)-es in its new state. That pin is
why English -ed ties directly to [wes](wes.md)'s staying-past
— both mark the down-edge where a fresh move has settled into a
held condition. Latin *-tus*, Sanskrit *-ta*, Greek *-tos* all
carry the same done-and-holding.

## Composes-with examples

- [dheh1](dheh1.md) + tos-to → Latin *factus* "done, made"
- [bher](bher.md) + tos-to → "borne, carried"
- [gneh3](gneh3.md) + tos-to → *gnōtos* "known"
