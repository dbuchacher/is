# iko

category: 4b  | morpheme: suffix

PIE: *-iko-
Gloss: adjectival suffix
Descendants: -ic (logic), Latin *-icus*

## Definition

Appends to a noun-root and marks pertaining-to — the word built
this way names the quality of belonging-with the stem's thing.
Where a root names music, root + iko names musical; where a
root names logos, root + iko names logic. The suffix is the
grammar's way of turning a thing into a quality adjacent to
it: the speaker holds the named-thing and bends the word-shape
so it becomes the tilt or flavor that stems from it. Greek
*-ikos* and Latin *-icus* ride this single pivot. Sits next to
a harder material-suffix which marks "made-of" belonging
rather than the softer pertaining-to.

## Composes-with examples

- music-root + iko → *musical*
- logos-root + iko → *logic*
- poet-root + iko → *poetic*
