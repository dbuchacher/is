# teuteh2

category: 3c

PIE: *teutéh₂
Gloss: people / tribe
Descendants: Deutsch, Teuton, Tuatha (Irish)

## Definition

The named-together folk — the whole body of kin and in-kin bound
under one [h1nomn](h1nomn.md), who speak one tongue
([dnghuh2](dnghuh2.md)) and share one set of [h2wiH-ro](h2wiH-ro.md)-s
to hunt and fight with. Where [h1lewdh](h1lewdh.md) names the
grown free one at self-scope, `teuteh2` names the many-as-one at
tribe-scope: the gathering of all those who answer when the
[kal](kal.md) goes up, all those whose [genh1](genh1.md)-lines run
back to a shared stem. Gives Germanic *Deutsch* (the folk of one
tongue), Latin *Teutoni* (the tribe-name turned exonym), Irish
*tuath* and *Tuatha* (the Tuatha Dé Danann — the folk of the
goddess). A `teuteh2` is not a place and not any line of rulers —
it is the body of folk who hold [sem](sem.md) against all other
bodies. [sem](sem.md) writ at the scale of a people.
