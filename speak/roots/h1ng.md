# h1ng

category: 1l

PIE: *h₁n̥ǵ-
Gloss: narrow / anxious
Descendants: anger, anguish, anxiety, Latin *angustus*

## Definition

The feeling of being squeezed narrow — the throat tight, the
chest held, the breath of [h2enh1](h2enh1.md) not coming full.
`h1ng` names the constriction that comes when danger is not yet
acute enough for outward flight but will not let the body rest:
the held-in fear, the anger that cannot fire, the anxious
waiting in the dark while the [h1engwi](h1engwi.md) is somewhere
near. Latin *angustus* is the narrow pass, the tight strait;
"anguish" and "anxiety" keep the inner narrowing whole. Pairs
with the outward-startle at the other pole of warning — where
that one sends the body out, `h1ng` clamps the body in.
