# wesr

category: 1e

PIE: *wésr̥
Gloss: spring
Descendants: Latin *ver*, Greek *ear*

## Definition

The opening season — when [ghyem](ghyem.md) breaks, when the
[snoygwh](snoygwh.md) melts back to [wodr](wodr.md), when the
[soh2wl](soh2wl.md) rises higher each day and [dheghom](dheghom.md)
begins to warm. `wesr` is the time green pushes up out of the
softening ground, the [deru](deru.md)-s put out new
[leyp](leyp.md)-s, the birds return, the she-beasts bring forth
young. The body that has held through `ghyem` can now loosen: the
hunter goes out longer, the camp moves, the stored food at last
gives way to fresh. It is the relieving turn — the moment after
long cold when the speaker knows the bad season has ended.
Paired against [ghyem](ghyem.md) as the opening half of the year
against the closed half. The root carries no overlap with
[wes](wes.md) (the seed meaning stay/dwell) despite the surface
resemblance — this is its own pointing: the loosening-of-cold,
the green returning.
