# peh2wr

category: 1e

PIE: *péh₂wr̥
Gloss: fire
Descendants: fire, pyre, Greek *pur*

## Definition

The hot bright thing that [h1es](h1es.md) in the hearth — fire as
a standing body, the one you sit around, the one the speaker can
point at and name. `peh2wr` is fire-as-thing: the flame-pile that
[steh2](steh2.md)-s on the hearth-stones, that throws
[leuk](leuk.md) across the faces ringed around it, that gives
[gwher](gwher.md) for cold hands to come near. Where
[h1ngwnis](h1ngwnis.md) names the active edge — the biting,
igniting, eating — `peh2wr` names the settled body: the fire
that holds, the fire you feed, the pile of coals that stays warm
through [nokwts](nokwts.md). What [h1eg](h1eg.md) watches, what
the camp gathers around, what the cook lays meat against. Two
roots for fire because fire is met two ways: the still body that
warms you, and the live-edge that burns.
