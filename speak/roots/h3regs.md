# h3regs

category: 3c

PIE: *h₃rḗǵs
Gloss: king / straightener
Descendants: rex, regal, royal, reich, raj, Latin *rex*, Sanskrit *rājan*

## Definition

The one who does the [h3reg](h3reg.md) stroke for the whole
[teuteh2](teuteh2.md) — the man whose [h1nomn](h1nomn.md) the folk
speak when they mean "the straightener." `h3regs` is not a ruler
of any sort but specifically the [h2wiH-ro](h2wiH-ro.md) whose
work is to keep the tribe in line: to settle the crooked case, to
[leg](leg.md) the gathered folk into order, to [wekw](wekw.md) the
judgment that holds. The speakers recognize him — [gneh3](gneh3.md)
and [genh1](genh1.md) show through in the king-word, because the
recognized one is the king-born, and the king-line is the line
whose [genh1](genh1.md) the folk have taken in. Latin *rex*,
Sanskrit *rājan*, Gaulish *rīx*, Irish *rí* — all descend from
this single man-naming. German *Reich* widens the same stem to the
realm under him. Where [h3reg](h3reg.md) names the stroke,
`h3regs` names the stroke-bearer: the one whose body is the body
the folk turn to when the line bends.
