# kap

category: 1h

PIE: *kap-
Gloss: to grasp
Descendants: have, captive, catch, Latin *capere*

## Definition

The bare grasping-power of [mr-gh](mr-gh.md) itself — what the
hand can do when it closes, before any choice of which thing to
grab and before any plan of what to do after. Where
[ghabh](ghabh.md) is the keeping-after-grasp, `kap` is the
hand's own capability: to be a grasper at all. [h1eg](h1eg.md)
`kap`-s means [h1eg](h1eg.md) has the hand-power to take hold.
From this the descendants split: the thing-taken is captive; the
power-to-take widens into capable; the holder becomes
the head of a thing (caput in Latin, via [kap-ut](kap-ut.md)'s
cousin-stem). Bare-power shade of the polysemy headed by
[ghabh](ghabh.md).
