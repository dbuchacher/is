# ghwer

category: 1f

PIE: *ǵʰwer-
Gloss: wild beast
Descendants: feral, Latin *ferus*, Greek *thēr*

## Definition

The kind of living thing that does not [wes](wes.md) near the
fire — a breathing body that lives out in the trees and the high
grass, beyond where [h1eg](h1eg.md) and [tu](tu.md) sleep.
`ghwer` names any beast met [h2epo](h2epo.md) from the camp, one
that [h1ey](h1ey.md)-s when sighted or turns on the hunter — not
the dog at the hearth, not the sheep one will later lead, but the
beast that still keeps its own ways. It is the general pointing
for the whole kin of big-toothed hunters, four-footed runners,
winged fliers, legless creepers, and small burrow-dwellers: what
they share is being out-there and unbent by human hand.
