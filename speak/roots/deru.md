# deru

category: 1g

PIE: *deru- / *dóru-
Gloss: tree / wood / firm
Descendants: **tree**, **true**, **trust**, **durable**, Latin *durus*, Greek *drus* (oak)

## Definition

The great standing thing and the hardness it holds, named as one
— a tree [steh2](steh2.md)-s without a body, upright out of
[dheghom](dheghom.md) for longer than any [h1eg](h1eg.md) lives,
and its wood stays hard through blade, through fire that
[h2ed](h2ed.md)-s the outside, through years of rain. `deru` is
this unity: the tree, the wood cut from it, and the firmness
that makes the wood trustable when it becomes a spear-haft or a
roof-beam. What you can lean on, what does not bend, what will
[h1es](h1es.md) there tomorrow as it does today — all one word,
because to the Paleolithic speaker they are one substance. The
later splits into English "true" and "trust" and Latin *durus*
(hard) are not metaphors from tree; they are the same pointing
kept: what holds like wood holds is `deru`, and a saying that
holds like wood holds is a `deru` saying. The firmness is not a
likeness of the tree — the firmness IS the tree-stuff wherever
it shows up. Sister to [steh2](steh2.md) (the posture of
holding-upright); cousin to [wes](wes.md) (staying-through-time).
