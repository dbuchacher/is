# dheh1s

category: 3e

PIE: *dʰeh₁-s-
Gloss: sacred / religious
Descendants: Greek *theos*? (disputed)

## Definition

The rightly-done stroke at rite-scope — the same write-side
[dheh1](dheh1.md) verb (put, do, make) lifted to name the
religious doing. Where [dheh1](dheh1.md) names any placing-of-a-
thing-so-it-holds, `dheh1s` narrows to the sacred doing: the rite
laid down just-so on the stone, the offering put in the fire at
the right moment, the prayer-word spoken in the right form. A
`dheh1s` act is one that has been done as the held rite demands
— the stroke that keeps [seh2kro](seh2kro.md) bound where it must
stay bound. The root is one ritual gesture: not any doing, but the
doing that ties folk to [deiwos](deiwos.md). Greek *theos* has
been argued (disputedly) to descend here — "the god" as "the
one-done-to-rightly." The elevation of [dheh1](dheh1.md)'s commit-
edge from hearth-stroke to altar-stroke, one verb stretching from
the everyday placing of a stone to the rite-placing of an offering.
