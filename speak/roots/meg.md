# meg

category: 1m

PIE: *meǵ(h₂)-
Gloss: great
Descendants: magnum, mega, much, Latin *magnus*, Greek *megas*

## Definition

Great-in-size — the quality the speaker points at when a thing is
too wide, too tall, too heavy for the hand to take hold of at once.
`meg` names the bigness of the [deru](deru.md) whose trunk the arms
cannot circle, of the [h2rtkos](h2rtkos.md) that rears up bigger
than any [h2wiH-ro](h2wiH-ro.md), of the cloud-bank ([megh](megh.md))
that swallows the whole sky. It is the size-pole against which the
small-boned and the just-grown are measured — one-thing-that-is-big,
not many-things-together. What [h1eg](h1eg.md) must step back from
to [weid](weid.md) whole, what the arm of [h2wiH-ro](h2wiH-ro.md)
cannot carry ([bher](bher.md)) in one pass.
