# leyp

category: 1g

PIE: *léyp-
Gloss: leaf
Descendants: leaf, Greek *lepis* (flake)

## Definition

The flat thin green thing the [deru](deru.md) puts out along its
branches — what catches light ([leuk](leuk.md)) and what falls
in heaps on [dheghom](dheghom.md) when the cold months
([ghyem](ghyem.md)) are near. A `leyp` is light
([h1lengwh](h1lengwh.md)) enough that the wind
([h2weh1](h2weh1.md)) turns it over and carries it; thin enough
that the hand closes around many at once. Named from the peeling
apart — something that splits off the tree in flat pieces, like
the flake of stone the knapper strikes, like the scale of a
cold-water beast. The tree's outer skin, renewed every year,
gathered at the hunter's feet when the cold comes.
