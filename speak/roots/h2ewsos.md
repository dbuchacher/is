# h2ewsos

category: 1e

PIE: *h₂éwsōs
Gloss: dawn
Descendants: east, Easter, aurora, Latin *aurora*, Greek *ēōs*

## Definition

The reddening edge of [dyews](dyews.md) that opens before the
[soh2wl](soh2wl.md) climbs over it — the turn from
[nokwts](nokwts.md) back to the lit day, seen first as a thin
pale line along the far [dheghom](dheghom.md), then as
[h1rewdh](h1rewdh.md)-coloring all along that side of the sky,
then as the first sliver of the bright round one rising. `h2ewsos`
is this specific time: not the full day, not the black of night,
but the gap in which the world comes back to the eye piece by
piece — first the shapes of near [deru](deru.md)-s out of the
gray, then color, then the [leuk](leuk.md) that sharpens every
edge. The [h2ster](h2ster.md)-s fade one by one as she opens the
door. Named by the side of the sky the opening happens on, the
side from which [soh2wl](soh2wl.md) enters.
