# e-grade

category: 4d

PIE: *-e- (basic / full vowel grade)
Gloss: the unmarked, stable-form vowel grade of a PIE root
Descendants: base forms of verbs (bher-, men-, weid-), present-tense stems in Sanskrit / Greek / Latin

## Definition

The stable ↕ of PIE's vowel apparatus — the default middle grade
that a verb-root carries in its unmarked shape, neither shifted up
into a derived form nor reduced down into a weakening. Where a PIE
root shows `e-grade`, the root is in its base form: [bher](bher.md)
carries the bare *bher-*, [men](men.md) carries the bare *men-*,
[weid](weid.md) carries the bare *weid-*. This is the phonological
trit's middle value — the vowel in its un-committed state, the
shape the root is [h1es](h1es.md) when nothing has shifted it.
Every e/o/zero alternation starts from `e-grade` as the home
position; the other two grades are departures from this middle.
