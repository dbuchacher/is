# h1esh2r

category: 1d

PIE: *h₁ésh₂r̥
Gloss: blood
Descendants: Greek *ear*, Hittite *ešḫar*

## Definition

The red wet that runs [h1en](h1en.md) the body — what pours
out when skin is cut, what runs through the whole of
[krep](krep.md) without stopping while [gweyh3](gweyh3.md)
holds. `h1esh2r` names blood as the inner-flow: unseen under
the skin, seen only when the body is opened by wound or kill.
The hunter knows the track of a struck beast by the red trail;
the speaker knows blood means life is leaving. A child of the
same [meh2ter](meh2ter.md) shares `h1esh2r` — the kin-tie is
carried in this word before any other naming of kind takes
hold.
