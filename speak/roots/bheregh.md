# bheregh

category: 1m

PIE: *bʰereǵ̑ʰ-
Gloss: high
Descendants: burg, burrow, Latin *fortis*

## Definition

Up-far from the ground — the quality of a thing that
[steh2](steh2.md)-s well [uper](uper.md) what lies on
[dheghom](dheghom.md). `bheregh` names the crag the hunter
climbs to watch the valley, the crown of the old
[deru](deru.md) where the eagle ([h3rneh](h3rneh.md)) nests,
the cliff the river has cut under, the hilltop where the
watchers sit to [weid](weid.md) far. A `bheregh` place reaches
up — it takes climbing to come to, and from it the eye sees
what the low places hide. The beast and the man that stand on
a `bheregh` stand safe: harder to reach from below. From this
comes the fortified high-place, the hill-hold, the strong seat.
Pair with [h1lengwh](h1lengwh.md) (light-bodied): a `bheregh`
place is reached more easily by what is [h1lengwh](h1lengwh.md)
than by what is [peyH](peyH.md).
