# nom-s

category: 4c  | morpheme: case-ending

PIE: *-s
Gloss: nominative singular
Descendants: Latin *-us/-is*, Greek *-os*, English genitive *'s*

## Definition

Appends to a noun-stem and marks the who-is-acting slot — the
body that drives the verb. Where a stem names wolf, stem +
nom-s names *wlkwos* as the subject: the wolf-that-does. The
suffix is the grammar's pointer at the agent-pole of the
clause, the shadow at word-scale of what [h1eg](h1eg.md) does
at utterance-scale: both mark who-does-the-doing, but nom-s
marks it inside the sentence's grid rather than by gesture
outside it. Every daughter tongue keeps a trace: Latin *-us*
and *-is*, Greek *-os*, Sanskrit *-s*. The who-slot of the
four-case grid sits here, opposite [acc-m](acc-m.md).

## Composes-with examples

- [wlkwos](wlkwos.md) stem + nom-s → *wlkwos* "the wolf (doing)"
- [h3regs](h3regs.md) stem + nom-s → *h3rēǵs* "the king (acting)"
- [h1eg](h1eg.md) already carries this pointer at utterance-scale
