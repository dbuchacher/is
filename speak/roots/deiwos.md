# deiwos

category: 3d

PIE: *deiwós
Gloss: god (from sky)
Descendants: divine, deity, Tuesday (Tiw-), Latin *deus*, Sanskrit *deva*

## Definition

A shining one of the [dyews](dyews.md) kin — the god-sense lifted
out of the sky-god [dyews](dyews.md) and made the word for any
bright-up-being. Where [dyews](dyews.md) is specifically the lit
dome and its speaker-facing face, `deiwos` is the wider class: any
of the shining ones that hold [h2en](h2en.md) over the folk, any
of the bright beings the tribe calls on. Latin
*deus*, Sanskrit *deva*, Old Norse *Tīwaz* (giving English
Tuesday), Latvian *dievs* — all descend here, all pointing at the
same shining-up-being. The stem is the [dyews](dyews.md)-stem
widened: god-as-sky yields god-as-any-bright-one. A speaker does
not see a `deiwos` the way she sees the [soh2wl](soh2wl.md); she
[gneh3](gneh3.md)-s it by the lit-aboveness it shares with
[dyews](dyews.md). The later split into "a god" (a specific bright
one) and "the divine" (the bright-above quality) is one pointing
kept. The ritual-call and the ritual-plea are the speaker's
ways of reaching toward a `deiwos`.
