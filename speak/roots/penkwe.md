# penkwe

category: 1b

PIE: *pénkʷe
Gloss: five
Descendants: five, Latin *quinque*, Greek *pente*, finger

## Definition

The full [mr-gh](mr-gh.md): five fingers held up, the count that
closes one hand. [h1eg](h1eg.md) lays [kwetwores](kwetwores.md)
plus [oynos](oynos.md) side by side and the open palm stands as
the tally itself — the body wearing its own count. After `penkwe`
the count crosses to the other [mr-gh](mr-gh.md) and begins
again, which is why so many later tongues carry fives at the
joints of their number-lines. The root binds the full-hand-shape
and the count-of-five into one word: the finger is the fiver.
