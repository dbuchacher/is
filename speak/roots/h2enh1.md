# h2enh1

category: 1e

PIE: *h₂enh₁-
Gloss: breathe
Descendants: animate, Latin *anima*, Greek *anemos*

## Definition

The air moving in and out of the chest — and the living-soul that
does the moving. One root for both, because in PIE to breathe IS
to be alive and the breath IS the self-in-body. When a body stops
`h2enh1`-ing, the body becomes [dheghom](dheghom.md) again; the
breath-going-out-for-the-last-time is what marks the crossing
from alive to dead. So the root holds the whole of it: the
in-breath, the out-breath, the rhythm that runs all night under
[legh](legh.md)-ing sleep, and the being-alive that those
breaths constitute. Not "breath" plus "soul" as two things joined
by metaphor — one thing, pointed at one way. The wind-outside
([h2weh1](h2weh1.md)) and the breath-inside are the same moving
air met at the skin-boundary: one carries the smoke off, one
carries the body on. When the speaker says the breath has left
this one, the whole is said.
