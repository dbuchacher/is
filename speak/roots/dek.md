# dek

category: 1m

PIE: *deḱ-
Gloss: proper / fitting
Descendants: decent, Latin *decere*

## Definition

Fitting-into-place — the quality a thing shows when it sits
right where it belongs, when its shape matches the space made
for it. `dek` names the spear-haft that slides clean into the
worked socket, the stone that sits square in the wall without
rocking, the meat-cut that falls where the joint parts, the word
[wekw](wekw.md)-ed that lands the way the hearers expected.
Where a thing is forced, broken, or leaves a gap, it is not
`dek`; where it [steh2](steh2.md)-s as if made for that place,
it is. The speaker reads `dek`-ness in the work of a skilled
hand: the knot that holds, the path that does not wander, the
gift [deh3](deh3.md)-ed at the right turning. From this comes
the later sense of what is owed, what is right, what suits.
