# ter-s

category: 1e

PIE: *ter-s-
Gloss: dry / thirst
Descendants: thirst, terrain?, Latin *torrere* (torrid)

## Definition

Dry — and the thirst a dry body feels — one root, because the
speaker's body reads its own inside-dryness the same way it reads
a dry riverbed: the absence of [wodr](wodr.md). `ter-s` is the
ground cracked open after long [kerh2](kerh2.md), the mouth and
tongue ([dnghuh2](dnghuh2.md)) that have gone without drinking,
the skin of meat cured in sun and wind ([h2weh1](h2weh1.md)), the
stretch of land with no spring, no stream, no pool. When the body
`ter-s`, it must find [wodr](wodr.md) before the [soh2wl](soh2wl.md)
sets again — a few days without drinking and the body fails.
Where [h2ed](h2ed.md) is the burning-that-dries (active, by
fire), `ter-s` is the state of being dry — the condition, felt in
the throat as thirst, seen in the land as parched ground.
