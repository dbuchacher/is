# h1nomn

category: 1d

PIE: *h₁nomn̥
Gloss: name
Descendants: name, Latin *nomen*, Greek *onoma*

## Definition

The sound-shape that [dnghuh2](dnghuh2.md) puts out to call a
thing — the breath-pattern a speaker [wekw](wekw.md)-s and
every [h2ews-a](h2ews-a.md) in the band learns to catch. `h1nomn`
is not letters and not a written mark — it is the spoken shape
itself, held between mouth and ear. A child has one, a mountain
has one, a [wlkwos](wlkwos.md) that circles the camp often
enough earns one. To know a thing's `h1nomn` is to be able to
summon it into the talk: say the name and every [men](men.md)
in the band turns toward that thing. Counted among the
body-parts because in the Paleolithic reckoning it lives with
the tongue and mouth that make it — the name-saying is as much
the speaker's body as the name-thing itself. Paired with
[dnghuh2](dnghuh2.md): tongue is the tool, name is what the
tool puts forth.
