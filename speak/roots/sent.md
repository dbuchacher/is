# sent

category: 1j

PIE: *sent-
Gloss: to sense / feel
Descendants: sense, Latin *sentire*

## Definition

To take something in through any of the body's openings at
once — the whole-body reading that sits under
[weid](weid.md), [h2ews-b](h2ews-b.md), [h3ed](h3ed.md),
[gusH](gusH.md), and the skin's own catching of warm and cold.
`sent` names the feeling-through, the way the body knows
[kerh2](kerh2.md) on the palm or [gel](gel.md) on the cheek
without needing words for it. What the senses give,
[men](men.md) gathers and weighs; but `sent` is the raw catching
before [men](men.md) turns it. The root sits at the gate where
outside becomes inside-felt — where the body, not yet the
thinker, already has the news.
