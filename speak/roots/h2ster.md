# h2ster

category: 1e

PIE: *h₂stḗr
Gloss: star
Descendants: star, asterisk, Latin *stella*, Greek *astēr*

## Definition

One of the many small bright points that [steh2](steh2.md) in
[dyews](dyews.md) at [nokwts](nokwts.md) — each one far, each one
fixed, none walking the way [soh2wl](soh2wl.md) or
[meh1ns](meh1ns.md) walk. `h2ster` is what the eye catches when
the dark has settled and the fire is low: pinpricks held against
the dark-dome, turning slowly all together so that the hunter who
watches long enough sees the whole field shift, but no single
point leaves its neighbors. The speaker names them in clusters —
the seven that hang together, the one bright at the top of the
dark — and reads from them when the cold is coming, when to
cross, which way the camp should face. They give almost no
[leuk](leuk.md), but they give the fixed marks by which
[h1eg](h1eg.md) keeps bearing under a sky that would otherwise
be featureless.
