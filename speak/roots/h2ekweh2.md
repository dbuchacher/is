## Definition

The living water ‚ÄĒ the kind that runs, the kind that gathers into
pools the hunter crosses and drinks from, the kind that
[kwel](kwel.md)-s over stones in a streambed. `h2ekweh2` names
water as what moves across [dheghom](dheghom.md): it finds the
low line, it pours, it floods, it carves the ground over many
turnings of [soh2wl](soh2wl.md). Where [wodr](wodr.md) names the
same substance caught in a vessel, held in the hand, poured to
the mouth, `h2ekweh2` names it loose on the land ‚ÄĒ the river, the
spring, the rain running in channels. Two roots for one substance
because the speaker meets it in two modes: the cupped portion you
drink, and the flowing body you ford. Drink the same thing you
wade in; name it twice because the meeting is twice.
