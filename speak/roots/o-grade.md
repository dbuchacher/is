# o-grade

category: 4d

PIE: *-o- (shifted / derived vowel grade)
Gloss: the shifted-upward vowel grade marking derivation, causative, and perfect tense
Descendants: phóros (bearer, from *bher-), gónos (offspring, from *ǵen-), perfect stems in Greek and Sanskrit

## Definition

The ↑ of PIE's vowel apparatus — the shift-upward that marks a
derived form, a perfect tense, an agent-noun, or a causative. Where
[e-grade](e-grade.md) is the stable middle of a root, `o-grade` is
the same root shifted up into a new shape: [bher](bher.md) in
*bher-* gives its o-shift *bhor-*, surfacing as Greek *phóros* (the
carrying-one, the bearer) and Sanskrit *bhāra-* (the burden, the
load-carried). [genh1](genh1.md) in *gen-* gives *gon-* surfacing
as Greek *gónos* (offspring). The root is not changed; the vowel
is committed upward, marking this shift as [h1ey](h1ey.md)-ing
where the base form is not. The write-side of the vowel trit: one
grade up from [e-grade](e-grade.md), the shift that marks a root
as doing derived work.
