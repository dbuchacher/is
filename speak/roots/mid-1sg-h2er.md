# mid-1sg-h2er

category: 4c  | morpheme: verb-ending

PIE: *-h₂er
Gloss: middle voice 1sg
Descendants: Hittite *-ḫari*, Latin *-r* (amor)

## Definition

Appends to a verb-stem and marks I-do-this-to-myself — binds
the speaker-pole ([h1eg](h1eg.md)) with the bend-back pointer
([se](se.md)) so the act loops from doer back to doer. Where
[verb-1sg-mi](verb-1sg-mi.md) aims the act outward from the
speaker, mid-1sg-h2er folds the act's aim back onto the
speaker's own body: the middle voice. Hittite *-ḫari* keeps
the shape clearly; Latin *-r* in *amor* "I am loved / I love
myself" is the eroded descendant. The ending carries the
reflexive grammar at the first-person pole.

## Composes-with examples

- wash-root + mid-1sg-h2er → "I wash myself"
- [weid](weid.md) stem + mid-1sg-h2er → "I see myself"
- love-root + mid-1sg-h2er → Latin *amor* "I am loved / love
  for myself"
