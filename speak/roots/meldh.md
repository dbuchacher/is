# meldh

category: 3d

PIE: *meldʰ-
Gloss: to pray / call on
Descendants: Slavic *modliti*, English *meld*?

## Definition

To speak words of plea or petition to a [deiwos](deiwos.md) — the
ritual-soft voice rather than the shout. Where [kal](kal.md) is
the gathering-shout at folk-scope, `meldh` is the pleading: hands
lifted, body bent, words asking. The speaker stands before the
bound stone or [peh2wr](peh2wr.md) on the hearth and
[wekw](wekw.md)-s the asking-words — for rain, for the sick one's
breath to come back, for the hunt to find beast. Slavic *modliti
sę* (to pray) keeps the whole stroke; the trace in Germanic is
fainter. Kin to [bheh2](bheh2.md) but lifted to the god-side:
where [bheh2](bheh2.md) shines-forth-words between folk, `meldh`
sends them up. The asking-voice among the ritual-speech kin,
softer than [kal](kal.md)-shout, bent-bodied rather than lifted-
breasted.
