# senos

category: 1m

PIE: *senos
Gloss: old
Descendants: senior, Latin *senex*, Greek *henos*

## Definition

Long-has-been — what has [wes](wes.md)-ed through many turnings
of [soh2wl](soh2wl.md) and [meh1ns](meh1ns.md) and now carries
the marks of that staying. `senos` names the [h2wiH-ro](h2wiH-ro.md)
whose hair has gone the white of [bhel](bhel.md), whose back
bends, whose [h1dont](h1dont.md)-s have loosened; the
[deru](deru.md) whose bark is thick and grey and whose crown has
split in storms; the worn stone-edge of a well-used tool. The
`senos` thing was once [newos](newos.md) and passed through
[h2yuh1en](h2yuh1en.md) and now [h1es](h1es.md)-es near the
leaving-edge, where [mer](mer.md) begins to draw close. What the
speaker hears the long-held story from; what the young sit near
to learn.
