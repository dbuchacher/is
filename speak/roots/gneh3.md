# gneh3

**seed** | category: 1j

PIE: *ǵneh₃- / *ǵenh₃-
Gloss: to know / beget
Descendants: know, can, ken, cognize, recognize, gnosis, beget, genesis, genus, gene, kin, king, Latin *noscere*, Greek *gignōskō*

## Definition

To know and to beget as one verb: the speaker takes something
in through [weid](weid.md) and [men](men.md), and in the same
stroke puts something out — [dheh1](dheh1.md) of a child, a
name, a fresh-made thing. What comes in through the eye comes
back out the hand or the loins; the inward [gwem](gwem.md) and
the outward [h1ey](h1ey.md) meet at self and make one act.
Knowing IS begetting in PIE; later tongues split the two halves
(gno- to know, gen- to beget) but here they are the single turn
of [kwel](kwel.md) where taking-in and giving-out coincide.
