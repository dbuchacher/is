# mr-gh

category: 1d

PIE: *mr̥-gʰ- / *man-
Gloss: hand
Descendants: manual, Latin *manus*

## Definition

The five-fingered end of the arm — the part of [krep](krep.md)
that does [dheh1](dheh1.md), [bher](bher.md), and
[deh3](deh3.md). The hand grasps the stone, the spear, the
child; it is the write-tool of the body, the one through which
what [men](men.md) thinks becomes what the world holds.
`mr-gh` points at the open palm and the gripping fingers as
one unit, the shape that closes around a thing to carry it
and opens to release it. Paired with [pods](pods.md) as the
body's two working ends: foot walks, hand works. The
same root also gives Paleolithic speakers the word for the
whole doing-person — hand as stand-in for the one who acts.
