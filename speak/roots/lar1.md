# lar1

category: 4d

PIE: *h₁- (first laryngeal)
Gloss: the neutral laryngeal — leaves adjacent e unchanged, preserves vowel quality
Descendants: all h₁-initial roots ([h1es](h1es.md), [h1ey](h1ey.md), [h1eg](h1eg.md), [h1ed](h1ed.md), [h1regwos](h1regwos.md), [h1rewdh](h1rewdh.md))

## Definition

The ↕ of PIE's three laryngeals — the neutral one that leaves
adjacent [e-grade](e-grade.md) unshifted, preserving the vowel's
quality without pulling it up or down. `lar1` leaves the vowel
alone: *h₁es-* gives Greek *es-ti* "is" with the *e* intact,
*h₁ey-* gives Latin *ire* "go" with the *e* preserved. The unmarked
middle of the three-laryngeal system — the one whose effect on
neighbors is no-effect. Lost in all daughter languages
except Anatolian (Hittite kept clear reflexes, which is how modern
linguistics recovered the three-way system). The ↕ trit-value at
the consonant-coloring layer: [h1es](h1es.md) in its own name,
phonology naming phonology.
