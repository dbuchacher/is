# mid-3sg-to

category: 4c  | morpheme: verb-ending

PIE: *-to / *-or
Gloss: middle voice 3sg
Descendants: Greek *-tai*, Sanskrit *-te*, Latin *-tur*

## Definition

Appends to a verb-stem and marks that-one-does-this-to-
themselves — binds the distal pole ([h1e](h1e.md) /
[so](so.md)) with the bend-back pointer ([se](se.md)) so the
act loops from the third body back onto itself. Where
[verb-3sg-ti](verb-3sg-ti.md) aims the act outward from that-
one, mid-3sg-to folds the aim back. Greek *-tai* (luētai
"he/she looses themselves"), Sanskrit *-te*, Latin *-tur*
(amātur "he is loved / he loves himself") all descend from
this hinge. Completes the middle-voice triangle across the
three person-poles.

## Composes-with examples

- loose-root + mid-3sg-to → Greek *luētai* "he/she looses
  themselves"
- love-root + mid-3sg-to → Latin *amātur* "he is loved"
- wash-root + mid-3sg-to → "he/she washes themselves"
