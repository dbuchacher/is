# bhugh

category: 1i

PIE: *bʰugʰ-
Gloss: to flee / bend
Descendants: bow, bend, Greek *pheugō*

## Definition

The body bends and runs — shoulders curve, knees ([genu](genu.md))
fold, the straight stand of [steh2](steh2.md) breaks as
[h1eg](h1eg.md) turns away from the bear or the spear and the
[pods](pods.md) drive hard away. Where [h1ey](h1ey.md) names
bare going-away and [bhegw](bhegw.md) names the urgent run
itself, `bhugh` holds the bend-and-flee as one stroke: the body
bowed by fear into a curve, running. The bend-shade and the
flee-shade share one root because the fleeing body is the bent
body — the coiled-spring shape of escape. Polysemy-sibling with
[bhegw](bhegw.md), two flee-roots splitting on the edge of
bending-versus-sprinting.
