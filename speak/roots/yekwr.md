# yekwr

category: 1d

PIE: *yḗkʷr̥
Gloss: liver
Descendants: hepatic, Latin *iecur*

## Definition

The big dark soft lump [h1en](h1en.md) the belly under the
ribs — the heaviest inner-piece of [krep](krep.md), the one
the hunter pulls out first when butchering because it spoils
fastest. `yekwr` is what a speaker finds when cutting open a
felled beast: a single great dense organ, warm,
blood-dark, set below [kred](kred.md). To the Paleolithic
eye it is counted alongside the heart as a seat of inner
life — some bands held that it held fate or courage, and the
later Greek and Roman diviners read it. Named for what it is
in the hand after the kill: the bigger lump.
