# ghew

category: 3d

PIE: *ǵʰew-
Gloss: to invoke / call
Descendants: god (from Proto-Germanic *gudan* = "invoked one"?), Sanskrit *hu-*

## Definition

To call a [deiwos](deiwos.md) by [h1nomn](h1nomn.md) and pour or
offer to it — the specifically ritual shout that opens the line
between speaker and shining-one. Where [wekw](wekw.md) is the
ordinary speaking between mouths and [kal](kal.md) is the gathering-
shout that brings folk in, `ghew` is the calling-up: the voice
lifted toward [dyews](dyews.md), the libation poured over the
stone while the name is spoken. Sanskrit *hu-* keeps the pour-and-
call sense; Proto-Germanic *gudan* ("the invoked one" — the one
whom `ghew`-ing is done toward) may lie behind English *god*,
making the Germanic god-word built from the calling-side rather
than (as in Latin *deus* from [deiwos](deiwos.md)) the shining-
side. A third mouth-stroke alongside plain speaking and gathering-
shout: the one pour-and-call that reaches upward.
