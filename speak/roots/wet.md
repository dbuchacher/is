# wet

category: 3f

PIE: *wet-
Gloss: year (as duration)
Descendants: Latin *vetus* (veteran, veterinarian), Greek *etos*

## Definition

The year counted as held-time — not the cycling turn of
[yeh1r](yeh1r.md) but the lived-through span itself: a piece of
[h2eiw](h2eiw.md) now standing behind the speaker as done. Where
[yeh1r](yeh1r.md) names the wheel of seasons returning, `wet` names
the duration that has been [wes](wes.md)-ed through: the one-year-
old calf is a calf who has [wes](wes.md)-ed one full circuit;
*veteranus* in Latin is the man who has [wes](wes.md)-ed many
such circuits. The root leans on the staying-through pole of being
— what has held for a span, what carries the marks of having held.
A `wet` is a unit of duration; a `yeh1r` is a unit of cycle. The
same Bronze-Age speaker names both, from two sides. Gives
"veteran" (many-years-held), *vetus* (old, having-stood-long),
Greek *etos*. Pairs with [senos](senos.md): *senos* names the state
of being old, `wet` names the count of the years that made the
old-state.
