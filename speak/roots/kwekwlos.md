# kwekwlos

category: 3a

PIE: *kʷékʷlos
Gloss: wheel
Descendants: wheel, cycle, Sanskrit *cakra*, Greek *kuklos*

## Definition

The round thing [h1eg](h1eg.md) carves out of [deru](deru.md) that
[kwel](kwel.md)-s under a load — [kwel](kwel.md) doubled on itself
to name not the turning-stroke but the made-round-thing that DOES
the turning. The Bronze-Age smith takes a disc of [deru](deru.md),
bores a hole through its middle, slides a through-stick into it,
and now the heavy load that [bher](bher.md) alone cannot
[telh2](telh2.md) rolls along [dheghom](dheghom.md) because the
disc turns while the load rides still. `kwekwlos` is the turning-
made-solid: the stroke of [kwel](kwel.md) held in wood and baked
into a carried thing. One word for the round body and the cycle it
traces — Sanskrit *cakra* keeps both, Greek *kuklos* keeps both.
Where [kwel](kwel.md) is the bare walking-in-a-ring, `kwekwlos` is
the crafted round-one that lets [wegh](wegh.md) carry more than
the body alone can. The doubling of the stem marks it: this is
the turning that has been made-to-be — the turn given a body.
