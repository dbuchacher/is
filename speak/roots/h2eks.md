# h2eks

category: 3a

PIE: *h₂eḱs- / *h₂eḱs-lo-
Gloss: axle / axis
Descendants: axle, axis, Latin *axis*, Greek *axōn*

## Definition

The through-stick that [per](per.md)-s the hole in
[kwekwlos](kwekwlos.md) and holds its center still while the wood
around it turns — a straight piece of hard [deru](deru.md) threaded
through the round body, pegged at both ends, letting the
[kwekwlos](kwekwlos.md) [kwel](kwel.md) without leaving its spot.
`h2eks` is named from the going-through itself: it is what
[per](per.md)-s the wheel. Two [kwekwlos](kwekwlos.md)-s share one
`h2eks`; the cart-frame sits on two `h2eks`-es; the pair of oxen
pulling in front depends on the stick behind them holding true.
The line `h2eks` holds is what later tongues keep as axis — the
stayed-still line around which a turning happens, the unmoving
middle of every [kwekwlos](kwekwlos.md). When the speaker watches
the cart roll, the rim flickers but `h2eks` seems to hang in the
air: the turning has a fixed line, and that line is this stick.
