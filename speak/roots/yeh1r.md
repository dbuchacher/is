# yeh1r

category: 3f

PIE: *yeh₁r-
Gloss: year / season / cycle
Descendants: year, Latin *hornus*, Greek *hōra* (hour, season)

## Definition

The returning turn of the seasons — the span that begins at one
spring, runs through the heat of [tep](tep.md)-summer, into the
falling leaf, into the [ghyem](ghyem.md) cold, and comes back
around to the next spring. `yeh1r` is the cycle-name: not just one
year-length but the turning itself, the wheel of the seasons
closing. The same word stretches to name the smaller turns inside
it — Greek *hōra* takes on "hour" and "season" both, because both
are spans that come back around. Literally a descendant of the
wheel-stroke at the sky-scale: [kwel](kwel.md) turned in heavens
instead of under a cart, [kwekwlos](kwekwlos.md) traced by the
[soh2wl](soh2wl.md) over the long run. Sits inside [h2eiw](h2eiw.md) — the longer life-arc holds many
yeh1r-cycles in a row. Where [meh1ns](meh1ns.md) is the moon-cycle
span, `yeh1r` is the sun-cycle span — the bigger loop the speaker
counts [meh1ns](meh1ns.md)-es inside.
