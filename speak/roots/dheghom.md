# dheghom

category: 1e

PIE: *dʰéǵʰōm
Gloss: earth
Descendants: humus, chthonic, Latin *humus*, Greek *chthōn*

## Definition

The ground under the foot ([pods](pods.md)) — the substance on
which [h1eg](h1eg.md) [steh2](steh2.md)-s, into which the dead
are laid, from which the plants push up — and at the same time
the stuff the speaker and every other walking body is made of.
One root for both, because in the Paleolithic seeing there is no
gap between the earth you walk on and the earth-stuff you are.
`dheghom` is what bodies come out of and go back into: the
hunter who dies [legh](legh.md)-s down and in a few turnings of
[meh1ns](meh1ns.md) becomes the ground again; the new child
[bhuH](bhuH.md)-s up out of a body that itself came from ground
and eats from plants that push up out of ground. The chain is one
substance turning. To name the earth is to name what the speaker
is — the earth-one, the ground-dweller — so `dheghom` holds both:
the ground beneath, and the being-made-of-ground that walks on
it. The [dyews](dyews.md) is overhead and not `dheghom`; the
water runs across and is not `dheghom`; everything else the body
meets, including the body itself, is some form of `dheghom`.
