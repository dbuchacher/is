# h1yewos

category: 3e

PIE: *h₁i̯ewos
Gloss: law / rite
Descendants: Latin *ius/iuris*, just, jury, justice

## Definition

The binding-principle the folk hold and the ruler speaks — the
body of sworn-and-done ways that ties the folk to each other.
`h1yewos` is not a written code and not the stroke of any one man
— it is the held pattern: what must be done when blood is spilled,
what must be done when a daughter is given in marriage, what must
be done when a man breaks a sworn word. Latin *ius* keeps it;
*iustitia* (justice) is the holding-up of `h1yewos`; *iurare* (to
swear) is the stroke that feeds into it; *iudex* (judge) the one
who speaks what it demands. Where [seh2kro](seh2kro.md) names the
bound-off state and [dheh1s](dheh1s.md) names the rightly-done
stroke, `h1yewos` names the pattern that says which strokes are
true. The tribe's held law, older than any single ruler, carried
by the elders who [h1es](h1es.md) long enough to remember its
shape.
