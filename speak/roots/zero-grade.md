# zero-grade

category: 4d

PIE: *-∅- (reduced / zero vowel grade)
Gloss: the reduced grade where the root vowel drops out entirely
Descendants: past participles (bhr-tós from *bher-), weak stems, reduplicated forms, Sanskrit sampradāna weak forms

## Definition

The ↓ of PIE's vowel apparatus — the reduction-to-nothing that the
vowel undergoes in weak forms, past participles, and certain
derivations. Where [e-grade](e-grade.md) is the stable middle of
a root, `zero-grade` is the shift-down: the vowel drops out
entirely, leaving the consonants of the root pressed together. [bher](bher.md) in *bher-* gives its
zero-grade *bhr-*, surfacing as the past participle *bhr-tós*
(Sanskrit *bhṛ-tá* "borne"). [men](men.md) gives *mn-* in the
weak stem *mn-tós* (thought-of). The root is not destroyed; it is
[wes](wes.md) in reduced shape, still the same root, just pushed
to its lowest grade. The phonological trit's bottom value: ↓ as
absence-where-something-was, the vowel consumed back into the
consonants around it.
