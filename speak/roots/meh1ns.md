# meh1ns

category: 1e

PIE: *mḗh₁n̥s
Gloss: moon / month
Descendants: moon, month, Latin *mensis*, Greek *mēn*

## Definition

The paler round one that [kwel](kwel.md)-s across
[dyews](dyews.md) at [nokwts](nokwts.md), and the counted turning
of days that it marks — one root for both, because to the
Paleolithic speaker the moon IS the month-counter. `meh1ns` grows
from a thin curve through full round and back to nothing, and
that cycle itself — from full-round to full-round — is the span
the speaker counts by. To name the moon is to name the span it
measures; no later division into the thing-in-sky and the
count-of-days, because there is no such split. The hunter watches
the face grow for half the count, shrink for the other half, and
knows when she-beasts will birth, when cold will deepen, when to
move camp. The [soh2wl](soh2wl.md) gives day; `meh1ns` gives the
counting that holds days together into a span [h1eg](h1eg.md) can
hold in [men](men.md).
