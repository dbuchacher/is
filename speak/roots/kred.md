# kred

category: 1d

PIE: *ḱred- / *ḱerd-
Gloss: heart
Descendants: heart, cardiac, Latin *cor/cordis*, Greek *kardia*

## Definition

The beating lump [h1en](h1en.md) the chest — the part of
[krep](krep.md) a speaker feels drumming under the ribs when
running, when afraid, when a loved one comes close. `kred`
names both the pump that moves blood ([h1esh2r](h1esh2r.md))
through the body and the seat where feeling sits: to the
Paleolithic speaker these are not two readings, they are one.
Fear shakes the heart, love warms it, anger hammers it —
every strong move of [men](men.md) is first felt here. It
[h1es](h1es.md) the body's inner drum; when it stops,
[gweyh3](gweyh3.md) leaves. Points at the spot a hand rests
when swearing truth — because what the heart does is what the
speaker means.
