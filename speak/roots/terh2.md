# terh2

category: 1i

PIE: *terh₂-
Gloss: to cross over
Descendants: through, trans-, Latin *trans*

## Definition

To walk from one side of a thing to the other side of it — the
body of [h1eg](h1eg.md) starting in [h1en](h1en.md) on this
side, stepping across the body of water or the stand of trees
or the line of stones, and ending in [h1en](h1en.md) on the far
side. Where [per](per.md) names the straight-line forward-drive
that does not stop at boundaries, `terh2` names the two-sided
crossing: there is a near-side and a far-side and the body has
gone from one to the other. The ford across a river, the pass
across a ridge — a `terh2` happens at a place where a crossing
is made and keeps its shape afterward. Descendants carry this
shade into every trans- word: the far-side reached by going
across.
