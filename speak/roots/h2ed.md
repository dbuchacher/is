# h2ed

category: 1e

PIE: *h₂ed-/-h₂eyd-
Gloss: burn / dry
Descendants: edify?, Greek *aithō*

## Definition

To burn — to be on fire and to be made dry by fire, one act with
one root. When [h1ngwnis](h1ngwnis.md) catches in wood and the
[kerh2](kerh2.md) takes hold, the wood `h2ed`-s: it turns black,
it gives off smoke, it shrinks toward ash. The outside of
[deru](deru.md) `h2ed`-s when fire eats at its surface without
consuming the whole — the scorching, the charring. And the sun's
strong [leuk](leuk.md) on [dheghom](dheghom.md) in the dry season
is `h2ed`-ing too: the ground cracks, the grass browns, the
shallow [h2ekweh2](h2ekweh2.md) disappears. Burn and dry are one
because both name the same thing done by heat — the taking-away
of moisture through fire's action, whether fast (the wood
burning) or slow ([soh2wl](soh2wl.md) beating on the land through
a hot month). The active undergoing of [kerh2](kerh2.md).
