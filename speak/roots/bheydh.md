# bheydh

category: 1l

PIE: *bʰeydʰ-
Gloss: to trust / persuade
Descendants: bide, faith, federate, Latin *fidere*, Greek *peithō*

## Definition

To rest one's weight on another's word — the trusting that says:
what you have [wekw](wekw.md)-ed stands, and I will [wes](wes.md)
on it as on firm ground. `bheydh` is both sides of this: the
trusting and the persuading, because to persuade is to make
another rest on your word, and to be persuaded is to choose to
rest. "Faith" is the settled form — the [men](men.md) at rest on
what cannot be seen; "federate" is the tribe-to-tribe `bheydh`,
where many folks bind themselves by one spoken pledge. "Bide"
keeps the stay-put side: to trust and to stay are one, for the
one who trusts does not flee. Kin to [leubh](leubh.md) — trust
is love's weight-bearing half.
