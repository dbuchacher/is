# h2ewg

category: 1h

PIE: *h₂ewg-
Gloss: to grow / increase
Descendants: augment, eke, Latin *augere*, Greek *auxanō*

## Definition

The growing-by-adding — what a pile does when one more stone is
laid on it, what a herd does when new young come, what a body
does when meat is piled onto bone-frame. Where [ker-b](ker-b.md)
names the pushing-out of new substance from inside and
[h2el](h2el.md) names the feeding-into-full-age, `h2ewg` names
the addition-from-outside: increase by heap, growth by
accumulation. The Paleolithic counting body saw `h2ewg` whenever
more was laid next to what already was — berries to the basket,
days to a life, strokes to a tool-edge. The descendants augment
and eke both keep this shade: to add-onto an existing pile, not
to generate from nothing.
