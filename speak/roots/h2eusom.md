# h2eusom

category: 3b

PIE: *h₂eus-om / *h₂éwso-
Gloss: gold (from "glow/dawn")
Descendants: Latin *aurum* (gold, aureate), English **gold** (via *ǵʰelh₃-*)

## Definition

The shining yellow metal named from the shining itself — the same
stem that gives [h2ewsos](h2ewsos.md) (dawn), here bent to name
the buried stuff that throws back [leuk](leuk.md) the way
[h2ewsos](h2ewsos.md) throws back the first pale sky-color of
morning. `h2eusom` is the soft heavy piece that [h1eg](h1eg.md)
finds in a stream-bed or beats out of ore and that holds the
[h2ewsos](h2ewsos.md)-glow in its face: when [soh2wl](soh2wl.md)
catches it, it gives back that same warm shine the dawn-side gives.
The naming says: this stuff IS a piece of the dawn-glow, taken out
of [dheghom](dheghom.md) and held in hand. Latin *aurum* keeps
the full form; English *gold* comes from a sister-stem ([ghelh3](ghelh3.md))
pointing at the yellow-shine from the other side. The warm-toned
one of the three Bronze-Age metals: the one named from dawn.
