# verb-1sg-mi

category: 4c  | morpheme: verb-ending

PIE: *-mi
Gloss: verb 1sg present
Descendants: Greek *-mi* (eimi), Sanskrit *-mi* (asmi)

## Definition

Appends to a verb-stem and marks I-am-doing-this-now — binds
the speaker-pole ([h1eg](h1eg.md)) and the present-pole
([h1es](h1es.md)) into the verb's tail. Where a stem names the
bare act, stem + verb-1sg-mi carries both the who and the when:
I-now-do. Greek *eimi* "I am" is built from [h1es](h1es.md)'s
own stem plus this very ending, proving the convergence — the
first-person-present ending carries the speaker inside the
copula itself. Sanskrit *asmi* rides the same hinge. Every
later I-present form in the old tongues passes through this
pivot.

## Composes-with examples

- [h1es](h1es.md) stem + verb-1sg-mi → *esmi* "I am"
- [deh3](deh3.md) stem + verb-1sg-mi → *didōmi* "I give"
- [dheh1](dheh1.md) stem + verb-1sg-mi → *tithēmi* "I place"
