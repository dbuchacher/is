# sed

**seed** | category: 1h

PIE: *sed-
Gloss: to sit
Descendants: sit, set, seat, Latin *sedere*, Greek *hedra*

## Definition

Grounds in pointing at a body folded at [genu](genu.md) (knee) with
hindquarters lowered onto [dheghom](dheghom.md) or a stone. The weight
leaves [pods](pods.md) and rests on the base of the body instead.
Where [steh2](steh2.md) stacks the body upright along the
[h2en](h2en.md)/[upo](upo.md) axis, `sed` halves that stack: the
upper body still rises, the lower body folds. A speaker `sed`-s at the
hearth to eat ([h1ed](h1ed.md)), to talk ([wekw](wekw.md)), to wait.
By extension the root also names the placing of a thing onto a
surface: to `sed` a pot is to fold it down onto its base. The
middle posture in the stand–sit–lie triad.
