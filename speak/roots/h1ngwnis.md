# h1ngwnis

category: 1e

PIE: *h₁n̥gʷnis
Gloss: fire
Descendants: ignite, Latin *ignis*, Sanskrit *agni*

## Definition

Fire as the live edge — the biting, eating, leaping part. Where
[peh2wr](peh2wr.md) is fire-as-thing (the hearth-pile held in
place), `h1ngwnis` is fire-as-act: the spark that catches when
stone strikes stone, the flame-tongue that climbs dry grass, the
hunger with which fire takes wood and makes it into light and
smoke and ash. To `h1ngwnis` is to start a fire, to make one
begin from nothing — and the fire that has just caught, the fresh
flame eating its first fuel, is `h1ngwnis` too. The speaker meets
this face when the fire is young, when it is spreading, when it
is dangerous. [h1eg](h1eg.md) can [dheh1](dheh1.md) the sticks
together, but the catching itself — the jump from stone-strike to
burning — is `h1ngwnis`, its own act with its own name. The
live-verb side of the same substance [peh2wr](peh2wr.md) holds as
noun.
