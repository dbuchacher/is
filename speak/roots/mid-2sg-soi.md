# mid-2sg-soi

category: 4c  | morpheme: verb-ending

PIE: *-soi
Gloss: middle voice 2sg
Descendants: Greek *-sai*, Sanskrit *-se*

## Definition

Appends to a verb-stem and marks you-do-this-to-yourself —
binds the addressee-pole ([tu](tu.md)) with the bend-back
pointer ([se](se.md)) so the act loops from the addressed
body back to the same body. Where [verb-2sg-si](verb-2sg-si.md)
aims the act outward from the addressee, mid-2sg-soi folds the
aim back onto the addressee's own body. Greek *-sai* (luēsai
"you loose yourself") and Sanskrit *-se* carry the shape
forward. Sits as the 2sg pole of the middle-voice set.

## Composes-with examples

- wash-root + mid-2sg-soi → "you wash yourself"
- loose-root + mid-2sg-soi → Greek *luēsai* "you loose
  yourself"
- [weid](weid.md) stem + mid-2sg-soi → "you see yourself"
