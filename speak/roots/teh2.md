# teh2

category: 1h

PIE: *(s)teh₂-
Gloss: to stand
Descendants: see above

## Definition

The same stand-root as [steh2](steh2.md) in its s-less shade — a
body held upright along the [h2en](h2en.md)/[upo](upo.md) axis,
same posture, but with the shade of rising-up-into-standing
rather than the bare stance itself. Where [steh2](steh2.md) names
the held stillness, `teh2` carries the edge of coming-to-stand:
the body pulling itself from [sed](sed.md) or [legh](legh.md) up
onto [pods](pods.md), the pillar-that-becomes-a-pillar. Like
[gweyh3](gweyh3.md) paired with [h1es](h1es.md), `teh2` sits next
to [steh2](steh2.md) as a polysemy twin — one root-stroke that
later tongues split into two forms, one keeping the s-, one
shedding it. The steady-stance reading stays with
[steh2](steh2.md); the rising-stance reading carries here.
