# dyews

category: 1e

PIE: *dyḗws
Gloss: bright sky / day-god
Descendants: Zeus, Latin *deus*, *dies*, Tuesday (Tiw), Sanskrit *dyaus*

## Definition

The bright-up-thing that holds [h2en](h2en.md) over all speakers
at once and is also the one that speakers call out to when they
[wekw](wekw.md) into the open air — sky and sky-being are one
word because to the speaker they are one face. When the
[h2ewsos](h2ewsos.md) (dawn) opens and the [h1regwos](h1regwos.md)
(dark) rolls back, what stands revealed is `dyews`: the lit dome
that [steh2](steh2.md)-s over every hunter from every place, the
same for [h1eg](h1eg.md) and for [tu](tu.md), seen from every
camp. It does not walk, it does not die, it carries the
[soh2wl](soh2wl.md) across itself every day and holds the
[h2ster](h2ster.md)-s every night. Because it looks down on every
eye at once and answers nothing and yet grants the light that
lets seeing happen, the speaker addresses it — and the address
and the thing-addressed are one root. The later split into Zeus
(the god) and Latin *dies* (the day) and *deus* (the divine) is
not three meanings; it is one pointing kept: the bright-up-one
that [weid](weid.md)-s all and is [weid](weid.md)-ed by all.
