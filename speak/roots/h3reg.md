# h3reg

category: 3c

PIE: *h₃reǵ-
Gloss: to rule / straighten
Descendants: right, reach, rex, regulate, Latin *regere*, Greek *oregō*

## Definition

To pull a bent thing back into a straight line — the hand taking a
warped stave of [deru](deru.md) and bending it true against the
knee, the arm [per](per.md)-ing outward along the line it wants to
hold. `h3reg` is the straightening-stroke at once on wood and on
folk: what is crooked is pulled into line, what is leaning is set
upright. When the stroke falls on a stave, the stave becomes
straight; when the stroke falls on a people gone out of order, the
folk come back into line. The physical stroke and the law-making
stroke are one root because in PIE seeing they are one act. Latin
*regere* keeps both (to rule a people, to direct a line), Greek
*oregō* keeps the bodily reach. Gives "right" (the straight line
made law) and "regulate" (the holding-straight of what drifts).
Kin to [dheh1](dheh1.md): where *dheh1* places a thing so it
holds, `h3reg` holds what is already placed along its true line.
