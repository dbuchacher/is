# ghabh

category: 1h

PIE: *ǵʰabʰ-
Gloss: to seize / have
Descendants: gift, have?, Latin *habere*

## Definition

To close [mr-gh](mr-gh.md) on a thing and keep holding it — the
grasp that does not let go afterward, so the thing stays with
[h1eg](h1eg.md) through time. Where [deh3](deh3.md) names the
release at the end of a passing and [dheh1](dheh1.md) names the
setting-down onto a spot, `ghabh` names the closing-and-keeping:
[h1eg](h1eg.md) takes the meat and it [wes](wes.md)-es in the
hold. The held-thing is gift when given, possession when kept.
This is the head-shade of a five-way polysemy: four more grasp-
roots each name a different edge of taking-and-keeping (the
snatch, the bare hand-power, the choosing pickup, the lifting-
to-hold). All five share one grasp-stroke; the tongue-splits
follow which edge of it the hearer stands closest to.
