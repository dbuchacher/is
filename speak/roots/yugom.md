# yugom

category: 3a

PIE: *yugóm
Gloss: yoke
Descendants: yoke, conjugal, junction, Latin *iugum*, Greek *zugon*, Sanskrit *yuga*

## Definition

The shaped bar of [deru](deru.md) laid across the necks of two
draft-beasts so they pull [sem](sem.md) — one piece of wood making
two bodies into one pulling-body. `yugom` binds two oxen at the
shoulder so that when one steps the other must step too; the load
pulled behind them [h1es](h1es.md)-es drawn by neither alone but
by the pair joined. The root carries the sem-stroke into gear: to
`yugom` is to [sem](sem.md) two into one work. From this single
bar Sanskrit *yuga* names a completed span — one full turning of
the age, the cycle from yoke-on to yoke-off — and Latin keeps the
joining-sense in *iugum* (the hill-ridge that joins peak to peak)
and *con-iug-* (married, two joined at the neck). Where
[sem](sem.md) is the bare gathering into one, `yugom` is the made
thing that holds two as one through the day's work. Extends the
wheel-complex built on [kwekwlos](kwekwlos.md): the cart rolls
because wheel, axle, and yoke all hold at once.
