# h2ek

category: 1m

PIE: *h₂eḱ-
Gloss: sharp / pointed
Descendants: acid, acute, edge, Latin *acer*, Greek *akros*

## Definition

Narrowing-to-a-point — the quality of anything that comes down
to an edge or tip fine enough to cut, to pierce, to stick. `h2ek`
names the bitten edge of a flaked stone, the tip of the spear
before it enters flesh, the worked point of a [h2ost](h2ost.md)
sliver, the [h1dont](h1dont.md) of the [wlkwos](wlkwos.md) in
the throat of the kill. A thing is `h2ek` when its narrowness
lets it go where broad things cannot — into the hide, through
the joint, between the ribs. The same word rides to the high
tip of a crag or the peak where the ridge comes to nothing —
the `h2ek` of the mountain is where its body narrows to air. It
is the quality that lets [dwis](dwis.md)-ing happen: without
`h2ek`, no cut.
