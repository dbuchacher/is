# bhel

category: 1e

PIE: *bʰel-
Gloss: white / shining
Descendants: Latin *flamma*, Greek *phalios*

## Definition

The bright-white quality of a surface that throws light back — the
pale of a [snoygwh](snoygwh.md) field under [soh2wl](soh2wl.md),
the gleam of a clean bone ([h2ost](h2ost.md)), the white streak
on the brow of a horse, the flash of a fish's belly turning in
[h2ekweh2](h2ekweh2.md), the sheet of flame climbing a dry branch.
Where [leuk](leuk.md) is light-as-giving (what pours out and lets
the eye work), `bhel` is the holding of that brightness on a
surface — the quality the eye reads as pale, as shining. To
`bhel` is to shine, and a thing that `bhel`-s is white-or-bright
in a way that catches the eye across distance: it pulls
[weid](weid.md) to it. From this the fire that throws visible
flames is `bhel`-ing, and from that come the flame-words of the
later tongues.
