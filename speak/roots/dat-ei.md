# dat-ei

category: 4c  | morpheme: case-ending

PIE: *-ei
Gloss: dative singular
Descendants: Latin *-i*, Greek *-i*

## Definition

Appends to a noun-stem and marks the for-whom slot — the body
that the verb's act aims at as its receiver. Where
[deh3](deh3.md) names the give-stroke (the hand-to-hand pass),
dat-ei is structurally the grammatical shadow of that stroke
held at word-scale: the ending that tags the receiving hand.
When [h1eg](h1eg.md) [deh3](deh3.md)-s meat to [tu](tu.md),
the you-word takes dat-ei to show it is the one the giving
lands toward. Latin *-i*, Greek *-i*, Sanskrit *-e* all carry
this receiver-tag. Sits in the four-case grid as the for-whom
pole, distinct from [acc-m](acc-m.md) (the direct target) by
marking the body the act passes through to rather than the
body it strikes.

## Composes-with examples

- [h3regs](h3regs.md) stem + dat-ei → "to/for the king"
- [tu](tu.md) stem + dat-ei → "to you, for you"
- [meh2ter](meh2ter.md) stem + dat-ei → "to/for the mother"
