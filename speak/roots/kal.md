# kal

category: 1k

PIE: *kal-
Gloss: to call / cry
Descendants: call, Latin *calare* (calendar), Greek *kaleō* (*ekklēsia* = assembly)

## Definition

To throw the voice out loud and far so that the ones who hear
come — the shout that gathers the tribe. Where [wekw](wekw.md)
is the ordinary speaking between two mouths and [klew](klew.md)
is the loud-catching at the ear, `kal` is the calling-side of
that loud-pair: the herder's cry that brings the
flock, the elder's voice that brings the scattered folk to one
fire, the alarm-shout that pulls every body up at once. From this
stem Latin named the day-calling (*calare*) that marked the first
of the month — hence "calendar" — and Greek named the called-out
gathering (*ekklēsia*) — the folk-brought-together by one voice
lifted.
