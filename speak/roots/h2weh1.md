# h2weh1

category: 1e

PIE: *h₂weh₁- / *wento-
Gloss: wind / blow
Descendants: wind, vent, Latin *ventus*

## Definition

The air that moves across [dheghom](dheghom.md) — the pushing
unseen thing that bends [deru](deru.md)-s, carries smoke away
from the hearth, turns [leyp](leyp.md)-s over, drives cold into
the hut through the door-gap. `h2weh1` is air-in-motion and the
moving-of-air at once: to `h2weh1` is what the wind does, and
`h2weh1` is what blows. The speaker feels it on skin before the
eye catches the leaves moving — a touch without a body touching,
a push that runs one way across the land but has no shape you
can see. It brings
[snoygwh](snoygwh.md) down off the high ground in cold months,
carries [megh](megh.md) across the sky, lifts dust. Close kin to
[h2enh1](h2enh1.md) (the breath that moves in the chest), since
to the Paleolithic speaker the wind-outside and the
breath-inside are the same moving air met two ways.
