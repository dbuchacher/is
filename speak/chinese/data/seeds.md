# Chinese seeds

Same discipline as PIE + Sumerian + Egyptian. ~25 core morphemes
grounded in deixis; everything else reduces to seeds +
already-defined non-seeds. Pinyin slugs, tones stripped.

## 1a — Pronouns and Deictics (4)

- **wo** — I / me / my (1sg). Oracle-bone 我 originally a weapon-
  graph repurposed as 1sg pronoun. Points at speaker.
- **ru** — you (2sg). Oracle-bone 汝. Points at addressee.
- **qi** — his / its / that (3sg possessive / distal demonstrative).
  Oracle-bone 其 originally a winnowing-basket graph. Points at
  previously-named.
- **shi** — this (proximal demonstrative). Oracle-bone 是. Points
  at near-at-hand.

## 1h — Being (2)

- **you** — to have / there is / to exist. Oracle-bone 有. The
  having-verb AND the existence-verb (same morpheme).
- **wu** — not-have / nothing / no. Oracle-bone 無 (dancer-graph).
  The negated-existence, paired against `you`.

## 1e — Cosmic axes (4)

- **tian** — sky / heaven / god / high-god. Oracle-bone 天
  (graph of a standing person with an enlarged head/top). One
  word for the overhead-vault AND the high-god Di/Tian. Sky and
  god are one morpheme — same pattern as Sumerian `an`, Egyptian
  `ra`/`nTr`-`pt` coupling.
- **di** — earth / ground / soil. Oracle-bone 地 / 土 (tǔ in its
  older form). The below-pole, paired with `tian`.
- **shang** — up / above / upon / high. Oracle-bone 上 (a mark
  above a reference-line). The upward-direction AND the upper-
  position.
- **xia** — down / below / under / low. Oracle-bone 下 (a mark
  below a reference-line). Paired against `shang`.

## 1e — Sun / moon / time (3)

- **ri** — sun / day. Oracle-bone 日 (a circle with a dot). The
  bright disc AND the span-of-bright. Sun AND day one word —
  same as Egyptian `ra`.
- **yue** — moon / month. Oracle-bone 月 (a crescent). The
  night-light AND the lunar-cycle-span. Moon AND month one word.
- **nian** — year / harvest. Oracle-bone 年 (a person carrying
  grain). The harvest-event AND the year-cycle (harvests mark
  years). Harvest AND year one word.

## 1d — Body (5)

- **shou_head** — head / chief / first. Oracle-bone 首 (a head
  with hair). Body-top and rank-top share the morpheme.
- **xin** — heart / mind / intention / center. Oracle-bone 心
  (a stylized organ). The chest-organ AND the seat of thought.
  Same cross-cultural heart=mind fusion as Egyptian `ib`,
  Sumerian `šà`.
- **shou_hand** — hand / grasp / one who does. Oracle-bone 手
  (five fingers). The grasping-organ AND the doer (as in
  "archer" = 弓手 bow-hand).
- **zu** — foot / leg / basis / enough. Oracle-bone 足 (a foot).
  The walking-organ AND "enough/sufficient" (a later extension
  but already present in oracle-bone usage of the graph).
- **kou** — mouth / opening / entrance. Oracle-bone 口 (a square
  opening). Body-mouth AND any opening — door-kou, well-kou,
  cave-kou. Same as Egyptian `rA`, Sumerian `ka`.

## 1i — Motion (2)

- **xing** — walk / go / conduct / do / act. Oracle-bone 行
  (a crossroads graph). One word for walking AND doing AND
  conduct — the motion-verb fused with the action-verb. A
  characteristic Chinese unity-preservation.
- **lai** — to come. Oracle-bone 來 (a wheat-graph, loaned for
  the coming-verb). The toward-speaker motion. Paired with
  going-sense of `xing`.

## 1j — Perception (2)

- **jian** — to see / to perceive / to meet. Oracle-bone 見 (eye
  over person). The seeing-primitive.
- **wen** — to hear / to ask / to smell / to learn-of. Oracle-
  bone 聞 (ear in doorway). One word for hearing AND asking AND
  learning-of — the aural-perception cluster.

## 1k — Speech (1)

- **yan** — speak / words / speech. Oracle-bone 言 (a tongue or
  mouth-with-marker). Speech-verb AND speech-product (the words
  themselves) — same morpheme.

## 1m — Qualities (2)

- **da** — big / great / large. Oracle-bone 大 (a person with
  arms outstretched — the body-showing-size graph).
- **xiao** — small / little. Oracle-bone 小 (three small dots).
  Paired against `da`.

## 1n — Numerals (3)

- **yi** — one. Oracle-bone 一 (a single horizontal stroke).
- **er** — two. Oracle-bone 二 (two horizontal strokes).
- **san** — three. Oracle-bone 三 (three horizontal strokes).

## Total: 28 seeds

All grounded in deixis (pointing-at-world) or in direct graph-
iconicity (the numeral-strokes, the sun-circle, the mouth-square,
the body-with-arms-out). Each may reference other seeds but not
non-seeds.

## Differences from PIE, Sumerian, Egyptian seed sets

- **Sky=god fused at root**: Chinese `tian` parallels Sumerian `an`
  and Egyptian `ra`/`nTr`-cluster. Three independent substrates
  (four with PIE's `dyeus`) converge on overhead-bright-divine as
  one morpheme.
- **Sun=day, moon=month**: Chinese keeps BOTH at seed-level. `ri`
  (sun=day) matches Egyptian `ra` (sun=day). `yue` (moon=month)
  is stronger than the Egyptian pairing — in Chinese the lunar
  cycle literally names the calendar unit. The celestial body
  AND its cycle-period are one word.
- **Heart=mind fused**: `xin` extends the cross-substrate pattern
  (Egyptian `ib`, Sumerian `šà`). Four substrates now attest
  thought-in-the-chest.
- **Mouth=opening fused**: `kou` matches Egyptian `rA`, Sumerian
  `ka`. Body-opening and generic-opening one word.
- **Walk=do fused**: `xing` is distinctive. Chinese names the
  motion-verb AND the action-verb AND conduct-of-life with one
  morpheme — the unity of go-forward and act-forward. Not
  matched at seed level in the other three substrates (though
  PIE `*gwem`/`*ei` cluster shows hints).
- **Harvest=year fused**: `nian` ties the calendar to the event
  that defines it. No direct parallel in the other three seed
  sets — a substrate-specific unity where time-counting is named
  after the concrete event that counts it.
- **Tonelessness is honest**: Oracle-bone tonogenesis is post-
  Shang. Using tone-less pinyin reflects the attested state, not
  imposing modern Mandarin categories on 1200 BCE morphemes.
