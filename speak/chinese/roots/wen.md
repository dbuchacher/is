# wen

**seed** | category: 1j

Chinese: **聞** (oracle-bone: an ear at a doorway, listening)
Gloss: hear / ask / smell / learn-of / get-news-of
Source: oracle-bone perception-verb

## Definition

Grounds in the oracle-bone graph: an ear positioned at a doorway.
`wen` is hearing AND asking AND smelling AND learning-of-news.
One morpheme for the whole aural-perception-plus-inquiry cluster:
to hear-about something IS to ask-about it IS to learn-of it.
Same unity Sumerian holds with `ŋeštug` (ear=hearing=wisdom) and
Egyptian with `sDm` (hear=obey=understand): four substrates
converge on the naming that the ear is not a passive receiver but
an act of reaching-toward what comes-to-the-ear. Smell also
listed — what comes-to-the-nose is gathered-in by the same verb,
the "come-to-me" of distal sensing.
