# fu

category: 1c

Chinese: **父** (fù, oracle-bone graph of a hand holding a stone-axe)
Gloss: father / senior-male / forebear
Source: oracle-bone corpus; central kinship term

## Definition

The male body one generation above — the one whose [shou_hand](shou_hand.md)
held the axe, whose decisions bound the clan. The father-primitive.
When a father [wu](wu.md)-s the body, he becomes a clan-spirit
and receives offerings. Paired with the mother-body in the
parent-pair.

## Groups

- [kin-roots](kin-roots.md) — the father-pole
- [male-roots](male-roots.md) — the senior-male body
- [parent-pair](parent-pair.md) — one pole of the two-parents set
