# xing

**seed** | category: 1i

Chinese: **行** (oracle-bone: a crossroads — four paths meeting)
Gloss: walk / go / do / act / conduct / carry-out
Source: oracle-bone graph of a crossroads; ubiquitous in all periods

## Definition

Grounds in the oracle-bone graph: a crossroads where paths meet.
`xing` is walking along a path AND doing-the-thing AND conducting-
oneself. One word for motion-forward and action-forward: to go
somewhere and to carry-something-out are named with the same
morpheme. A ruler's "xing" is both their walking-around-the-realm
AND their exercise-of-rule. This is a distinctive Chinese unity-
preservation: the motion-verb and the action-verb and the
conduct-of-life verb are one. The deep naming: to act IS to move
forward; to move forward IS to act.
