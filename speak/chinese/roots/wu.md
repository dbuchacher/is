# wu

**seed** | category: 1h

Chinese: **無** (oracle-bone: a dancer holding plumes — loaned for negation)
Gloss: not have / there is no / nothing / no
Source: primary existential negator in oracle-bone

## Definition

Grounds by pointing at where a body might be expected but is not.
The absence-verb, paired against [you](you.md). In oracle-bone
divinations: "wu yu" = "there will be no rain." The negated-pole
of presence. Same word for "not-have" (possessive absence) and
"there-is-no" (existential absence) — the unity Chinese preserves:
having and being are one naming, so not-having and not-being are
one naming.
