# you

**seed** | category: 1h

Chinese: **有** (oracle-bone: a hand holding a piece of meat)
Gloss: to have / there is / to exist / possess
Source: foundational; most common existential verb in oracle-bone

## Definition

Grounds by pointing at what-is-present. When the speaker names a
body that stands in the speaking-world, the verb is `you`. One
word for "there is X" AND "I have X" — possession and existence
are the same naming. In oracle-bone divinations: "you yu" = "there
will be rain" AND "we have rain," same form. Paired against
[wu](wu.md) (not-have / nothing). The ↕ of presence.
