# yi

**seed** | category: 1n

Chinese: **一** (yī, oracle-bone graph of a single horizontal stroke)
Gloss: one / single / unified / alone
Source: oracle-bone corpus

## Definition

Grounds by a single mark — the graph IS the count-of-one made
visible. `yi` names one-thing, and extends to unity: 天下一
"under-sky unified" = the whole world as one. One-count AND
oneness share the morpheme. The first count, paired with
[er](er.md) (two) and [san](san.md) (three) as the base
trio of counts.
