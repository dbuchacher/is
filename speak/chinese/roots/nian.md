# nian

**seed** | category: 1e

Chinese: **年** (oracle-bone: a person carrying grain on their back)
Gloss: year / harvest / harvest-cycle
Source: the oracle-bone graph shows the harvest-event itself

## Definition

Grounds in the oracle-bone graph: a body bearing grain. `nian` is
the harvest-event AND the year-cycle that the harvest marks. One
word for the concrete-event and the calendar-unit named after it.
The long cycle completed by [ri](ri.md)-after-[ri](ri.md) and
[yue](yue.md)-after-[yue](yue.md) until the grain comes up again.
In oracle-bone divinations the king asks whether "this nian will
be good-harvest" — the year IS the harvest, the harvest IS the
year. No split between the calendar and the concrete event that
defines it.
