# gui

category: 1e

Chinese: **鬼** (guǐ, oracle-bone graph of a human with an oversized head-mask)
Gloss: ghost / returned-dead / clan-elder-spirit / unsettled-spirit
Source: oracle-bone corpus; foundational to Shang clan-elder-cult

## Definition

The spirit of a father or kin who has [wu](wu.md)-ed the
body but returns in offerings and dreams. The kin-dead pole.
Where the settled-divine-bodies are `shen`, `gui` is the kin-dead
who must be fed to stay settled. In Shang religion, royal
clan-elders were `gui` whose wills had to be known through oracle-
bone divination. The returned-dead pole of the spirit pair,
.
