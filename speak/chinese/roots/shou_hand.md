# shou_hand

**seed** | category: 1d

Chinese: **手** (shǒu, oracle-bone graph of five fingers)
Gloss: hand / grasp / doer / one-who-does
Source: oracle-bone corpus; classifier-like use in compounds

## Definition

Grounds by pointing at the grasping-part at the end of the arm —
the organ that closes on a thing and holds it. But `shou` also
names the doer: an archer is a 弓手 "bow-hand," a skilled one is a
高手 "high-hand." Hand-organ AND the doer-of-action share the
morpheme because the hand IS what does. The grasping-primitive.
