## Shells are integer magnitudes

Each dimension is a trit. Values are integers: ..., -2, -1, 0, +1, +2, ...
No fractions. No half-shells. What was once called "k=½" is just
magnitude 2 — the next shell up.

- **Shell 1**: coords from {-1, 0, +1} → 3⁴ = 81 points
- **Shell 2**: coords reach ±2 → 5⁴ − 3⁴ = 544 new points
- **Shell 3**: coords reach ±3 → 7⁴ − 5⁴ = 2,016 new points
- **Origin**: (0,0,0,0) = 1 point = the basket 𓎡


## 24 = The Extremes of Shell 1

The 24 uniliterals split into two groups at opposite ends of the
dimension-activity spectrum:

- **8 atoms**: 1 active dimension, C(4,1) × 2¹ = 8
- **16 bonds**: 4 active dimensions, C(4,4) × 2⁴ = 16

Atoms are the simplest coords (one axis). Bonds are the most
complex (all axes). The uniliterals ARE the endpoints.

The middle (2-3 active dims) = 24 + 32 = 56 coords. These are
the biliterals that aren't also uniliterals.

Found independently five times:

- Lattice: 16 bonds + 8 atoms = 24

Same floor. Same d=4. The minimum complete set of any
