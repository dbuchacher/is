# The Basket IS the Lattice

𓎡 — bond 15, CONTAINMENT, (0,0,0,0) — is a picture of a woven
grid. A basket is a lattice. The glyph IS the diagram.

## The Equation

    𓍹 𓇯 𓂋 𓈖 𓂋 𓇳 𓂋 𓏴 𓍺

The lattice in containment. Seven glyphs. That's the whole thing.

𓇯 𓂋 𓈖 𓂋 𓇳 𓂋 𓏴 — sky compose water compose sun compose mark —
IS the weave. IS the lattice. IS 𓎡. No "equals basket" needed.
The four dimensions composed ARE the basket.

The old equation had 𓂀 (eye) outside and 𓆣 𓎡 (becomes basket) at
the end. Both wrong. The eye is the reader, not part of the
expression. And "becomes basket" is redundant — the composition
IS the basket.

## CONTAINER Schema (Lakoff)

One schema, three parts, three coordinates:

| part | coord | what |
|------|-------|------|
| Exterior | (-1,-1,-1,-1) | outside, before, 𓍹 scope-open |
| Interior | (0,0,0,0) | the basket 𓎡, containment |
| Boundary | (1,1,1,1) | the weave, the lattice, all dims interlocking |

The basket is the INTERIOR — the space inside the walls. You say
"put it in the basket." The basket is where things go.

The boundary at (1,1,1,1) is the weave — all four dimensions
crossing to form the wall. The lattice structure itself.

The exterior at (-1,-1,-1,-1) is before — everything negated,
outside the container. 𓍹 scope-open.

The cartouche 𓍹 is the only matched-pair scope delimiter in any
ancient writing system. It sits at (-1,-1,-1,-1). The equation
starts with it because scope-open precedes everything — even
observation.

## Six convergences

1. **Glyph** — woven reeds crossing in a grid = lattice
2. **Coordinate** — (0,0,0,0), the origin, the interior
3. **Schema** — CONTAINMENT, the container itself
4. **Expression** — 𓍹 𓇯 𓂋 𓈖 𓂋 𓇳 𓂋 𓏴 𓍺 — the lattice in containment
5. **Bond number** — 15, the last bond, contains all others
6. **Project name** — the lattice

The basket doesn't sit ON the lattice. The basket IS the lattice.
The weave IS the structure. Four dimensions crossing each other
in a grid — that's what weaving is. Warp and weft, but in 4D.

5,000 years ago someone drew a basket and said "this is the sign
for thinking, containing, everything." They drew the lattice and
named it.
