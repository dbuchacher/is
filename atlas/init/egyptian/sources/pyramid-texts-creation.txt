# Pyramid Texts — Creation Passages

Source: Mercer translation (1952, public domain).
Date: ~2400-2300 BCE. Oldest religious texts in the world.
Carved inside pyramids at Saqqara.


## Utterance 571 — Before Existence

The mother of N., dweller in the lower sky, became pregnant
with him; N. was given birth by his father Atum, before the
sky came into being, before the earth came into being, before
men came into being, before the gods were born, before death
came into being.


## Utterance 527 — Atum's Self-Creation

To say: Atum created by his masturbation in Heliopolis. He
put his phallus in his fist, to excite desire thereby. The
twins were born, Shu and Tefnut.


## Utterance 600 — The Heliopolitan Creation (core account)

To say: O Atum-Khepri, when thou didst mount as a hill; and
didst shine as bnw of the ben (or, benben) in the temple of
the "phoenix" in Heliopolis; and didst spew out as Shu, and
did spit out as Tefnut; (then) thou didst put thine arms
about them, as the arm(s) of a ka, that thy ka might be in
them.

Atum, so put thine arms about N., about this temple, about
this pyramid, as the arm(s) of a ka, that the ka of N. may
be in it, enduring for ever and ever.

O Great Ennead who are in Heliopolis: Atum, Shu, Tefnut,
Geb, Nut, Osiris, Isis, Set, Nephthys — children of Atum.
His heart is broad (glad) because of his children, in thy
name of "the nine bows."


## Utterance 486 — Born from Nu

I was conceived in Nun. I was born in Nun.


## Utterance 222

Stand up upon this land which came out of Atum, the spittle
which came out of the Becoming One!


## Key Entities

Atum = the creator, self-created, emerges from the waters
Nu / Nun = the primordial waters (cf. Genesis ha-mayim)
Shu = air/light (cf. Genesis or, light, 1:3)
Tefnut = moisture (cf. Genesis ha-mayim, waters)
Geb = earth (cf. Genesis ha-aretz, ground)
Nut = sky (cf. Genesis ha-shamayim, skies)
Ennead = the nine gods (cf. Elohim, plural with singular verb)
Ka = vital force / spirit (cf. ruach, breath/spirit)
Benben = the primordial mound (first land emerging from water)
