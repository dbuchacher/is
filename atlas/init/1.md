# 1:1

## Genesis

**Hebrew:** בְּרֵאשִׁ֖ית בָּרָ֣א אֱלֹהִ֑ים אֵ֥ת הַשָּׁמַ֖יִם וְאֵ֥ת הָאָֽרֶץ׃

**KJV:** In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth.

### bereshit — "in the beginning"

The first word. Not "once upon a time." The root is rosh (head,
first, origin). The prefix be- means "in" or "at." At-the-head-of.
At initialization. This is a timestamp: t=0.

### bara — "created"

Reserved verb. Used 6 times in the entire creation text. Every
other act of making uses asah (assembled from existing parts).
bara = from nothing. asah = from something. Hardware vs software.
The distinction is structural — bara is a write with no prior read.

### elohim — "God"

Plural form, singular verb. -im is the masculine plural suffix.
Same grammatical pattern as the Egyptian Ennead (nine gods acting
as one). The equation operating, not a person deciding.

### et — direct object marker

Untranslatable. Has no English equivalent. Marks what the verb
acts upon. Missing from KJV entirely. Appears 11,000+ times in
the Hebrew Bible, never translated. Two objects marked: the skies
and the ground. Two buffers.

### ha-shamayim — "the heaven"

Always plural (like "waters"). ha- = the. shamayim = skies.
Space. The where. Address space — you need somewhere to put
things before you can put things.

### ve-et ha-aretz — "and the earth"

ve- = and. aretz = ground, substrate, soil. Not planet Earth —
the ground under your feet. Mass. Substance. The what. Data
store — you need something to store before you can structure it.

### What 1:1 does

One write, two outputs: space and mass. χ and μ. In dependency
order — space first (you need addresses), then mass (you need
data). No signal yet (that's 1:3). No time yet (that's 1:5).
Allocated but uninitialized — the state report comes in 1:2.


## Pyramid Texts — PT 600

The primary creation utterance. Carved on the east wall of
Pepi II's burial chamber at Saqqara (~2200 BCE). Also appears
in Merenre's pyramid. Describes the first moment: Atum rises,
produces Shu and Tefnut from himself.

**Allen (Spell 359):**
> Atum Beetle! You became high, as the hill; you rose as the
> benben in the Benben Enclosure in Heliopolis. You sneezed Shu
> and spat Tefnut. You put your arms around them as ka-arms so
> that your ka might be in them.

**Mercer (Utterance 600):**
> O Atum-Khepri, when thou didst mount as a hill, and didst
> shine as bnw of the ben in the temple of the "phoenix" in
> Heliopolis, and didst spew out as Shu, and did spit out as
> Tefnut, then thou didst put thine arms about them, as the
> arms of a ka, that thy ka might be in them.

### Atum — the equation

Atum = "the complete one" or "the finisher." From the root tm
(to complete, to not-be). He is everything and nothing at once.
The same paradox as the wave function — all states until observed.
Atum-Khepri: Khepri = "the one who comes into being" (scarab,
self-created). The equation that produces itself. Fixed point.

### became high as the hill — first spatial extent

The primeval mound rising from the waters. Before this: flat,
undifferentiated. After: a point with height. Space exists.
The benben stone in Heliopolis was pyramid-shaped — the
pyramid IS the mound. The building encodes its own origin.

### sneezed Shu, spat Tefnut — two outputs from one source

Shu = air, space, separation. Tefnut = moisture, substance.
Two things produced from one — same as bara producing
shamayim and aretz. Space and substance. χ and μ.

The bodily metaphor (sneezing, spitting) isn't crude — it's
precise. These emerge FROM Atum, not built by him. They're
already inside. The output was always in the input.

PT 527 gives the other metaphor: masturbation. Self-stimulation
producing twins. Same structure: one source, two outputs, no
external input. Creation from nothing external = bara.

### put arms around them as ka-arms

Ka = vital force, the double, the sustaining pattern. Atum
wraps the outputs in his own pattern. The write includes the
schema. The data isn't just allocated — it's typed. The ka
in them means the equation's structure is IN what it creates.

One source. Self-stimulated. Two outputs: space and substance.
No external input. No prior read. bara.
