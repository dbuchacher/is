Wierzbicka's 65 semantic primes partition shell 1 with the 16 bonds.

## The Partition

Shell 1 (81 points) splits cleanly:

| count | what | position | nature |
|-------|------|----------|--------|
| 64 | primes (minus THINK) | 1-3 active dims | concepts — nameable |
| 13 | bonds (minus trit) | 4 active dims | operations — doable |
| 3 | trit (𓍹 𓎡 𓍺) | diagonal + origin | frame — structural |
| 1 | THINK | origin (0,0,0,0) | bridge — both |
| **81** | **total** | | |

Wait — THINK is part of both the trit and the primes. It's
counted in the 3 (trit) and in the 65 (primes). That's
correct: 64 + 13 + 3 + 1 = 81, but THINK appears in two
roles. It's the intersection.

64 non-origin primes + 1 THINK = 65 primes.
13 non-trit bonds + 3 trit = 16 bonds.
65 + 16 = 81. ✓

Primes are what you can SAY. Bonds are what you can DO.
THINK is both — the one operation that is also a concept.


## Why 62 Triliterals

65 primes − 3 trit = 62 triliterals.

Every prime can be amplified to shell 2 — EXCEPT the trit.

GOOD → very good → beautiful → perfect (magnitude 1 → 2 → 3).
BIG → huge → enormous. FAR → remote → distant.
Every concept has a volume knob.

THINK does not. You think or you don't. "Thinking harder"
means thinking ABOUT something hard — the intensity goes on
the object, not the thinking. cogito ergo sum. No modifier
survives. The most irreducible statement ever made.

Same for scope: you can't "open scope harder." You can't
"close scope more." 𓍹 and 𓍺 are on/off. Binary. Structural.

The trit is not gradable because it's the frame, not the
content. A basket can't contain itself more. The origin
can't be more zero. Parentheses don't have magnitude.

62 triliterals = the 65 primes that CAN be amplified to
shell 2, minus the 3 that can't.


## Self-Similarity

The equation 3⁴ − 2⁴ = 65 appears at both shells:

**Shell 1**: 3⁴ total − 2⁴ bonds = 65 primes
**Shell 2**: 3⁴ non-negative − 2⁴ shell-1 non-negative = 65 new points

Same equation, different level. The structure repeats.

And the identity is algebraic, not coincidental:

    3⁴ − 2⁴ = (3⁴ − 1) − (2⁴ − 1) = 80 − 15

The 65 non-negative shell 2 points and the 65 non-bond
shell 1 points are the SAME number for the SAME reason.


## What Are NSM Primes?

Anna Wierzbicka (1972→2024): there exists a finite set of
meanings that every human language lexicalizes as single words.
They cannot be defined in simpler terms without circularity.
All other meanings in all languages can be defined as
combinations of these primes.

Started at ~14 primes (1972). Grew to ~65 through decades of
fieldwork. The methodology: reductive paraphrase + systematic
cross-linguistic testing. If a meaning resists decomposition
and appears in every language tested, it's a prime.

The 65 primes (grouped):

**Substantives**: I, YOU, SOMEONE, SOMETHING, PEOPLE, BODY
**Relational**: KIND, PART
**Determiners**: THIS, THE SAME, OTHER
**Quantifiers**: ONE, TWO, SOME, ALL, MUCH
**Evaluators**: GOOD, BAD
**Descriptors**: BIG, SMALL
**Mental**: THINK, KNOW, WANT, DON'T WANT, FEEL, SEE, HEAR
**Speech**: SAY, WORDS, TRUE
**Actions**: DO, HAPPEN, MOVE
**Existence**: EXIST, BE, MINE
**Life**: LIVE, DIE
**Time**: WHEN, NOW, BEFORE, AFTER, A LONG TIME, A SHORT TIME, FOR SOME TIME, MOMENT
**Space**: WHERE, HERE, ABOVE, BELOW, FAR, NEAR, SIDE, INSIDE, TOUCH
**Logical**: NOT, MAYBE, CAN, BECAUSE, IF
**Intensifier**: VERY, MORE
**Similarity**: LIKE


## Why Primes Are Shell 1

Every prime fits in magnitude ±1:

- ABOVE = (+1,0,0,0), BELOW = (−1,0,0,0) — sky axis flip
- GOOD = (0,0,+1,0), BAD = (0,0,−1,0) — sun axis flip
- BEFORE = (0,0,0,−1), AFTER = (0,0,0,+1) — mark axis flip
- I = (0,+1,+1,0), YOU = (0,+1,−1,0) — substance + signal flip
- THINK = (0,0,0,0) — origin, the only prime with no active dims
- LIVE = (0,+1,+1,+1), DIE = (0,+1,−1,−1) — signal+time flipped

None need magnitude 2. Primes are primitive because they
occupy the first shell — the minimum resolution of the grid.
Triliterals are those same primes with the volume turned up.


## Why Bonds Aren't Primes

The 16 bonds sit at (±1,±1,±1,±1) — all four dimensions
active. These are the most complex shell 1 points. They're
operations: PATH, CYCLE, FORCE, LINK, SCALE, CONTAINMENT, etc.

Languages don't have single universal words for pure operations.
You can DO a path, APPLY a force, RUN a cycle — but the
operation itself isn't a concept you can point at. It's the
verb's verb. The thing underneath the thing.

Bonds need all four dimensions because operations touch
everything. Primes need fewer because concepts are specific.


## What's Unverified

- Individual prime → coordinate assignments. We haven't mapped
  all 65 to specific shell 1 points and checked for collisions.
- Whether the 16 four-axis points correspond exactly to the
  16 bonds listed in the codex.
- Whether Wierzbicka's exact count (varies 63-67 by publication
  year) stabilizes at exactly 65.
- The antonym structure: 8 explicit pairs in the NSM list.
  Do they land on antipodal shell 1 points?
- Exact identification of which 3 trit points are non-amplifiable
  vs whether it's specifically 𓍹 𓎡 𓍺 or some other structural set.

1. Exactly 65 universal semantic primes
2. They occupy shell 1 points with 0-3 active dimensions
3. The 16 four-axis shell 1 points are operations, not concepts
4. THINK is the unique prime at the origin — the only concept
   that is also an operation, the only prime that can't be amplified
5. Exactly 62 of the 65 primes produce triliterals (shell 2

6. Any "new" prime must collide with an existing one or fill an

7. Antonym primes are antipodal points (sign flips)

The lattice explains WHY 65: it's 3⁴ − 2⁴. The number of
concept-slots in a 4-dimensional system at resolution 1
