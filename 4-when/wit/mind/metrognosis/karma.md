# Karma

Sanskrit **कर्म** (*karma*) derives from the verbal root **√kṛ**,
meaning "to do, to make, to perform." Same PIE family as English
*create*, *ceremony*, Latin *creare* (root **\*kʷer-** / **\*ker-**).

Literal meaning: **VERB**. Action. The thing you do.

Not "cosmic moral accounting." That's the late gloss. The root word
just means *doing*.

## The popular misunderstanding

Western pop-Buddhism / New Age reading:

> "Karma is the universe's moral ledger. Do good → good returns.
> Do bad → bad returns. Cosmic justice."

This is the **secondary** meaning, stacked on top over centuries. The
primary meaning is bare:

> **karma = action = verb.**

"Good karma / bad karma" is shorthand for "good actions / bad
actions." The wheel-closure reading (do X, X returns) is structural,
not moralistic (see § circulation below).

Same slippage as English *energy*: technical term → New Age aura.
Root sense is geometric, not ethical.

## Samsara = the wheel, literally

*Saṃsāra* decomposes as *sam* ("together, with") + *sṛ* ("to flow,
run"). Literal: "flowing around together." The translation "wheel
of existence" is already interpretive — the etymology is just
*circulation*.

Match to framework:
- **flowing** → spin, rotation, verb-tier
- **around** → closed loops, hub-return
- **together** → one substrate, all observers on the same wheel

This is not poetic licence. It's a direct description of rotating
substrate with closed walks.
