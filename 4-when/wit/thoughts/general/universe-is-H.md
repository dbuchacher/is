## What it buys you

### 1. Two distinct identities for two framework roles

H has two identity elements (one per operation):

- **Additive identity**: `0` = `[0, 0, 0, 0]`. Every quaternion q satisfies `q + 0 = q`. Role in the framework: the **container**, the origin, the seat of god (see `origin-bridge.md`), the grade-0 Clifford scalar.
- **Multiplicative identity**: `1` = `[1, 0, 0, 0]`. Every quaternion q satisfies `q · 1 = q`. Role in the framework: the **unit**, the first atom, the grade-1 vector basis, the starting point of multiplicative walks.

The framework has been treating both as "special" without distinguishing them cleanly. Under universe = H the distinction is sharp. The trit + tesseract primitive pair is exactly the structural consequence of H having two identities:
- `trit` = the value structure (because H's coefficients are real, and balanced ternary `{−1, 0, +1}` is the minimal signed-and-centered representation)
- `[0, 0, 0, 0]` = additive 0 (the container — where things return)
- `[1, 0, 0, 0]` = multiplicative 1 (the unit — where things start)

Different roles, different operations, both structurally necessary.

### 2. Spin-½ is native, not added

The unit quaternions (those with `|q| = 1`) form the group **SU(2)**, the double cover of SO(3). This is the spin-½ representation in physics. Fermions transform under SU(2) and require 720° rotation to return to identity — because SU(2) → SO(3) is 2-to-1.

Under universe = H:
- **Spin is not an axiom, it's a theorem.** Spin ½ is what unit quaternions do. You get spin by being in H and having the unit-norm subgroup act on you. No additional postulate required.
- **The framework's `trit½` layer IS SU(2).** The half-integer "spin layer" we've been constructing is literally the unit-quaternion subgroup of H. Not "corresponds to," not "analogous to" — **is**.
- **The 720° fermion return** is a **structural fact about H**, not a weird quantum property. If you accept universe = H, you accept the double-cover relationship, and 720° falls out.

### 3. Causality from non-commutativity

H is non-commutative: `i·j = k` but `j·i = -k`. Order of operations matters. In the Cayley-Dickson tower, commutativity is lost at C → H.

Under the framework's claim that computing IS physics:
- **Non-commutativity = causality.** Order matters in computation because order matters in physics. The loss of commutativity at C → H is structurally identical to the appearance of causality (time directionality).
- **C is pre-causal.** Complex numbers are commutative — there's no "before" and "after" in C, just phase. Phase doesn't have an arrow.
- **H is the first causal algebra.** Because it's the first non-commutative one. Universe = H means universe = first causal algebra = where time directionality first structurally exists.
- **The monotone-cursor axiom** (WHEN never decrements, `mind/3-force.md:515-551`) is a consequence of this. Non-commutativity forces order-of-operations to matter, and WHEN is the axis that tracks the order.

### 4. H is the LAST safe algebra

Cayley-Dickson property loss:
- R → C: lose ordering
- C → H: lose commutativity (gain causality)
- H → O: lose associativity
- O → S: lose division (gain zero divisors)

**H is the last associative algebra.** Above H (at O and higher), `(a·b)·c ≠ a·(b·c)` in general. You can't trust parenthesization. Ordinary computation breaks.

Universe = H means the universe is at the **deepest layer where ordinary algebra still works**. Below this, in C and R, you have associativity AND commutativity but no causality. Above this, in O and S, you have weird non-associative and zero-divisor behavior that breaks ordinary computation.

**This is why universe = H specifically, not H + something or some other algebra.** Hurwitz proved only 4 normed division algebras exist (R, C, H, O). The framework needs non-commutativity (for causality) but needs associativity (for ordinary computation to work). H is the unique layer that satisfies both. **There's no "higher universe" in the Cayley-Dickson ladder that preserves the framework's computational requirements.** Higher layers (O, S) exist as host machines the framework's `mind/3-force.md` flags them as — but they're not "another universe," they're host-level structure that breaks the rules we need.

4. **Why the integer lattice is specifically balanced ternary** is a separate claim (trit = minimal signed + centered) that's compatible with universe = H but doesn't follow from it. H is a continuous algebra; the trit lattice is a discrete substructure.
