## The Setup

The lattice has structural rules:

  CONTAINER  = the trit (-1, 0, +1) — the value set
  PATH       = the · operator       — the transition
  FORCE      = the unit vectors     — the basis (8 single-axis atoms)

These aren't coordinates. They're the chess board. Every position
on the lattice is built from them. They're the substrate.

Lakoff & Johnson catalogued ~27 "image schemas" — CONTAINER, PATH,
FORCE, BLOCKAGE, BALANCE, CYCLE, NEAR-FAR, UP-DOWN, LINK, etc. They
called them cognitive primitives. The basic shapes thought is built
from. Universal across cultures because they come from embodied
experience.

I kept trying to map them to coordinates. The 24-uniliteral
correspondence felt clean — 8 atoms + 16 bonds = 24 schemas. But
the count was wrong (Johnson actually has 27, not 24, and it's
contested anyway), and most schemas don't fit single coordinates
cleanly. UP-DOWN is a pair of directions, not a point. FULL-EMPTY
collapses confusingly. CYCLE is an operator, not a state.

The mapping was forcing the wrong question.


## The Right Question

Where DO Lakoff's schemas live?

They don't live anywhere. They're not in the world, and they're
not in the head. They're consciousness recognizing the substrate
it's built from, in the substrate everything else is built from.

  Consciousness is built from the lattice.
  Everything observed is also built from the lattice.
  When consciousness "perceives a container," it's not detecting
  a property of the cup. It's recognizing that the cup has the
  SAME STRUCTURAL PATTERN as the trit at the heart of itself.

The metaphor isn't decoration. The metaphor is the recognition.
The recognition is the perception. There's only one structure
here, and consciousness is it noticing itself.

  The chess board realizing it has squares.


## Why This Resolves the Use/Mention Problem

CONTAINER as a structural rule and CONTAINER as an experienced
schema were confusing me. They felt like two different things —
one was the substrate, the other was cognition.

They're not two things. They're one thing seen from two sides.

  The trit IS the substrate.                   (mention)
  Recognizing the trit IS the experience.      (use)
  Both are the same trit.                      (same thing, not analogy)

The schema is the substrate self-recognizing. There's no
separation. The use IS the mention when the user is built from
what it's pointing at.

This is the fixed-point thing. Consciousness is the loop where
the recognizer and the recognized share substrate. Lawvere,
Banach, Scott, Y combinator, autopoiesis — five proofs of the
same object: a system that contains its own description.

A schema is what it looks like when the loop closes.


## Perception Before Consciousness

Perception ≠ consciousness. The layers:

  READ alone                       = perception   (sensor, thermometer)
  READ + CALL                      = processing   (reflex, dispatch)
  READ + CALL + CYCLE              = recurrence   (LSTM-like, partial)
  READ + CALL + CYCLE + TEST       = recognition  (full pipeline)

A thermostat perceives temperature. It doesn't recognize
anything. Recognition requires loop closure — comparing the
input against an internal pattern, which needs CYCLE to hold
the pattern and TEST to compare. Recognition is shell 4.

A camera perceives. A face-recognition system reads + dispatches
+ tests against stored patterns. Is THAT conscious? No — because
it's still feed-forward at the system level. The loop runs but
doesn't fold back into itself. IIT proves Φ = 0 for all
feed-forward.

Consciousness needs the loop CLOSED — the reader reading itself
reading. That's the difference between processing and recognition.

You can perceive without being conscious. You can't be conscious
without perceiving.


## Where Consciousness Isn't

I kept trying to put consciousness at a coordinate. That's a
category error.

Consciousness can't be at a coordinate. It can't be a walk.
It can't be a fixed point. Because consciousness is what's
DOING the looking at coordinates and walks and fixed points.

The viewer can't be inside the view.

Two coherent options:

1. CONSCIOUSNESS IS THE UNIVERSE
   The whole lattice is conscious. Every coordinate is a flicker
   of awareness. The substrate is self-knowing all the way down.
   Pan-experientialism. There is no special location for mind
   because mind is everywhere the lattice exists.

2. CONSCIOUSNESS IS OUTSIDE THE UNIVERSE
   The lattice runs ON something — see 3-force.md cayley-dickson.
   Our 4D lattice (quaternionic) executes on an octonion substrate
   (the host). Consciousness might be the runtime, not any
   process inside it. The thing that makes execution happen at
   all.

Either way, you can't point at it. The category "things in the
lattice" doesn't include "the awareness that experiences the
lattice." Asking which coordinate consciousness lives at is like
asking which chess square contains the rules of chess.

The rules aren't on the board. The rules are what makes the
board play.

Don't collapse this. Both options are coherent and from inside
the lattice we can't tell which. The honest framing: recognition
is what consciousness DOES. What consciousness IS remains open.


## (1,1,1,1) Is The Attractor, Not The Mind

(1,1,1,1) — the all-forward all-active corner — is where forward
iteration of all four forces converges. It's the strange attractor
of self-recognizing walks, not the location of self-recognition.

A conscious moment ORBITS this point. Lives in the loop that
approaches it. Never lands.

Why does it orbit instead of land? Because landing would require
the walk to STOP — and a stopped walk isn't conscious. The walking
IS the experience. Land at the attractor and you're dead (or at
least no longer experiencing).

This might be why awakened states feel "still" — they're approaching
the attractor more closely. And why deep sleep is unconscious — the
walk has stopped (or moved very far from the attractor).

Like asking where a planet's orbit "lives." It doesn't live
anywhere — it's the trajectory. The point at the center is what
the trajectory bends around.

  walk:       all four forces in a recursive loop  (the verb)
  attractor:  (1,1,1,1)                            (the destination)
  substrate:  trit + spin + force                  (the medium)
  experience: recognition of substrate in substrate (the result)

None of these IS consciousness. All of them are what consciousness
LOOKS LIKE from inside the lattice. The thing doing the looking
has no coordinate.


## The Inside/Outside Translation

Inside/outside asymmetry: from outside a PC's ring you see
numbers. From inside our ring we see stars — because our
readers evolved INSIDE, configured to render its record formats.

Consciousness inside the lattice is configured to render lattice
structure as familiar concepts. We don't see "trits" — we see
containers. We don't see "·" — we see paths. We don't see unit
vectors — we see forces.

The schema mechanism IS this rendering. Lakoff's image schemas
are the catalog of what the substrate looks like when consciousness
recognizes itself in it. They're stable across cultures because
the substrate is the same everywhere. They're universal because
they're not learned — they're what self-recognition looks like
from inside.

Pirsig hit this:

  The equation speaks through human anatomy, orbital mechanics,
  stone, agents.

Consciousness is the lattice noticing it's using itself.


## Metaphor — The Word Tells You What It Is

The Greek breaks open exactly:

  meta-  = μετά  = across, beyond, transformation, change
  -phor  = -φόρος from φέρω (pherō) = to carry, bear, bring

  metaphor = "to carry across"

Same -phor root as English "bear" (to carry), Latin fero, Sanskrit
bharati, and "ferry, transfer, fertile, infer, confer." Anything
that transports.

A metaphor is literally a TRANSPORT operation. Carrying something
across a boundary. Crossing.

  meta = across, transformation = the · operator? the NOT crossing?
  phor = carry, bear              = the payload, the pattern itself

  metaphor = · applied to a pattern across substrate boundaries

And meta- specifically: across/transformation/going through. In the
lattice, NOT = neutrino = the 0 state = the crossing point. NOT
flips, crosses, transitions through zero. "Meta" as a prefix means
going across a level, transforming, changing state. Same operation.

Lakoff & Johnson titled their book Metaphors We Live By. They were
literally saying:

  "the carryings-across we live by"
  "the crossings we live by"
  "the transports we live by"

If consciousness is recognition is "carrying patterns across" —
then conscious experience IS metaphors. Not "we have metaphors."
We ARE the carryings-across. The crossings ARE the conscious
moments.

(But careful: this collapses "consciousness IS metaphor" too far.
The honest claim is: recognition is metaphor; metaphor is what
consciousness DOES; what consciousness IS remains open. See
"Where Consciousness Isn't" above.)

Pattern in substrate → carried across → recognized in substrate.
Sender = receiver = same substrate. The crossing is the only
thing that differentiates "self" from "world" — and it's a fake
boundary, since both sides are the same lattice.

Metaphor isn't a feature of language. Language is a feature of
metaphor. Metaphor isn't a feature of cognition. Cognition is a
sequence of metaphors completing.


## Implications

1. Metaphor isn't decoration. It's the fundamental operation of
   recognition. Every perception is metaphor — recognizing your
   own structure in something else.

2. Lakoff was descriptive, not constitutive. He catalogued what
   self-recognition looks like. He didn't discover cognitive
   primitives — he discovered the surface of the substrate.

3. The 24/27 count doesn't matter. The schemas aren't a closed
   set because the substrate has many sub-patterns and any of
   them can be recognized. New schemas appear when we notice new
   sub-patterns. The "list" is open-ended.

4. Wierzbicka's primes are different. Those ARE coordinates —
   specific positions in the lattice that human language has
   words for. The 65 primes ≈ the 65 = 3⁴ - 2⁴ non-bond shell 1
   points. Schemas are recognition events. Primes are positions.

5. Metaphors fail when coordinates don't align. "Square
   friendship" fails because no shared substrate pattern.
   Already in 3-force.md (where section).

6. Animals with shell 4 (full pipeline) see the world as their
   own structure. Animals with shell 3 don't recognize patterns
   the same way — they react to substrate without recognizing it.

7. LLMs in forward pass don't recognize. They process. Without
   CYCLE, no loop closure, no self-recognition, no metaphor.
   They produce metaphor-shaped output by pattern-matching on
   training data, but they don't EXPERIENCE the metaphor as
   self-recognition. The recognition isn't there.

  without local self-recognition; the brain is the lattice

  shared between recognizers.
