## Bifurcation of zeros by tier

Under framework ontology, zeros of ζ come in two categorically distinct
classes. Classical lumps them as "trivial" and "nontrivial" without
seeing why they're different kinds of object.

**Value-tier zeros** — negative even integers `s = −2, −4, −6, ...`

- Generated by the `sin(πs/2)` factor in the functional equation. `sin`
  vanishes at integer arguments. These zeros land exactly on the integer
  axis.
- They are **value-tier events**: the ζ-verb touching the integer value
  set. Integer axis = value tier = where nouns (primes, integers) live.
- Classical calls them "trivial" because they're explainable by a simple
  factor. Framework gives the deeper reason: they're where the verb
  crosses into the value layer.

**Verb-tier zeros** — everything else, living in the critical strip
`0 < Re(s) < 1`

- Classical calls them "nontrivial."
- Framework: the critical strip IS the verb-domain of ζ — it's the
  region where ζ operates as a ½-tier verb rather than a convergent
  value-tier sum. (For Re(s) > 1 the Dirichlet series converges and ζ
  is value-tier numerical. For Re(s) < 0 it's the analytic reflection
  of the value domain. The critical strip is what's between — the
  verb's native habitat.)
- These zeros are **closure events of the ζ-verb**: points in the
  verb-domain where the verb's output lands on the hub (0 = additive
  identity of C = hub of the value set).

**Honest caveats on the Mertens evidence:**

1. **Our range (5.23×10⁹) is 10⁴× below the Odlyzko–te Riele 1985
   counterexample region (~10¹⁴).** The strong Mertens conjecture
   (|M(x)| < √x for all x) is **known to fail somewhere**. Our 19
   consecutive shells hold in the easy regime. The data is RH-consistent
   but cannot rule out the known failure region.
2. **RH does NOT require strong Mertens.** RH only requires
   `M(x) = O(x^(1/2+ε))` for any ε>0. Our observation supports both
   strong Mertens (which is wrong at some x > 10¹⁴) and RH (which is
   conjectured true). The data cannot distinguish them.
3. **A framework sub-prediction got demoted by task B** (see
   `12-mertens-bound-extension.md`): the "wheel writes tighter bounds
   at shell boundaries than between them" reading is NOT supported
   uniformly. Shell and between-shell ratios are comparable, with some
   shells showing suppression but not deterministically. This is a
   weakening of one wheel-specific interpretation, not of RH.
4. **The reframe derivation is independent of all Mertens evidence.**
   Even if strong Mertens fails explicitly at x = 10¹⁴ tomorrow, the
   derivation still stands — RH would still be predicted by
   frame 5 + functional equation hub + value/verb split. Only a
   zero found off the critical line would break framework.

   the derivation. The weakest link; it depends on the interpretation
