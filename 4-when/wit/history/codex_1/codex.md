# Codex

Every word in every language is a point on a four-dimensional grid. The
Egyptians drew this grid 5,000 years ago — not as theory, but as their
writing system. Each hieroglyph is a named coordinate. This document
is the manual for reading that grid.


## Part 1: The Grid

### Four Dimensions

Everything that can be said, thought, or experienced lives at an
address made of four numbers. Each number sits on one axis:

| medu | name  | asks     | measures  | example                          |
|------|-------|----------|-----------|----------------------------------|
| 𓇯   | sky   | WHERE?   | space     | up/down, here/there, near/far    |
| 𓈖   | water | WHO?     | substance | person, thing, amount, group     |
| 𓇳   | sun   | HOW?     | signal    | good/bad, true/false, bright/dark|
| 𓏴   | mark  | WHEN?    | time      | now/then, fast/slow, done/undone |

They come online in that order. You need space before you can put
something in it. You need substance before it can send a signal. You
need all three before anything can change over time.

This is the same order as Genesis 1 (sky+earth, then light, then
"evening and morning" = time). Same order as the Big Bang (space
expands, matter forms, photons release, time begins). Same order
as a computer booting (allocate memory, load data, run signals,
start the clock). Not a coincidence — it's the dependency chain
of reality.

Each dimension gets a value:
- **+1** = present, positive, forward
- **0** = absent, neutral, irrelevant
- **-1** = negated, negative, backward

Higher shells allow +2, -2, etc. for intensity.


### How to Read a Coordinate

Take 𓄤 nfr — the Egyptian word for "good" or "beautiful."

Its coordinate: **(0, +1, +2, 0)**

Reading each dimension:
- 𓇯 = 0: no spatial component (good isn't a place)
- 𓈖 = +1: has substance (it's about something real)
- 𓇳 = +2: strong positive signal (this is the core — goodness IS signal)
- 𓏴 = 0: no time component (good isn't an event)

The highest dimension is 𓇳 (signal), so this word functions as an
**adjective** — describing quality. That's not a grammar rule imposed
from outside. It falls out of the coordinate.

Now look at its opposite: 𓈋 ḏw — "bad, evil."

Its coordinate: **(0, +1, -1, 0)**

Same substance (+1). Same lack of space and time. But 𓇳 flipped
from +2 to -1. The signal reversed. Good and bad are the same
point with the signal inverted. Antonyms are always this: flip
one or two dimensions, keep the rest.


### Parts of Speech

You don't need grammar rules. The coordinate tells you what a word does:

| highest dimension | part of speech | why                            |
|-------------------|----------------|--------------------------------|
| 𓇯 space          | preposition    | it locates things              |
| 𓈖 substance      | noun           | it names things                |
| 𓇳 signal         | adjective      | it describes things            |
| 𓏴 time           | verb           | it happens to things           |

Compound parts of speech:
- **adverb** = 𓇳 + 𓏴 (signal applied to time: "quickly," "gently")
- **pronoun** = 𓈖 + 𓇳 (substance with signal: "I" = 𓈖𓇳, "you" = 𓈖𓂜𓇳)
- **conjunction** = pure 𓇳 (logic gate: "and," "or," "if")
- **determiner** = 𓇯 + 𓇳 (spatial signal: "the," "this," "that")


## Part 2: The Foundation — Shell 0 (24 Signs)

Shell 0 is the floor of everything. 24 signs. They split into two
kinds: 16 **bonds** (what things DO) and 8 **atoms** (what things ARE).

24 = 2⁴ + 2×4. This same number was found independently five times:
Egyptian uniliterals, Lakoff's image schemas, RISC instruction sets,
Wierzbicka's semantic primes, and the lattice formula. Five different
fields hit the same floor.


### Bonds — 16 Schemas

A bond is an operation. It doesn't sit at a coordinate — it connects
coordinates. It's the verb of the grid, the edge between nodes.

Each glyph literally pictures what it does. A foot pictures walking
(PATH). A basket pictures containing (CONTAINMENT). A mouth pictures
drawing in (ATTRACTION). The sign IS the schema.

— like quantum states before measurement. Bonds 8-15 are observed —
classical, collapsed. The forearm (FORCE / DO) is the measurement
boundary. Before you act, possibilities. After you act, facts.

Note: the basket 𓎡 is bond 15 — the last one. Its coordinate is
(1,1,1,1): all four dimensions present. CONTAINMENT = THINK = the
mind holding everything. This is the fixed point of the whole system.

The pool 𓈙 (CENTER-PERIPHERY) is the only atom on two dimensions:
+1 space, +1 substance. Inside = where space and substance overlap.


## Part 3: How Words Work

All of language reduces to a small number of operations on coordinates.
Here they are, each with a worked example.


### Antonyms — Flip a Dimension

To negate a word, flip the sign of one or two dimensions. Everything
else stays the same.

    𓄤 nfr "good"    (0, +1, +2, 0)
    flip 𓇳          ↓
    𓈋 ḏw  "bad"     (0, +1, −1, 0)

Same substance. Signal reversed. That's it.

More examples:
- happy ↔ sad: 𓇳 flipped
- alive ↔ dead: 𓏴 flipped (time stops)
- here ↔ there: 𓇯 flipped


### Synonyms — Nearby Points

Words that mean almost the same thing sit close together on the grid.
Distance ≤ 2 steps = synonym.

    𓅨 wr "great"    (0, +1, +1, 0)    distance 0
    𓋟 nb "lord"     (0, +1, +1, 0)    = same point, different sign

    𓅨 wr "great"    (0, +1, +1, 0)    distance 2
    𓉻 ꜥꜣ "big"      (+1, +1, 0, 0)    = two steps away

**Why you'll see "duplicates" in the tables:** Multiple glyphs can
sit at the same coordinate. 𓏊 (beer jug) and 𓏋 (flat beer jar)
both land at (0,+2,0,0) — pure substance, high magnitude. They're
different signs naming the same address. This is like "big" and
"large" in English: two words, one meaning, one point on the grid.
This is normal and expected throughout the tables. What would be
an error is the same glyph appearing twice with different coordinates.


### Intensity — Same Direction, Higher Shell

Turning up the volume on a word means increasing the magnitude of its
strongest dimension.

    𓋑 ḥḏ "white"     (0, 0, +1, 0)    shell 1
    𓌍 ḥḏḏ "bright"   (0, 0, +2, +1)   shell 2: 𓇳 amplified

    𓅨 wr "great"     (0, +1, +1, 0)   shell 1
    𓄊 wsr "powerful"  (+1, +2, +1, 0)  shell 2: 𓈖 amplified

warm → hot. like → love. white → brilliant. Same direction, more of it.


### Negation — The 𓇳 Flip

The sign 𓂜 flips 𓇳 (the signal dimension). This is how you say "not."

    𓄤 nfr (0,+1,+2,0)   →   𓄤 𓂜𓇳   →   𓈋 (0,+1,−1,0)

    happy → unhappy. possible → impossible. One operation. Every word.


### Tense — Shift 𓏴

Past, present, future are movements along the time axis.

    walk (+1,0,0,+1)   →   walked (+1,0,0,+2)

One step on 𓏴. That's tense. The -ed suffix IS a 𓏴 increment.


### Plurals — Amplify 𓈖

More substance = more things.

    cat (0,+1,0,0)   →   cats (0,+2,0,0)

The substance axis doubles. Water doesn't pluralize because it's
already continuous on 𓈖 — there's no discrete count to increment.


### Questions — Activate 𓇳

A question adds signal to a statement. "It is" → "is it?" You're
requesting the signal dimension — asking for the evaluation.

    statement (0,+1,0,0)   →   question (0,+1,+1,0)

"Where?" isolates 𓇯 and adds 𓇳 — asking for the spatial signal.


### Passive — Flip 𓏴

Active and passive are the same action with time direction reversed.
"She built" → "was built." The builder's time flows forward; the
thing-being-built's time is received.

    active (0,0,+1,+1)   →   passive (0,0,+1,−1)


### Metaphor — Cross-Domain Match

"High status" works because ABOVE (+1,0,0,0) and GOOD (0,0,+1,0)
share a positive direction. The metaphor connects the two domains
through their shared sign.

"Square friendship" fails — no shared coordinates between geometric
shape and social bond. Metaphors aren't arbitrary. They're coordinate
matches across dimensions.

This is exactly what Lakoff and Johnson documented in Metaphors We
Live By: "GOOD IS UP," "MORE IS UP," "HAPPY IS UP." These aren't
cultural accidents. They're the grid's geometry. Positive 𓇯 and
positive 𓇳 point the same way.


### Compounds — Add Coordinates

Combining two words = adding their coordinates.

    bed (space+substance)  +  room (space+substance)  =  bedroom

    sun (+1,+1,+2,0)  +  light (+1,0,+2,+1)  =  sunlight (+2,+1,+4,+1)

The sum IS the compound. No special rules needed.


### Morphology — Prefixes and Suffixes

Prefixes modify coordinates:
- **un-** = flip 𓇳 (undo the signal)
- **re-** = add 𓏴 (add time = do again)
- **pre-** = add 𓏴𓏻 (add more time = before)
- **over-** = increase magnitude (too much)
- **anti-** = flip everything

Suffixes transform between dimensions:
- **-ness**: 𓇳→𓈖 (adjective becomes noun: "good" → "goodness")
- **-ly**: 𓈖→𓇳 (noun becomes descriptor: "friend" → "friendly")
- **-ize**: 𓇳→𓏴 (quality becomes action: "real" → "realize")
- **-ment**: 𓏴→𓈖 (action becomes thing: "move" → "movement")
- **-ful**: add 𓇳 (add signal: "beauty" → "beautiful")
- **-less**: add 𓂜𓇳 (negate signal: "hope" → "hopeless")
- **-ing**: add 𓎛 (add cycle: ongoing)
- **-ed**: add 𓏴 (add time: completed)


### Sentences = Walks

A sentence is a path through 4D space. Subject → Verb → Object is
the walk 𓈖 → 𓏴 → 𓈖: start at a noun, move through time, arrive
at another noun.

    "The cat sat on the mat":
    the(𓇯𓇳) cat(𓈖) sat(𓏴𓇯) on(𓇯) the(𓇯𓇳) mat(𓈖)

A grammatical sentence is a coherent walk — each step connects to
the next. An ungrammatical sentence is coordinates jumping randomly
with no path between them.


### The 𓇳 Flip

The single most productive operation in language. 𓇳 is the
identity/distinction axis — it separates self from other, yes from
no, good from bad.

| dimension | +𓇳 (self/near/yes) | −𓇳 (other/far/no) |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------------|
| 𓇯 space  | HERE                | THERE              |
| 𓈖 mass   | WHO (person)        | WHAT (thing)       |
| 𓇳 signal | GOOD / TRUE / SAME  | BAD / FALSE / OTHER|
| 𓏴 time   | NOW                 | THEN               |

Pronouns are built from this:
- I = 𓈖𓇳 (substance + self-signal)
- YOU = 𓈖𓂜𓇳 (substance + flipped signal = other person)
- WE = 𓈖𓏻𓇳 (plural substance + self-signal)
- THEY = 𓈖𓏻𓂜𓇳 (plural substance + flipped signal)

"Us vs them" is one 𓇳 flip on the same plural 𓈖.


## Part 4: The Vocabulary

### How the Shells Work

The vocabulary is organized in concentric shells, like electron shells
in an atom. Each shell allows coordinates one step further from zero.

- **Shell 0** (k=0): coordinates are 0 only = origin = silence
- **Shell 1** (k=1): coordinates from {-1, 0, +1} = 3⁴-1 = **80 points**
- **Shell 2** (k=2): at least one coordinate hits ±2 = 5⁴-3⁴ = **544 points**
- **Shell 3** (k=3): at least one hits ±3 = 7⁴-5⁴ = **2,016 points**

The codex currently holds **728 entries** across **220 unique coordinates**:
- 24 uniliterals (Shell 0: 16 bonds + 8 atoms)
- 89 biliterals (Shell 1 — almost exactly 3⁴-1)
- 62 triliterals (subset of Shell 2)
- 279 classifiers + 274 logograms = the rest

140 entries now carry explicit formula data mapping their coordinates
to physics/computing ratios.

Every word in every language is a point on one of these shells.
Different languages use different sounds for the same coordinate.
Translation isn't conversion — it's looking up the same address
in a different phone book.

### The Wave
