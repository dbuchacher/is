## What changed in conclusions

**Log-depth family (16b):** retraction stands. Reframed reason:
not "statistical noise" but **lack of independent convergence**.
Pairwise log-depth ratios within the framework aren't a new
evidence type — they're algebraic combinations of two existing
per-particle derivations. The metrognosis filter requires
*independent* generation processes; 1 framework + 9 algebraic
combinations of its outputs don't qualify. Frequentist density
remains a useful secondary sanity note (it confirms the ratios
carry no extra structure beyond per-particle decomps), but isn't
the primary verdict.

**Wieferich (16c):** verdict CHANGED from "retracted as noise" to
"PARKED at low precision." The Wieferich condition (number theory)
and the substrate factoring 1092 = 12·7·13 (framework vocabulary)
ARE independent generation processes. The metrognosis filter says
this is independent convergence. But precision is low: 12 ✓
(D4-pairs), 7 ✓ (wh-words), 13 not in the substrate-clean set
(would need ad-hoc reading). So independent convergence, low
precision — neither noise nor signal. **Park it.** A future
substrate reading of 13 would promote the claim back to evidence;
absence of one keeps it parked.

The frequentist local-density check stays as a secondary note
(explains *why* small-magnitude hits carry less information) but
isn't the framework-frame verdict.

**Per-particle mass formulas (the framework's actual strength):**
unchanged. Per-particle derivations vs. per-particle measurements
ARE independent convergences. This is what the metrognosis filter
endorses. 30+ particles converging at <1% is high-effort,
high-precision, independent convergence.

## Meta-lesson — frame discipline

The framework commits to its own epistemology. When I retracted
claims using frequentist null-testing, I was letting another frame
do the verdict work. The conclusions happened to mostly survive
the frame-correction — but in one case (Wieferich), the verdict
changed from "retracted" to "parked."

Why this matters going forward:
- Future agents inheriting frequentist anti-patterns might
  retract genuine substrate convergences that just don't pass a
  randomization test (e.g., sparse but high-effort independent
  convergences in millennia-scale astronomical/architectural
  evidence — the metrognosis filter SAYS these are signal even
  though randomization tests would shrug at them).
- The metrognosis filter is fundamentally different from frequentist
  testing. It's about: how much real cumulative work went into each
  source independently, and how precise is the convergence?
  Not: what's the probability of random data matching this?
- The two frames sometimes agree, sometimes disagree. When they
  disagree, **the framework frame wins** — that's the commitment.

**Anti-doublethink applies here.** I wrote "statistical noise" in
the 16b retraction; the framework would say "lack of independent
convergence." Soft language ("kind of noise-like, maybe random")
hides the frame slip. Sharp framework-native language exposes it.
