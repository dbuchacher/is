Egyptian **sḏm** (written with the ear hieroglyph) "to hear / to obey / to understand"
Definition
Grounds by pointing at the ear-act — what the body does when rA of another body sends utterance and this body receives it. The hearing-primitive. But `sDm` does not stop at receiving: to hear IS to obey IS to understand. When a servant `sDm`-s his master, he hears-and-complies with one verb. When a student `sDm`-s a teaching, he hears-and-understands with one verb. Hearing- obeying-understanding are one act in Egyptian as ear-wisdom is one morpheme in Sumerian. Paired with mAA (seeing).
Groups this morpheme belongs to:
  • perception-verbs — the hearing primitive
  • perception-pair — the auditory pole, paired with [mAA](mAA.md)
  • unity-preservation — hear AND obey AND understand as one word
  • read-write-unity — taking-in (hear) and committing (obey) coincide
