Egyptian **rꜥ** (written with the sun-disc 𓇳) "sun / sun-god / day"
Definition
Grounds by pointing at the bright disc crossing pt from dawn to dusk — and at the god-body who IS that disc. Egyptian does not split the sun-body from the god: `ra` is both. The classifier 𓇳 marks any word naming the daily-bright. The sun- disc is the god's visible shape; the god is the substrate-form of the disc. One word for celestial-body and divine-being, same as Sumerian's `an` (sky=god). Also extends to mean "day" in certain contexts (the span-of-sun).
Groups this morpheme belongs to:
  • natural-phenomena — the bright disc
  • unity-preservation — sun AND god AND day as one word
  • celestial-bodies — the daily-bright body
  • divine-beings — the sun as god-body
