Egyptian **ib** "heart / mind / inner-self / will / desire"
Definition
Grounds by pointing at the inner-beating-organ AND at the seat of thought and will. Egyptian did not split mind from heart: `ib` is where thinking happens, where decisions are made, where wisdom is kept, AND it is the muscle in the chest. When a scribe writes `ib=j` ("my heart"), the speaker means both organ and mind at once. In Memphite theology, Ptah creates by what ib has devised — thought lives in heart, and heart is the READ-seat of the creative act. Paired with rA (mouth/tongue) in the creation-pair: ib devises, rA commands.
