Egyptian **ḥw** "authoritative-utterance / divine-speech / command-that-makes-real"
Definition
The speech that makes what it says come to wn — not ordinary Dd, but the command-speech of creators and kings. When a nTr `Hw`-s, the thing spoken IS. `Hw` is divine performative utterance: the tongue-act that commits the world to a new state. In Memphite theology paired with sjA (perception / divine-thought) — thought first, then command. Frame 5 hub-event: `sjA` is the READ, `Hw` is the WRITE, together they bring things into xpr. Personified as a minor god riding in the solar barque alongside Sia.
