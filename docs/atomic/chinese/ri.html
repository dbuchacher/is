Chinese **日** (oracle-bone: a circle with a central dot — the sun-graph) "sun / day / daytime"
Definition
Grounds by pointing at the bright disc crossing tian from dawn to dusk — AND at the span-of-bright itself, the span English calls "day." One word for the celestial body AND its cycle-unit. Same unity Egyptian holds with `ra`: sun AND day one morpheme. In oracle-bone divinations the king asks about "this ri" meaning "today" — the body-of-the-sun and the span-of-light it governs are named with a single word. Paired with yue as the day-body / night-body pair.
Groups this morpheme belongs to:
  • natural-phenomena — the bright daily disc
  • unity-preservation — sun AND day as one word
  • celestial-bodies — the daytime celestial
  • time-cycles — the sun-cycle unit, paired with [yue](yue.md) and [nian](nian.md)
