wen
chinese morpheme — atomic file
Chinese **聞** (oracle-bone: an ear at a doorway, listening) "hear / ask / smell / learn-of / get-news-of"
Definition
Grounds in the oracle-bone graph: an ear positioned at a doorway. `wen` is hearing AND asking AND smelling AND learning-of-news. One morpheme for the whole aural-perception-plus-inquiry cluster: to hear-about something IS to ask-about it IS to learn-of it. Same unity Sumerian holds with `ŋeštug` (ear=hearing=wisdom) and Egyptian with `sDm` (hear=obey=understand): four substrates converge on the naming that the ear is not a passive receiver but an act of reaching-toward what comes-to-the-ear. Smell also listed — what comes-to-the-nose is gathered-in by the same verb, the "come-to-me" of distal sensing.
Groups this morpheme belongs to:
  • perception-verbs — the distal-sensing verb
  • unity-preservation — hear AND ask AND learn-of AND smell as one word
  • read-write-unity — the reach-toward-and-receive side of the speaking-loop
