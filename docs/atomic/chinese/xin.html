xin
chinese morpheme — atomic file
Chinese **心** (oracle-bone: a stylized heart-organ) "heart / mind / intention / center / the-seat-of-thought"
Definition
Grounds by pointing at the beating-organ in the chest AND at the seat of thought, feeling, intention. Chinese does not split mind from heart: `xin` is where thinking happens, where wishes live, where decisions are made, AND it is the muscle that keeps the body alive. One word for all of it — same unity Egyptian holds with `ib` and Sumerian with `šà`. A speaker's "xin" is their heart-organ AND their mind AND their will. Later Chinese philosophy builds on this unity (Mencius, Xunzi, Chan Buddhism all take `xin` as one thing doing many jobs) — but the unity is already in the oracle-bone seed.
