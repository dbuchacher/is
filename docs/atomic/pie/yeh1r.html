yeh1r
pie morpheme — atomic file
PIE *yeh₁r- "year / season / cycle"
Descendants: year, Latin *hornus*, Greek *hōra* (hour, season)
Definition
The returning turn of the seasons — the span that begins at one spring, runs through the heat of tep-summer, into the falling leaf, into the ghyem cold, and comes back around to the next spring. `yeh1r` is the cycle-name: not just one year-length but the turning itself, the wheel of the seasons closing. The same word stretches to name the smaller turns inside it — Greek *hōra* takes on "hour" and "season" both, because both are spans that come back around. Literally a descendant of the wheel-stroke at the sky-scale: kwel turned in heavens instead of under a cart, kwekwlos traced by the soh2wl over the long run. Sits inside h2eiw — the longer life-arc holds many yeh1r-cycles in a row. Where meh1ns is the moon-cycle span, `yeh1r` is the sun-cycle span — the bigger loop the speaker counts meh1ns-es inside.
