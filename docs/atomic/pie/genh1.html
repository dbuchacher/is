genh1
pie morpheme — atomic file
PIE *ǵenh₁- "beget / kin"
Descendants: **kin**, **king**, **gene**, **genus**, **genesis**, **Latin *genus***, Greek *genos*
Definition
The gen-branch of the know-beget single-verb gneh3 — same root-stem, split by tongue-drift into two glosses. Where gneh3 keeps the take-in-and-put-out unity visible as one stroke, `genh1` carries only the putting-out half: the bringing-forth of new life, the act that makes a fresh young kin stand where none stood before. h1eg and the mate do the `genh1` act together; what comes out is kin, same line, same blood. The root then widens: those who share one `genh1` are one kin-group, and the head of a kin-group is its king — all stemming from the same bringing-forth stroke.
