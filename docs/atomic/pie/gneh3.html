PIE *ǵneh₃- / *ǵenh₃- "to know / beget"
Descendants: know, can, ken, cognize, recognize, gnosis, beget, genesis, genus, gene, kin, king, Latin *noscere*, Greek *gignōskō*
Definition
To know and to beget as one verb: the speaker takes something in through weid and men, and in the same stroke puts something out — dheh1 of a child, a name, a fresh-made thing. What comes in through the eye comes back out the hand or the loins; the inward gwem and the outward h1ey meet at self and make one act. Knowing IS begetting in PIE; later tongues split the two halves (gno- to know, gen- to beget) but here they are the single turn of kwel where taking-in and giving-out coincide.
