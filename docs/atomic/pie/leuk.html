PIE *leuk- "light / bright"
Descendants: light, lunar, Latin *lux*, Greek *leukos*
Definition
Light itself — what soh2wl pours onto the world, what peh2wr throws from the hearth, what the eye needs to weid anything at all. `leuk` is the quality that h1es when things are seen and fails when h1regwos takes over. To weid requires `leuk`: no light, no sight. The root pairs with seeing at the ground level — light is not just a bright-thing floating separately, it is what sight depends on. The speaker names the quality where it h1es: the brightness of a meh1ns-lit snowfield, the glint off moving h2ekweh2, the pale glow of a coal in the dark that guides the hand to it. Kin to bhel (white-shining) — both name the bright, but `leuk` is the active giving-of-sight while `bhel` is the quality of a bright surface that holds the light given.
