deru
pie morpheme — atomic file
PIE *deru- / *dóru- "tree / wood / firm"
Descendants: **tree**, **true**, **trust**, **durable**, Latin *durus*, Greek *drus* (oak)
Definition
The great standing thing and the hardness it holds, named as one — a tree steh2-s without a body, upright out of dheghom for longer than any h1eg lives, and its wood stays hard through blade, through fire that h2ed-s the outside, through years of rain. `deru` is this unity: the tree, the wood cut from it, and the firmness that makes the wood trustable when it becomes a spear-haft or a roof-beam. What you can lean on, what does not bend, what will h1es there tomorrow as it does today — all one word, because to the Paleolithic speaker they are one substance. The later splits into English "true" and "trust" and Latin *durus* (hard) are not metaphors from tree; they are the same pointing kept: what holds like wood holds is `deru`, and a saying that holds like wood holds is a `deru` saying. The firmness is not a likeness of the tree — the firmness IS the tree-stuff wherever it shows up. Sister to steh2 (the posture of holding-upright); cousin to wes (staying-through-time).
