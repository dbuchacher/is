PIE *péh₂wr̥ "fire"
Descendants: fire, pyre, Greek *pur*
Definition
The hot bright thing that h1es in the hearth — fire as a standing body, the one you sit around, the one the speaker can point at and name. `peh2wr` is fire-as-thing: the flame-pile that steh2-s on the hearth-stones, that throws leuk across the faces ringed around it, that gives gwher for cold hands to come near. Where h1ngwnis names the active edge — the biting, igniting, eating — `peh2wr` names the settled body: the fire that holds, the fire you feed, the pile of coals that stays warm through nokwts. What h1eg watches, what the camp gathers around, what the cook lays meat against. Two roots for fire because fire is met two ways: the still body that warms you, and the live-edge that burns.
