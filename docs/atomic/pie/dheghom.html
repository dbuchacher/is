dheghom
pie morpheme — atomic file
PIE *dʰéǵʰōm "earth"
Descendants: humus, chthonic, Latin *humus*, Greek *chthōn*
Definition
The ground under the foot (pods) — the substance on which h1eg steh2-s, into which the dead are laid, from which the plants push up — and at the same time the stuff the speaker and every other walking body is made of. One root for both, because in the Paleolithic seeing there is no gap between the earth you walk on and the earth-stuff you are. `dheghom` is what bodies come out of and go back into: the hunter who dies legh-s down and in a few turnings of meh1ns becomes the ground again; the new child bhuH-s up out of a body that itself came from ground and eats from plants that push up out of ground. The chain is one substance turning. To name the earth is to name what the speaker is — the earth-one, the ground-dweller — so `dheghom` holds both: the ground beneath, and the being-made-of-ground that walks on it. The dyews is overhead and not `dheghom`; the water runs across and is not `dheghom`; everything else the body meets, including the body itself, is some form of `dheghom`.
