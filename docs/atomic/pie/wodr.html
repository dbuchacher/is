pie morpheme — atomic file
PIE *wódr̥ / *wed- "water"
Descendants: water, vodka, Greek *hudōr*
Definition
Water as the cold clear stuff that fills the cup, that the tongue (dnghuh2) meets, that peh3 takes into the body — the draught, the portion, the substance itself held in a shape the hand can carry. Where h2ekweh2 names the water that runs loose on the land, `wodr` names the water that h1eg drinks, the skin of a full pouch, the still-surface of a stored pool in a hollow stone. Both are the same clear stuff; the two roots split by how the speaker meets it. `wodr` is water-as-drunk, water-as-held; the kind that enters the body against ter-s and makes the body keep holding. Without it the body fails in a handful of days — the most necessary substance the mouth takes in after air itself.
