meh1ns
pie morpheme — atomic file
PIE *mḗh₁n̥s "moon / month"
Descendants: moon, month, Latin *mensis*, Greek *mēn*
Definition
The paler round one that kwel-s across dyews at nokwts, and the counted turning of days that it marks — one root for both, because to the Paleolithic speaker the moon IS the month-counter. `meh1ns` grows from a thin curve through full round and back to nothing, and that cycle itself — from full-round to full-round — is the span the speaker counts by. To name the moon is to name the span it measures; no later division into the thing-in-sky and the count-of-days, because there is no such split. The hunter watches the face grow for half the count, shrink for the other half, and knows when she-beasts will birth, when cold will deepen, when to move camp. The soh2wl gives day; `meh1ns` gives the counting that holds days together into a span h1eg can hold in men.
