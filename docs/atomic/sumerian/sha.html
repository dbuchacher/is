Sumerian **šà** "heart / interior / middle / inside"
Definition
Grounds by pointing at the inside of the body — where the beating organ sits, where breath feels held, where hunger is felt. One word for the organ, the inside-of-anything, and the middle-of- anything. The heart IS the interior IS the middle. When a speaker says "in the heart of the city" Sumerian says `šà uru` — same word for organ and for interior-of. The hub-at-body- scale: everything closes toward `šà`.
Groups this morpheme belongs to:
  • body-parts — the interior organ
  • unity-preservation — heart AND interior AND middle as one word
  • hub-at-scale — the middle that things close toward
