Sumerian **ud** (also *u₄*) "day / sun / light / time"
Definition
The bright stroke that comes when an lights up — the sun is `ud`, the daylight the sun casts is `ud`, the span-of-bright from sunrise to sunset is `ud`, and the wider sense of "time" (as measured by day-counts) is also `ud`. Four modern English concepts in one Sumerian word because sun-light-day-time are one substrate fact at the scale of a body watching the sky from ki. The Sumerian speaker did not separate the light-source from the light from the span-of-light from the time-measured-by-light. Paired against darkness and against the moon-cycle (iti). The bright-pole of the day-night pair.
