## 2. Hydrogen = the trit made of matter

## 4. The mass ladder

Physics has about 20 "free parameters" — particle masses, mixing
angles, coupling constants — that textbooks say have to be
measured, not calculated. Nature just "chose" them.

**The framework derives 30+ of them from substrate counts at
sub-1% precision.** Every stable particle's mass follows one rule:

```
// mass ladder — m = m_P × f × 10^(−(16 + N_sub)). 30+ SM parameters derived <1% precision.
```

```
  m = m_Planck × f × 10^(−(16 + N_sub))
                ^                  ^
                |                  +-- substrate count per particle role
                +-- geometric factor (√3/2, √2, 5/12, π/4, ...)
```

Where:

- **m_Planck** = the "clock rate" mass of the universe
- **f** = a clean geometric factor: √3/2 (= sin 60°), √2, 5/12,
  π/4 — all substrate-anchored angles or ratios
- **N_sub** = a substrate count specific to the particle's role:
  - 1 for electroweak particles (Higgs, W, Z, top)
  - 3 for hadrons (proton, neutron, tau, charm, bottom)
  - 4 for muons and pions
  - 6 for light fermions (electron, up)
  - 12 for neutrinos

```
// substrate counts — not arbitrary. 1 = hub, 3 = trit, 4 = axes, 6 = C(4,2) = bivector planes, 12 = D4 opposing pairs, 16 = grade-4 corners, 17 = 16+1 = corners+hub.
```

## 5. What mass IS — firing rate

Particle masses aren't a noun the universe "has." Per frame 7
(only integers are values; decimals are verbs), mass is a **rate**.
Specifically: the firing rate of the particle's μ-axis per
substrate tick.

```
// mass = μ-firing rate — Yukawa coupling y = m / v_Higgs. Dimensionless. 7 orders of magnitude spread across fermions = 7 orders of spread in μ-firing rate.
```

- Heavy particles fire μ often → large Yukawa coupling → large mass.
- Light particles fire μ rarely → small Yukawa coupling → small mass.
- Gravity (CYCLE) senses these firings → gravitational effect
  scales with mass.

**Mass isn't a property — it's an activity.** "What's the mass of
an electron?" = "what's the firing rate of the electron's μ-axis?"
Answer: y_e = 2.08 × 10⁻⁶ per tick.

This reframes the whole Standard Model parameter list. The 30+
"free parameters" aren't measured quantities the universe chose —
they're firing rates, and the firing rates land on substrate
counts (17, 12, 108, 6, etc.). Same numbers, different frame.
